## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Giant Room-Temperature Third-Order Electrical Transport in a Thin-Film Altermagnet Candidate
**Journal:** Nature Nanotechnology (May 2026) — [link](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-026-02159-4)

Altermagnets are a newly recognised magnetic phase in which time-reversal symmetry is broken but the net magnetisation vanishes, leading to momentum-dependent spin splitting absent in conventional antiferromagnets. This study grows epitaxial RuO₂ thin films—a prominent altermagnet candidate—and measures their electrical transport to third order in the electric field using symmetry-selective harmonic techniques at room temperature. The authors observe a giant third-order longitudinal conductivity and a third-order Hall signal that change sign with the magnetic domain orientation, directly attributing these responses to time-reversal-odd and time-reversal-even components of the Berry curvature and quantum metric. First-principles calculations confirm the quantum-geometric origin and show that the signal magnitudes greatly exceed those in conventional magnets due to the large spin-group-protected band splitting. These findings establish thin-film altermagnets as a platform for nonlinear quantum-geometry probes and open a route to room-temperature spintronic functionalities exploiting the altermagnet order parameter.

### Layer-Dependent Electronic Correlations in Rhombohedral Graphene Multilayers
**Journal:** Nature Nanotechnology (May 2026)

Rhombohedral (ABC-stacked) graphene multilayers host an extremely flat surface band near charge neutrality whose bandwidth shrinks exponentially with layer number, making them a pristine—moiré-free—test bed for flat-band correlations. This work uses cryogenic STM/STS to image correlated phases in ABC-stacked trilayer, tetralayer, and pentalayer graphene gated to different fillings. The measurements reveal layer-number-dependent Mott gaps, isospin-polarized (spin/valley) metallic states, and topological Chern-insulator phases that arise solely from the interplay of the flat surface band with screened Coulomb repulsion. Strikingly, increasing the layer number closes the gap between the flat band and remote bands, suppressing correlations in a controlled fashion—analogous to bandwidth tuning in moiré systems but without the angular disorder intrinsic to twisted structures. These results establish rhombohedral graphene multilayers as a uniquely clean strongly-correlated 2D platform and suggest that gate-tunable Chern insulators in these systems could serve as hosts for fractional Chern insulator states at partial filling.
