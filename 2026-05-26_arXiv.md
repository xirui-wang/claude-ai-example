## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [2605.21449] Hybrid Improper Ferroelectricity and Moiré Superlattices-induced Exciton Quantization in Layered 2D Halide Perovskite

Sanika S. Padelkar, Sharidya Rahman et al. study solution-grown (PA)₂FAPb₂I₇ single crystals, a 2D Ruddlesden-Popper halide perovskite, and discover that pseudo-merohedral twinning—driven by trilinear hybrid improper ferroelectric mode coupling—generates an inadvertent moiré superlattice within the crystal. Below 123 K the commensurate moiré phase imposes a periodic confinement potential that quantizes free excitons into an equidistant photoluminescence ladder; warming to 298 K drives a commensurate-to-incommensurate transition that randomizes the potential and broadens emission. This directly resolves the longstanding debate over anomalous secondary photoluminescence in layered 2D perovskites, attributing it to moiré-trapped excitons rather than defects. The findings connect twistronics physics to solution-processable 2D perovskites and open pathways to piezo-optoelectric and photoferroelectric devices without the need for mechanical stacking.

### [2605.18506] Probing Dielectric Screening in van der Waals Heterostructures via Pressure-Tuned Exciton Rydberg Series

Shalini Badola, Adlen Smiri, Thomas Pelini, Clement Faugeras and collaborators use the exciton Rydberg series of a monolayer WSe₂ encapsulated in hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) as a sensitive in situ probe of dielectric screening under hydrostatic pressure. By tracking how the energies of excited exciton states (1s, 2s, 3s, …) shift with pressure, the authors quantitatively extract the pressure-induced changes in the effective dielectric environment provided by the surrounding hBN layers. The method is non-destructive and captures subtle interlayer screening changes inaccessible to direct optical measurements of the ground-state exciton alone. These results establish exciton Rydberg spectroscopy as a powerful and general technique for characterizing dielectric properties in van der Waals heterostructures, with direct relevance to engineering excitonic devices that leverage pressure or strain tuning.

### [2605.09695] Emerging 2D Materials for Beyond von Neumann Computing: A Perspective

Yaser Banad surveys three converging device thrusts that exploit the unique properties of 2D materials to overcome the memory–bandwidth bottleneck of the von Neumann architecture. First, graphene and graphene nanoribbon (GNR) transistors offer atomically thin, gate-tunable channel materials compatible with scaled CMOS; second, memristors integrating 2D materials with oxides enable analog in-memory matrix operations that collapse the data-movement overhead of neural network inference; third, silicon-compatible 2D photonic emitters and thermal structures provide optical computing primitives. The perspective emphasizes that the combination of electrically tunable carrier density, large optical nonlinearity, and atomic thickness uniquely positions 2D materials to realize compute-where-you-store, event-driven, and optical processing paradigms in a single material platform. The roadmap has implications for energy-efficient AI hardware as conventional scaling plateaus.

### [2605.16778] Emergent Impedance due to Antiferromagnetic Domain Wall Dynamics

Yuta Yamane, Jotaro J. Nakane, Yasufumi Araki and Jun'ichi Ieda extend the emergent-impedance concept—previously established for spiral ferromagnets—to antiferromagnetic domain walls, which are of central importance for ultrafast spintronic and magnonic devices operating in the microwave and THz regimes. The authors develop a full analytical formalism for the ac electrical response of an antiferromagnetic domain wall driven by ac currents, deriving closed-form expressions for the emergent inductance and resistance. Two distinct dynamical modes are identified: translational motion of the domain-wall center, which contributes an impedance proportional to velocity, and time-dependent canting of the sublattice magnetizations, a contribution unique to antiferromagnets with no ferromagnetic analogue. These results provide a quantitative framework for designing and characterizing antiferromagnetic domain-wall-based microwave circuit elements, relevant to next-generation spintronic oscillators and sensors.

### [2605.18478] Spin-caloritronic Signatures of Soft Magnons in Bilayer CrSBr

This work theoretically investigates the spin Seebeck effect in the 2D van der Waals layered antiferromagnet bilayer CrSBr, which hosts magnon modes in the GHz microwave regime due to its moderate exchange and anisotropy energies. The authors show that triaxial anisotropy and intralayer dipolar interactions renormalize the magnon spin angular momentum in a non-trivial way, and that under a moderate in-plane magnetic field perpendicular to the zero-field Néel vector, one magnon mode softens to zero frequency. This softening drives an algebraic divergence of the magnon spin angular momentum and a logarithmic divergence of the thermal spin Seebeck conductivity, providing a clear and experimentally accessible caloritronic signature. The work highlights bilayer CrSBr as a platform for probing soft-mode physics and GHz spin caloritronics in 2D magnets, with direct implications for magnon-based microwave signal processing.

### [2605.22522] Competing Incommensurability, Electronic Correlations, and Superconductivity in a Hybrid Transition Metal Dichalcogenide

Jean C. Souza and collaborators use scanning tunneling microscopy and spectroscopy combined with DFT+DMFT calculations to investigate the polytype 4Hb-TaS₂, which alternates between strongly correlated 1T and metallic 1H TaS₂ layers. STM reveals an emergent incommensurate interlayer potential arising from the natural lattice mismatch between the two layer types; this potential coexists and competes with a pre-existing incommensurate charge-density wave (CDW) that suppresses its long-range order. DFT+DMFT shows that local variations in the interlayer distance modulate both hybridization strength and charge transfer between the correlated and metallic layers, creating a spatially heterogeneous electronic landscape. The study establishes a fundamentally new mechanism for proximity-induced correlated phenomena in 2D van der Waals polytypes, with implications for understanding and engineering unconventional superconductivity in stacked TMD systems.
