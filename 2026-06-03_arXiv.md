## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [2606.01012] Property Prediction of Stacked Bilayer Materials: A Multimodal Learning Approach
This paper directly addresses one of the central challenges in 2D materials science: predicting the emergent properties of stacked bilayer van der Waals heterostructures that cannot be inferred from the monolayer constituents alone. The multimodal learning framework integrates structural, compositional, and materials property data to model the effects of interlayer stacking order, twist angle, and registry on quantities such as band alignment, interlayer coupling, and optical response — precisely the variables that define the functionality of 2D device platforms like twisted bilayer graphene and TMD heterobilayers. The work fills a significant gap in AI-accelerated materials discovery, where prior machine learning models have focused on bulk or monolayer crystals; here the bilayer stacking degree of freedom is treated as a first-class physical variable. Accepted to IJCAI 2026, the approach positions AI as a practical screening tool for the enormous combinatorial space of van der Waals heterostructure pairings, orientations, and layer counts.

### [2606.02206] Moiré Strain Skyrmions in Sliding Twisted Bilayers
This paper introduces a fundamentally new type of topological object — the moiré strain Skyrmion — arising purely from the elastic/mechanical degree of freedom in twisted bilayer van der Waals systems, entirely distinct from spin or charge Skyrmions. Using an empirical continuum elastic model combined with symmetry analysis, the authors show that the Skyrmion lattice is the elastic ground state of the moiré bilayer, with topology protected by the interlayer stacking registry pattern. Under interlayer sliding, these strain Skyrmions undergo a Skyrmion Hall effect in which transverse motion is driven perpendicular to the applied sliding force, with the Hall angle determined by the bilayer chirality and inversely proportional to the moiré twist angle. This establishes a direct, experimentally tunable knob (twist angle) for controlling topological elastic transport, directly relevant to 2D moiré engineering platforms. The finding opens a new, purely mechanical information-transport channel in van der Waals heterostructures, complementing the electronic and magnetic channels already exploited in 2D material devices.

### [2606.02072] Giant dielectric permittivity in Nb-doped rutile crystals
This paper is directly relevant to microwave-frequency dielectric engineering. Rutile-type TiO₂ and its doped variants are classic microwave dielectric materials used in resonators, filters, and high-permittivity substrates, where low dielectric loss is critical. The authors characterize ~1.5 at% Nb-doped rutile single crystals across an exceptionally broad frequency range (MHz through THz and infrared) and identify two distinct permittivity mechanisms: the dominant giant permittivity arises from a near-electrode depletion (surface barrier-layer capacitor) effect producing strong thermally activated relaxation in the MHz range, while a previously unreported overdamped "central mode" appears specifically in the microwave (GHz) frequency band upon Nb doping, absent in undoped crystals. This GHz-range overdamped mode constitutes a new source of microwave dielectric loss in Nb-doped rutile, with direct implications for the design and quality factor of microwave ceramic resonators and filter components incorporating rutile-family materials. The identification of the surface barrier-layer mechanism as the dominant source of the giant permittivity also clarifies which loss channels can be suppressed by microstructural engineering (grain boundary control, electrode preparation), guiding the development of next-generation low-loss microwave substrates.
