## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [Physics-Informed 3D Atomic Reconstruction and Dynamics of Free-Standing Graphene from Single Low-Dose TEM Images](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07271)
**arXiv:2604.07271** | Xiaojun Zhang et al.

Resolving the three-dimensional atomic geometry of free-standing graphene in real time is crucial for understanding how intrinsic rippling governs its electronic transport and optical properties, but low electron doses required to minimize radiation damage impose severe signal-to-noise constraints on conventional reconstruction methods. This work presents a physics-informed computational framework that reconstructs 3D atomic coordinates of single-layer graphene from individual low-dose TEM frames (8×10³ e⁻/Å², 1 ms temporal resolution) by combining simulated annealing optimization with molecular dynamics regularization to enforce physical energy landscapes. The method achieves sub-angstrom out-of-plane accuracy (σz < 0.45 Å), sufficient to resolve nanoscale ripples in real time and enabling direct correlation of atomic geometry with local electronic properties. This capability opens the door to dynamic studies of graphene under applied strain, heating, or chemical functionalization, directly linking structural fluctuations to the anomalous low-energy transport observed in this archetypal 2D material.

### [Determining the Free-Carrier Fraction in 2D Perovskites using Power Dependent Photoluminescence](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07262)
**arXiv:2604.07262** | Antonella Cutrupi, Marc Melendez Schofield, Raquel Utrera-Melero, Michel Frising, Enrique Arevalo Rodriguez, Upasana Das, Ferry Prins

Whether photo-excited carriers form excitons or free electron-hole pairs fundamentally determines the design of photovoltaic and optoelectronic devices based on two-dimensional perovskites, yet no quantitative method existed to extract the free-carrier fraction from routine optical measurements. This work derives a complete analytical framework grounded in the Saha ionization equation that maps power-dependent peak photoluminescence directly onto the equilibrium exciton–free-carrier balance without requiring time-resolved techniques. The authors validate the approach on a systematic series of Ruddlesden-Popper perovskites with varying layer thickness, showing that the conventional power-law exponent (between 1 for pure excitons and 2 for pure free carriers) is correctly reproduced as a limiting case and that the method yields a quantitative free-carrier fraction across all intermediate regimes. These results provide a practical optical diagnostic with broad applicability to 2D halide perovskites as well as other nanostructured semiconductors where the exciton binding energy is comparable to the thermal energy.

### [Microscopic evidence of spin-driven multiferroicity and topological spin textures in monolayer NiI₂](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06959)
**arXiv:2604.06959** | Haitao Wang, Tianxing Jiang, Weiyi Pan, Xu Wang, Hongyu Wang, Junchao Tian, Lianchuang Li, Dongming Zhao, Qingle Zhang, Chenxi Wang, Ying Yang, Hongjun Xiang, Changsong Xu, Donglai Feng, Tong Zhang (Published: Phys. Rev. Lett. 136, 026402 (2026))

Monolayer NiI₂ is a rare van der Waals multiferroic in which ferroelectricity is driven by a spin spiral, but the microscopic coupling between spin, charge, and topological order at the monolayer limit was previously uncharacterized at atomic resolution. Using vectorial spin-polarized scanning tunneling microscopy, the authors fully determine the spin rotation plane of the canted spin-spiral and reveal an accompanying 2Q charge modulation that arises directly from spin-charge coupling. At spin-spiral domain walls, topological spin textures composed of meron/antimeron pairs are discovered and found to carry distinct charge patterns and notable band shifts, interpreted as local bound charges induced by spatial variations of the ferroelectric polarization. This atomic-scale portrait of multiferroic domain walls in a strictly 2D system establishes monolayer NiI₂ as a platform for topological magnonic and ferroelectric device concepts where domain walls act as topologically protected objects with tunable electromagnetic properties.

### [Optical Hall absorption sum rule and spectral compensation in time-reversal-breaking moiré and Hofstadter systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08043)
**arXiv:2604.08043** | Yixin Zhang, H. Huang

Optical spectroscopy is increasingly used as a contact-free probe of topology in two-dimensional moiré materials, yet exact sum-rule constraints on the antisymmetric (Hall) component of the optical conductivity — which governs chirality-dependent optical absorption — have been much less developed than their symmetric longitudinal counterparts. This work derives the first-frequency-moment sum rule for the antisymmetric optical conductivity and applies it to two paradigmatic 2D systems with broken time-reversal symmetry: zero-field topological moiré continuum models and Hofstadter lattices under uniform magnetic flux. For the moiré system, the moment vanishes exactly, implying that any low-frequency anomalous Hall absorption must be strictly compensated by higher-frequency spectral weight of the opposite sign — a powerful constraint for interpreting THz and mid-infrared measurements of twisted graphene and TMD moiré systems. For Hofstadter systems, the moment takes a universal value fixed solely by the magnetic flux density per unit cell, independent of microscopic parameters, providing a direct optical route to measuring the effective flux in a magnetic lattice without transport contacts.
