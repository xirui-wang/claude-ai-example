## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [2606.20377] Charge imprinting biases topology of correlated insulator in hBN-aligned rhombohedral multilayer graphene
**Lei Qiao, Xin Lu, Fu-Chun Zhang, Jianpeng Liu** — *cond-mat.mes-hall / cond-mat.str-el*

Rhombohedral multilayer graphene aligned with hexagonal boron nitride (RMG-hBN) hosts correlated Chern insulators, but the precise microscopic role of the hBN moiré potential—especially when the active electrons are spatially displaced from the interface—had remained unclear. This work performs comprehensive Hartree-Fock calculations sweeping over layer number (N), twist angle, displacement field, filling, and hBN alignment, finding that correlated insulators are most robust at small twist angles and intermediate N ≈ 6, where bandwidth suppression balances the layer delocalization of wavefunctions. Crucially, even under moiré-distant conditions at ν = 1 filling, the topology of the insulating state is strongly biased by "charge imprinting": the hBN alignment locally sculpts the valence-band charge texture near the interface via the moiré potential, which then acts as a remote electrostatic template through long-range Coulomb interactions to determine which Chern-number sector the correlated conduction electrons occupy. This mechanism explains why experimentally observed topological phase diagrams in RMG-hBN depend sensitively on the hBN stacking angle and provides design principles for engineering Chern insulators in van der Waals heterostructures without direct moiré coupling.

### [2606.21065] Propagating edge and interfacial states in corrugated graphene: Robustness and configurability
**Adel Belayadi, Dawei Zhai, Nancy Sandler** — *cond-mat.mes-hall / cond-mat.mtrl-sci*

Strain engineering of graphene on patterned substrates offers a moiré-like route to flat bands and topological physics without requiring a twisting degree of freedom. This study shows that the interplay between a strain-induced pseudomagnetic field and a scalar potential controlled by displacement field creates isolated narrow bands and multiple energy gaps near charge neutrality as well as at higher energies. Strikingly, even though some of the low-energy bands carry valley-opposite Chern numbers that sum to zero—seemingly precluding chiral edge transport—propagating in-gap edge states nonetheless emerge robustly across a wide range of nanoribbon geometries that preserve valley symmetry. The authors demonstrate that these edge states are configurable through geometric and field parameters, making periodically corrugated graphene a versatile platform for designing topological channels in an all-graphene, substrate-compatible architecture.

### [2606.23598] Quantum Geometry Driven Finite-Momentum Exciton Fluctuations in Flat-Band Systems
**Yi-Chun Hung, Xiaoting Zhou, Arun Bansil** — *cond-mat.mes-hall / cond-mat.mtrl-sci / cond-mat.str-el*

Flat-band systems—such as magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene and other moiré 2D materials—are known to host unconventional collective phases, but the role of quantum geometry (quantum metric and Berry curvature) in driving excitonic order has been less explored. This work demonstrates that the quantum geometry of flat-band eigenstates directly generates finite-momentum exciton fluctuations that can dominate over zero-momentum excitonic instabilities. The quantum metric tensor controls the exciton kinetic energy in the flat-band limit, selecting finite-center-of-mass-momentum excitonic pairing and potentially stabilizing a Fulde-Ferrell-like excitonic condensate or pair-density-wave exciton phase. These findings highlight quantum geometry as a key ingredient in determining the nature of excitonic order in moiré heterostructures and other flat-band 2D materials, opening new routes to engineering finite-momentum condensates.

### [2606.23479] Magnetic-Free Quantum Interference and Universal Josephson Diode Effect Driven by a Supercurrent Gauge Field
**Haowei Ye, Wenxue He, Kaixuan Fan, Yingpeng Zhang, Shijin Li, Yu Pan, Dechao Geng, Fan Yang, Kenji Watanabe, Takashi Taniguchi, Hechen Ren** — *cond-mat.supr-con / cond-mat.mes-hall*

The Josephson diode effect—nonreciprocal supercurrent flow (I_c⁺ ≠ I_c⁻)—is highly sought for dissipationless rectification in superconducting circuits, but most implementations require an external magnetic field. This work achieves a field-free Josephson diode in graphene-based Josephson junctions by exploiting a supercurrent gauge field: the flowing supercurrent itself breaks time-reversal symmetry and generates an effective vector potential that produces nonreciprocal quantum interference without any external magnet. Using hBN-encapsulated graphene devices with gate-tunable carrier density (Watanabe/Taniguchi hBN), the authors demonstrate a highly tunable and gate-controllable diode efficiency. The mechanism is universal and does not depend on intrinsic spin-orbit coupling or magnetic order, making it applicable across a broad class of Josephson junction platforms and directly relevant to microwave-frequency superconducting circuit elements.

### [2606.19171] Cavity Enhanced Superconductivity
**Hanxiang Zhang, Zexin Feng, I-Te Lu, Zhiwei Li, Songhao Guo, Qiuyu Shang, Dening Luan, Mingcheng Panmai, Kenji Watanabe, Takashi Taniguchi, Angel Rubio, Weibo Gao** — *cond-mat.supr-con*

Vacuum electromagnetic fluctuations inside a microwave/THz cavity have recently been proposed as a means to modify collective quantum phases, but experimental realizations remain scarce. This work couples few-layer NbSe₂ (a well-known 2D superconductor) to a complementary split-ring resonator carefully tuned to 2.04 THz—resonant with key phononic modes of the material—and measures a clear enhancement of the superconducting transition temperature in trilayer NbSe₂ from 3.02 K to 3.41 K (~10% increase). The cavity couples to phonon modes that are critical mediators of the electron-phonon pairing interaction, effectively strengthening the attractive interaction between electrons. The experiment uses hBN-encapsulated NbSe₂ (Watanabe/Taniguchi hBN) for sample quality, and density-functional theory calculations based on quantum electrodynamics (QEDFT) support the phonon-mediated mechanism. These results establish split-ring resonator engineering as a practical route to enhance superconductivity in 2D materials and motivate exploration of cavity-QED control of quantum phases more broadly.

### [2606.04914] Tunable Resonator Integrated Magnetometry
**Colin Stack, Brian Sears, Aruna Ramanayaka, David Ferguson, Ilya Sochnikov, Tony X. Zhou** — *cond-mat.mes-hall*

Superconducting sensing technology at millikelvin temperatures offers unrivaled sensitivity and non-invasiveness for probing mesoscale quantum systems, but combining the bandwidth of lumped-element resonators with the flux sensitivity of SQUIDs has remained challenging. This work introduces a flux-tunable superconducting resonator (tRes) designed and fabricated in a superconducting chip foundry, which integrates an inductor-capacitor circuit architecture with a SQUID-based flux-sensitive inductive element. The tRes achieves low-loss, quantum non-demolition measurement characteristics and can perform magnetometry at millikelvin temperatures with high sensitivity and bandwidth. The design exploits microwave-frequency readout (RF reflectometry) of the resonance frequency, which shifts with applied flux, and is directly compatible with standard cQED measurement setups used for superconducting qubit readout. This platform bridges the gap between SQUID magnetometry and circuit-QED techniques, enabling sensitive in-situ magnetic field sensing integrated into quantum processor architectures.
