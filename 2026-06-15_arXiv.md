## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Engineering electrically-switchable quantum anomalous Hall states by spin-orbit coupling — [2606.13651](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13651)

This paper investigates how proximity coupling to monolayer WSe₂ can be used to engineer the nonvolatile electrical switching of quantum anomalous Hall (QAH) states in twisted monolayer–bilayer graphene (tMBG). By analyzing the magnetic energy landscape of the proximitized moiré system, the authors show that the large spin-orbit coupling (SOC) induced by WSe₂ locks the spin and valley degrees of freedom of the graphene bands, fundamentally changing the hierarchy and character of competing magnetic states involved in the gate-driven switching process. As a result, the magnetization reversals that mediate the topological phase transitions—and that ultimately enable nonvolatile switching of the chiral edge state—are reshaped compared to bare tMBG, yielding more controllable and robust switching behavior. The work provides a concrete van der Waals materials design principle: by selecting the proximitizing layer, one can tune the SOC-mediated energy landscape to optimize topological memory performance in graphene moiré devices.

### Observation of intertwined charge density wave order and superconductivity in Janus monolayer — [2606.13828](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13828)

This study employs density functional theory and density functional perturbation theory to examine the competition and coexistence of charge density wave (CDW) order and conventional phonon-mediated superconductivity in the 1T-ZrSeTe Janus monolayer—a two-dimensional TMD in which Se and Te atoms occupy opposite faces, breaking the mirror symmetry present in standard TMD monolayers. The authors identify a stable 2×2×1 CDW reconstruction and calculate the electron-phonon coupling constant, finding that the Janus inversion asymmetry tunes the electron-phonon interaction strength in a way not available in symmetric TMDs. The results demonstrate that CDW order and superconductivity are intertwined rather than strictly competing, with the structural asymmetry providing a microscopic knob to shift the balance between the two instabilities. This work positions Janus TMD monolayers as a versatile 2D platform for studying the CDW-superconductor relationship, complementing experiments on quasi-1D bulk materials such as ZrTe₃.

### Strain- and doping-tunable optical resonance in Kekulé-Y graphene — [2606.14424](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14424)

Kekulé-Y graphene is a structured variant of graphene featuring a Y-shaped bond-length and on-site-energy modulation that opens a gap at the Dirac point and creates flat-band-like features in the low-energy spectrum. In this work (published in Phys. Rev. B 113 (2026) 165406), the author performs tight-binding and optical conductivity calculations to show that the dominant interband optical resonance in the Kekulé-Y phase is sensitively controlled by both uniaxial strain—which shifts the resonance through deformation of the moiré-like mini-Brillouin zone—and carrier doping, which activates or suppresses transitions via Pauli blocking. The results demonstrate a continuously and independently tunable optical resonance without requiring changes to sample composition, making Kekulé-Y graphene attractive for 2D optoelectronics and tunable mid-infrared detectors. The findings also highlight how bond texturing in graphene derivatives can generate optical activity in frequency ranges inaccessible in pristine graphene.
