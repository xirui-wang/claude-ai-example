## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [2605.00661] Architecting mechanosensitive nanofluidic transport in graphite nanoslits

Lizée et al. engineer a pressure-sensitive, bipolar ionic conductance in atomically thin graphite nanoslits—canonical 2D material channels—without any mechanical structural change to the channel. The key innovation is the selective electrostatic charging of a single channel inlet, which generates a reservoir of mobile ion carriers (an excess electrical double layer) that is then advected into or expelled from the channel by pressure-driven water flow, reversibly modulating ionic conductance. A comprehensive electrohydrodynamic model coupling diffusion, convection, diffusio-osmosis, surface transport, and interfacial slippage quantitatively reproduces the observed mechanosensitive behavior. This work demonstrates that rational surface-chemistry engineering at the inlet of 2D material nanochannels—rather than complex mechanical actuation—suffices to realize mechanosensory functionality, opening routes to biomimetic nanofluidic sensors and pressure-gated ion-transport devices.

### [2605.00477] Signatures of time-reversal-symmetry breaking in multiband 2H-TaS₂ revealed by zero-field Josephson nonreciprocity

This study investigates 2H-TaS₂, a van der Waals (2D layered) superconductor, by fabricating Josephson junctions with 2H-NbSe₂ and measuring the nonreciprocal supercurrent response. A finite Josephson diode efficiency is observed at zero applied field in as-fabricated junctions and shown to be absent in symmetry-controlled (time-reversal-symmetric) and molecule-intercalated control devices, ruling out extrinsic origins such as interface transparency variations or TaS₂ thickness dependence. Nonlinear Hall transport in the normal state further indicates multiband correlated electronic structure. Theoretical modelling within a Josephson framework shows that interband scattering acts as a phase-locking mechanism producing an anomalous phase difference φ₀ and asymmetric current-phase relation, consistent with intrinsic spontaneous time-reversal symmetry breaking arising from multiband superconducting order in this 2D material.

### [2604.27788] Chern number reversal and emergent superconductivity in rhombohedral graphene induced by in-plane magnetic fields

Rhombohedral multilayer graphene is a 2D van der Waals material that has emerged as a highly tunable platform for correlated and topological phases. This work studies hBN-aligned eight-layer rhombohedral graphene moiré superlattices and finds that in-plane magnetic fields drive a reversal of the topological Chern number of the quantum anomalous Hall state alongside the nucleation of three unconventional superconducting phases. The coexistence of QAH and superconducting phases in the same device, along with field-tunable topology, highlights the rich interplay of magnetism and Cooper pairing in this 2D graphene system. The results establish rhombohedral graphene as an exceptionally clean platform for engineering topological superconductivity and probing the interplay between topology and pairing symmetry in 2D materials.
