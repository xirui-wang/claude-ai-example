## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Field-induced asymmetric band flattening and ideal quantum geometry in rhombohedral graphene ([2605.05199](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05199))
Rhombohedral pentalayer graphene (R5G) hosts an exceptionally rich phase diagram of correlated and topological states that depend sensitively on displacement field, with a striking electron-hole asymmetry: superconducting and correlated states concentrate on the hole side at small fields, while the fractional quantum anomalous Hall effect (FQAHE) appears exclusively on the electron side at large fields. This work directly resolves this asymmetry by applying nanospot ARPES with in-situ electrostatic gating, imaging the field-dependent evolution of the flat valence band (FVB) and flat conduction band (FCB) in R5G: the FVB develops an M-shaped dispersion under high field (reducing its flatness), while the FCB progressively flattens and acquires finite Berry curvature and near-ideal quantum geometry. Comparison with tight-binding calculations identifies the critical structural parameters—interlayer hopping asymmetries—that govern the opposing evolutions of the two flat bands and control the underlying quantum geometric tensor. These results provide a unified microscopic framework explaining why FQAHE is stabilized on the electron side, directly linking experimental band structure observation to the emergence of topological flat-band physics in a 2D graphene system.

### Gossamer Superconductivity in Moiré WSe₂ Bilayer ([2605.03766](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03766))
Moiré twisted bilayer WSe₂ has been established as a platform where superconductivity emerges at half-filling—a regime dominated by strong on-site Coulomb repulsion that ordinarily suppresses pairing—posing a challenge for conventional BCS theory. Jin, Ji, Wang, Wang, and Zhang revisit the "gossamer" superconductivity concept, originally proposed for high-T_c cuprates, in which moderate Coulomb repulsion partially suppresses charge fluctuations while leaving a finite density of mobile doublons and holes that form Cooper pairs through antiferromagnetic superexchange. Mapping the moiré continuum model to an extended single-orbital Hubbard model on a triangular lattice and applying renormalized mean-field theory, they find a chiral d+id superconducting ground state whose twist-angle-dependent phase diagram naturally evolves from a Mott insulator through the gossamer SC to a correlated metal, consistent with experiments. The rapid suppression of pairing upon carrier doping also explains the experimentally observed narrow superconducting dome, making gossamer superconductivity a compelling theoretical framework for the strongly-correlated 2D TMD superconductors.

### Superconductivity in moiré transition metal dichalcogenide bilayers: comparison of two theoretical approaches ([2605.04693](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04693))
The experimental discovery of superconductivity in moiré TMD bilayers—especially twisted WSe₂—at remarkably low carrier densities raises open questions about whether pairing is conventional (phonon-mediated, s-wave) or unconventional (correlation-driven, with exotic gap symmetry). Akbar and Zegrodnik directly compare two theoretical frameworks applied to the same system: a negative-U Hubbard model yielding isotropic s-wave pairing unaffected by the repulsive Coulomb interaction, and a t-J-U model in which strong correlations substantially renormalize quasiparticle weight and permit chiral or other unconventional gap symmetries. By confronting the predicted spectroscopic and thermodynamic observables of each scenario with available experimental data on moiré WSe₂, the authors establish a diagnostic benchmark for determining the correct pairing mechanism in 2D correlated superconductors. This comparative framework will guide future experiments—particularly scanning tunneling spectroscopy and angle-resolved photoemission—in discriminating between conventional and unconventional pairing in moiré 2D systems.

### Microscopic evidence of spin-driven multiferroicity and topological spin textures in monolayer NiI₂ ([2604.06959](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06959))
Monolayer NiI₂ is predicted to be a type-II multiferroic in which ferroelectric polarization is generated by a noncollinear spin spiral, making it among the first single-layer van der Waals materials to combine magnetism and ferroelectricity. Wang et al. employ vectorial spin-polarized scanning tunneling microscopy (SP-STM) to provide atomic-scale proof of the canted spin-spiral ground state, fully resolving the spin rotation plane and imaging the concomitant 2Q charge density modulation that confirms spin-charge coupling. Beyond the uniform spin-spiral state, the work discovers meron/antimeron pairs—topological spin textures with fractional winding numbers—localized at spin-spiral domain walls, representing emergent topological objects in a 2D magnetic material. The simultaneous presence of type-II multiferroicity and topological spin textures in a single atomically-thin layer makes monolayer NiI₂ a unique playground for coupled magnetoelectric and topological physics at the 2D limit, published as Phys. Rev. Lett. 136, 026402 (2026).

### Imaging GHz surface acoustic wave modes in electrostricted LaAlO₃/SrTiO₃ heterostructures ([2605.04402](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04402))
The LaAlO₃/SrTiO₃ (LAO/STO) interface hosts a gate-tunable 2D electron gas (2DEG) that supports superconductivity and can be lithographically patterned into quantum devices including ballistic waveguides and quantum dots, positioning it as a promising oxide platform for quantum information. Ramachandran, Biswas, Mandal, Lee, Msall, Eom, Irvin, Levy, and Yuan image GHz-frequency surface acoustic wave (SAW) modes excited by electrostrictive coupling in LAO/STO heterostructures, leveraging the large electrostriction of SrTiO₃ near its structural phase transition to generate and spatially map acoustic resonances at the interface. Operating in the GHz microwave regime, these SAW modes create a traveling electrostatic potential at the 2DEG that can capture and coherently transport individual electrons or Cooper pairs between spatially separated quantum devices at nanosecond timescales. This demonstration establishes GHz acoustic waves as a microwave-compatible quantum bus for the LAO/STO platform, with potential for hybrid quantum transduction between microwave photons and acoustic phonons in a superconducting quantum circuit architecture, published in Applied Physics Letters 128, 183503 (2026).
