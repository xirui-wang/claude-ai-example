## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Probing sliding ferroelectricity in bilayer Td-WTe₂ with high-harmonic generation
**arXiv:** [2604.25483](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25483) | **Authors:** Elias Greil, Alba de las Heras, Angel Rubio, Anna Galler | **Category:** cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Bilayer Td-WTe₂ is a two-dimensional semimetal that supports an out-of-plane ferroelectric polarization driven by interlayer sliding—a so-called sliding ferroelectric. The authors employ first-principles time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) to simulate high-harmonic generation (HHG) in this material, demonstrating that the mirror-symmetry breaking underlying the ferroelectric state leaves a robust, polarization-dependent imprint on the high-harmonic spectrum. Specifically, polarization-resolved HHG spectra differ sharply between the two ferroelectric states, providing a non-invasive, ultrafast optical probe of the ferroelectric domain configuration. This work establishes HHG spectroscopy as a powerful diagnostic tool for sliding ferroelectricity and interlayer symmetry in 2D quantum materials, with implications for all-optical ferroelectric memory readout.

### Phase transformation kinetics in MoS₂ governed by S-S repulsive interactions and defect-interface compatibility
**arXiv:** [2604.24209](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24209) | **Authors:** Pai Li et al. | **Category:** cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Monolayer MoS₂ can exist in a metastable metallic T' phase and a stable semiconducting H phase, and controlled phase engineering underpins proposed phase-change device applications. Using machine-learning-accelerated molecular dynamics (MLMD) combined with first-principles calculations, the authors reveal that the kinetic persistence of the T' phase originates from repulsive sulfur–sulfur interactions that impose high energy barriers during both nucleation and grain-boundary propagation of the H phase. Sulfur vacancies partially alleviate these barriers but are thermodynamically unstable at the most energetically favorable interface, migrating away from the growth front rather than providing sustained assistance. Direct simulations of MoS₂ nanostructures confirm that H-phase nucleation preferentially initiates at corners and edges, with all observed growth fronts adopting the ZZ-Mo| configuration. These mechanistic insights provide quantitative guidelines for kinetically trapping or converting phase in TMD monolayers.

### Microscopic evidence of spin-driven multiferroicity and topological spin textures in monolayer NiI₂
**arXiv:** [2604.06959](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06959) | **Published:** Physical Review Letters 136, 026402 | **Category:** cond-mat.mes-hall

Monolayer NiI₂ is a rare 2D type-II multiferroic in which ferroelectricity is induced by a spin spiral. Using vectorial spin-polarized scanning tunnelling microscopy (SP-STM), the authors resolve the canted spin-spiral ground state with full determination of the spin-rotation plane, and identify a commensurate 2Q charge modulation consistent with multiferroic theory. At spin-spiral domain walls, they discover topological spin textures consisting of meron/antimeron pairs, each associated with distinct charge patterns and local band shifts that signal bound charges generated by the variation of ferroelectric polarization across the wall. The observations are quantitatively captured by a realistic spin model incorporating Kitaev interactions together with a generalized spin-current mechanism for type-II multiferroicity, providing the first atomic-scale microscopic confirmation of spin-driven multiferroicity in a 2D material and suggesting a platform for low-dissipation electric-field control of topological spin textures.

### Super-moiré spin textures in twisted two-dimensional antiferromagnets
**Journal:** Nature Nanotechnology 21, 359–365 (2026) | **Link:** [s41565-025-02103-y](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-025-02103-y) | **Authors:** — (University of Edinburgh collaboration)

Twisting van der Waals antiferromagnets creates moiré magnetic landscapes whose spatial scales can vastly exceed the twist-angle-defined moiré wavelength. Using scanning nitrogen-vacancy (NV) magnetometry on twisted double-bilayer CrI₃ devices, the authors observe long-range magnetic textures that extend across multiple moiré unit cells—a "super-moiré" magnetic state. Counterintuitively, the size of the spontaneous magnetic texture increases with twist angle (opposite to the moiré wavelength), reaching ~300 nm in 1.1° devices—roughly an order of magnitude larger than the moiré period. The magnetic field maps are consistent with the formation of antiferromagnetic Néel-type skyrmions spanning multiple moiré cells. This geometry-driven skyrmion nucleation—requiring no lithography, heavy metals, or large currents—provides a clean route to low-power topological spin-texture control and spintronic device integration using purely twist-angle engineering.

### Excitonic negative refraction mediated by magnetic orders in 2D magnet CrSBr
**Journal:** Nature Nanotechnology | **Link:** [s41565-025-02118-5](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-025-02118-5) | **Authors:** Zhang Xiang; Liu Xiaoze; Chen Zuxin et al. (University of Hong Kong / Wuhan University / South China Normal University)

CrSBr is a layered 2D antiferromagnetic semiconductor with strongly bound excitons whose optical response is intimately coupled to the magnetic order. The authors demonstrate that in the magnetically ordered phase, exchange-enhanced excitonic resonance along specific crystal axes drives the real part of the dielectric constant negative in those directions, producing a hyperbolic optical iso-frequency surface. This anisotropic dispersion supports negative refraction of excitonic polaritons in a natural material without artificial metamaterial engineering. They realize an on-chip excitonic hyperlens—a precisely cleaved CrSBr slab integrated on a photonic chip—which confines and focuses light to sub-wavelength spots via curved polariton trajectories controlled by the magnetic order. The device is reconfigurable by a modest external magnetic field, opening pathways to ultra-compact, magnetically tunable nanophotonic components based on 2D quantum magnets.

### Two-dimensional crystalline hard masks for high-aspect-ratio nanofabrication
**Journal:** Nature Materials | **Link:** [s41563-026-02524-7](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-026-02524-7) | **Authors:** — | **Published:** April 16, 2026

Modern semiconductor manufacturing requires hard masks with extreme plasma resistance and nanoscale flatness for deep etching of high-aspect-ratio features. The authors demonstrate that van der Waals metal oxyhalides—specifically CrOCl and FeOCl—function as exceptional hard masks owing to their strong covalent/ionic bonds and self-terminating crystalline surfaces. CrOCl achieves etch rates as low as ~2.4 nm min⁻¹ in SF₆/O₂ plasma with a selectivity >200:1 relative to silicon, representing improvements of ~30× over Si₃N₄ and ~20× over TiN under identical conditions, while exhibiting plasma-induced surface smoothening rather than roughening. The masks are chemically patternable with Cl₂ plasma and can be mechanically transferred to diverse substrates including perovskite oxides, polymers, glasses, and monolayer 2D semiconductors. These results position atomically thin 2D crystalline layers as an enabling platform for next-generation three-dimensional semiconductor device patterning.
