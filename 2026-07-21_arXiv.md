## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [Multistate ferroelectricity and switchable layer-locked anomalous valley Hall effects in bilayer ReIrGe₂Se₆](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14461)
*arXiv:2607.14461 — T. Yao, Q. Shen, J. Tan, J. Dong*

This first-principles study predicts that AA′-stacked bilayer ReIrGe₂Se₆ hosts four energetically stable ferroelectric polarization configurations—three connected by reversible switching pathways and one with a unidirectional pathway—yielding a cyclic semiconductor-metal-semiconductor evolution in the electronic band structure. Each ferroelectric configuration produces distinct layer-locked anomalous valley Hall conductivities, enabling four-state information encoding at the intersection of ferroelectricity, altermagnetism, and valley physics in a 2D van der Waals system. The results establish bilayer ReIrGe₂Se₆ as a multiferroic valleytronics platform offering nonvolatile, electrically switchable control of spin-valley-layer degrees of freedom without a net magnetic moment, making it compelling for low-power, high-density 2D spintronic memory devices.

### [Competing Orders Driven by Wigner Crystal Phase in Rhombohedral Graphene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15014)
*arXiv:2607.15014 — Z. Zhou, K. Krötzsch, R. Ayache, Y. Li, S. Joy, K. Watanabe, T. Taniguchi, M. Heiblum, P. Roulleau, M. Banerjee*

This experimental work identifies a Wigner crystal (WC) insulating phase on the low-carrier-density side of chiral superconductivity in rhombohedral pentalayer graphene (R5G), where strong electronic correlations and flat-band topology coexist at low densities and high displacement fields. Slightly hole-doping past the WC boundary produces a metallic Wigner crystal phase, and under out-of-plane magnetic fields the system hosts competing field-stabilized superconductivity and unconventional reentrant quantum Hall states that evolve continuously from the WC and metallic WC states. These results reveal that Wigner crystallization acts as a parent instability underpinning several emergent phases in rhombohedral graphene, analogous to the role of Mott-insulating parent states in moiré systems, and provide a key new piece in understanding the rich phase diagram of strongly correlated 2D carbon systems.

### [Wireless millikelvin interconnects for superconducting quantum hardware](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13834)
*arXiv:2607.13834 — University of Strathclyde and collaborators*

This paper addresses the scalability bottleneck of superconducting quantum processors—the dense forest of electrical wires connecting cryogenic chips to room-temperature control electronics—by demonstrating the first wireless excitation of a superconducting microwave resonator of the type used for qubit readout, operating at millikelvin temperatures inside a dilution refrigerator. A direct comparison of wired and wireless coupling configurations shows that wireless coupling preserves the intrinsic resonator frequency and linewidth while also revealing parasitic electromagnetic pathways arising from stray microwave radiation bouncing within the reflective cryostat cavity, a previously uncharacterized source of loss and crosstalk. These results represent a key proof of principle for wireless microwave cryogenic interconnects that could significantly reduce thermal loading and wiring density in large-scale superconducting quantum processors, complementing ongoing work on cryogenic CMOS and microwave-to-optical transduction.

### [Realization and manipulation of spiral charge density waves in a two-dimensional metal](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13878)
*arXiv:2607.13878 — Multiple authors*

This work engineers a micron-scale van der Waals strain network in the 2D metal NbSe₂ by exploiting substrate interactions, creating spatially inhomogeneous strain fields that lift the near-degeneracy of competing CDW orders and separate them into distinct spatial domains: the intrinsic 3×3 CDW in unstrained regions, a 4×1 unidirectional CDW under 1D-confined compression (stable up to 70 K, far above the bulk 33 K CDW), and a 2×2 CDW under biaxial tension. At nodes of the strain network, the CDW further organizes into chiral spiral textures that can be reversibly melted by electrical voltage pulses, demonstrating real-space control and chirality manipulation of CDW order. The results establish strain-engineered van der Waals heterostructures as a versatile platform for controlling competing electronic orders in 2D metals, with implications for memory elements and reconfigurable electronic phases.

### [Chiral, Electronically Decoupled Layers of 1T′-WS₂ Topological Insulator via Neutral-Molecule Intercalation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10457)
*arXiv:2607.10457 — J. Xie, F. Katmer et al.*

Monolayer 1T′-WS₂ is predicted to be a 2D topological insulator, but its electronic properties are inaccessible in the bulk parent phase 2M-WS₂ because strong interlayer coupling makes it a superconducting metal and renders mechanical exfoliation impractical. This paper shows that wet-chemical intercalation of neutral amine molecules into 2M-WS₂ expands the interlayer spacing from 0.5 to 1–4 nm, electronically decouples the 1T′ layers, and reversibly switches the material from a superconducting metal to an insulator whose activation gap matches that of an isolated 1T′ monolayer. Controlled de-intercalation recovers the original superconducting state, providing a reversible path between superconducting and topological insulator phases within the same material platform and opening direct experimental access to the symmetry-protected edge states predicted for monolayer 1T′-WS₂.
