## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Excitons in Large Disordered Boron-Nitride Layer using Linear-Scaling Bethe-Salpeter Simulations
[2606.30585](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30585) — Thomas Galvani, Lorenzo Sponza, Hakim Amara, Sylvain Latil, Stephan Roche — *cond-mat.mtrl-sci*

This paper introduces a real-space, linear-scaling (O(N)) Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) framework tailored for excitonic spectroscopy in large and structurally disordered two-dimensional materials, demonstrated here on monolayer hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN). The approach maps localized electron-hole pairs onto a sparse tight-binding model via a sublattice-resolved perturbative decoupling, then applies the Kernel Polynomial Method (KPM) to compute optical absorption spectra for systems with up to 10⁵ orbitals — scales inaccessible to conventional BSE methods. Applying this to Anderson-disordered h-BN, the authors find that random on-site potential fluctuations induce an asymmetric broadening of the bright exciton peak, a crossover from quadratic to linear energy redshift with increasing disorder strength, and ultimately Anderson localization of the exciton center of mass. This work is significant because it enables realistic, quantitative modeling of optical properties in large, defect-laden 2D materials, directly relevant to the understanding of light emission and exciton dynamics in experimentally synthesized h-BN films and heterostructures.

### Emergence of half-semiconductor behavior and tunable magnetism via carrier doping in Janus VXSe (X=Cl, Br, I) monolayers
[2606.28713](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28713) — Zhixiang Wang et al. — *cond-mat.mtrl-sci*

Using first-principles density functional theory calculations, this paper predicts that Janus vanadium chalcogenide monolayers VXSe (X = Cl, Br, I) form a new family of intrinsic ferromagnetic 2D semiconductors featuring indirect band gaps in the range 1.66–2.33 eV, Curie temperatures up to 128 K, and substantial magnetic anisotropy energies reaching 910 μeV per vanadium atom. The Janus symmetry (two distinct chalcogen surfaces) induces a built-in structural asymmetry that modulates the crystal field environment around V atoms, while the VISe variant is found to be a pristine half-semiconductor with fully spin-polarized conduction and valence bands at the Fermi level. Carrier doping unveils a rich magneto-electronic phase diagram: hole doping in VBrSe rotates the magnetic easy axis, enabling electrically switchable spin orientation for a proposed spin-field-effect transistor, while electron doping in VClSe and VBrSe unlocks fully spin-polarized metallic states. These findings establish Janus VXSe monolayers as a versatile 2D spintronic platform combining high magnetic anisotropy, electrical tunability, and symmetry-broken interface properties in a single atomic layer.

### Superconducting cavity probes sliding ferroelectricity in small-angle twisted WSe₂
[2606.22140](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22140) — Krishnendu Maji, Supriya Mandal, Meghan P. Patankar, Kenji Watanabe, Takashi Taniguchi, Vibhor Singh, Mandar M. Deshmukh (TIFR, Mumbai) — *cond-mat.mes-hall* — Published in *Nano Letters* 2026, 26, 21, 7157–7165

This work pioneers the use of a superconducting coplanar waveguide (CPW) cavity — the foundational circuit-QED microwave resonator — as a sensitive, non-invasive probe of sliding ferroelectricity in small-angle parallel-stacked bilayer WSe₂. By embedding the van der Waals heterostructure as part of the CPW's dielectric environment, the authors track the gate-voltage-dependent resonant frequency and quality factor of the microwave cavity, detecting hysteretic shifts that directly map ferroelectric domain switching in the 2D material. The technique additionally resolves time-domain relaxation dynamics associated with domain-wall motion, revealing the rich kinetics of ferroelectric switching that are invisible to DC transport measurements. This hybrid superconducting-circuit/2D-material approach represents a new paradigm for studying ferroelectric and other order parameters in van der Waals systems at microwave frequencies, with immediate relevance to quantum sensing, non-volatile 2D memory, and circuit-QED integration.

### Spin-orbit coupling driven topological superconductivity in twisted bilayer graphene-WSe₂ heterostructures
[2606.27013](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27013) — *cond-mat.mes-hall*

Starting from the Bistritzer-MacDonald continuum model of twisted bilayer graphene (tBLG) with proximity-induced Ising, Rashba, and intrinsic spin-orbit couplings inherited from an adjacent WSe₂ layer, this study constructs a full Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) Hamiltonian with conventional s-wave pairing to map out topological superconducting phases. The calculations identify multiple topological phases characterized by non-trivial Chern numbers, featuring effective p-wave pairing profiles and inverted band dispersions protected by bulk gaps, with chiral Majorana edge modes as their boundary signatures. The topological phase boundaries are tunable via the strength of proximity-induced spin-orbit coupling and the superconducting order parameter, which are both controllable in experiment through gate voltage and proximity to a bulk superconductor. The results provide targeted theoretical predictions for realizing topologically protected Majorana modes in experimentally accessible tBLG/WSe₂ heterostructures, building on demonstrated superconductivity in this material platform.
