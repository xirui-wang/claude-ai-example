## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Visualizing orbital magnetism in electron doped rhombohedral multilayer graphene
[2605.30316](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30316) — Owen I. Sheekey, Trevor B. Arp, Benjamin A. Foutty et al. — *cond-mat.mes-hall*

Rhombohedral multilayer graphene is a highly tunable 2D platform where a nearly flat band at high displacement fields drives strong many-body interactions and topological ground states. Using nanoSQUID-on-tip magnetometry, the authors spatially map the orbital magnetization of electron-doped rhombohedral graphene devices with thicknesses between 3 and 13 layers. At high displacement fields—where the band minimum is nearly flat with near-ideal quantum geometry—the system forms a 'quarter metal' by spontaneously selecting a single spin-valley flavor. The orbital magnetization within this phase peaks at finite carrier density, consistent with Berry curvature concentrated at a finite-momentum 'ring of fire' in the Fermi lune rather than at zero momentum. Strikingly, this same region of the density–displacement-field parameter space hosts a zero-resistance state attributed to a chiral superconductor with finite-momentum Cooper pairs, directly linking quantum geometry in 2D graphene multilayers to emergent topological superconductivity.

### Ferroelectric polarization controlled orbital Hall conductivity in d1T-phase monolayer MoS₂
[2605.17722](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17722) — *cond-mat.mtrl-sci / cond-mat.mes-hall*

The distorted 1T (d1T) phase of monolayer MoS₂ has been proposed as a 2D higher-order topological insulator, but exploiting its topological properties for device functionality requires an effective control knob. This work demonstrates theoretically that ferroelectric polarization switching in d1T-MoS₂ directly controls the orbital Hall conductivity, providing electrical on/off tuning of the orbital Hall effect without requiring magnetic fields or spin-orbit engineering. The mechanism relies on ferroelectric order coupling to the orbital texture of the topological bands, reversibly reconfiguring the Berry curvature distribution as the polarization is switched. This represents a new route to orbital Hall devices in 2D materials where the ferroelectric and topological degrees of freedom are intrinsically linked in a single monolayer material.

### Locally resolved electronic textures of reconstruction domains in marginally twisted monolayer-bilayer graphene
[2605.26393](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26393) — Sean M. Walker, Patrick Sarsfield, Isaac Soltero, et al. (Ottawa / Manchester / NIMS) — *cond-mat.mes-hall*

At small twist angles, the lattice mismatch between twisted graphene layers drives structural reconstruction into stacking-energy-minimum domains separated by narrow soliton walls. In marginally twisted monolayer-bilayer graphene, STM/STS measurements reveal two distinct local electronic environments: regions that locally resemble AB-stacked bilayer graphene (with a massive Dirac spectrum) and regions resembling AB-stacked monolayer graphene (with a massless Dirac spectrum). The reconstruction domain length scale and soliton width set a new spatial length scale governing both correlated and topological phenomena in this heterostructure, distinct from the moiré wavelength. Understanding these domain textures is critical for interpreting the transport and optical properties of marginally twisted graphene systems, where reconstruction competes with moiré-induced band engineering to determine the ultimate electronic ground state.

### Absolute measurement of penetration depth of superconducting thin films using microwave stripline resonators
[2605.28759](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28759) — Arghya Dutta, Ajeet Salunke, Mahesh Poojary, Vivas Bagwe, Sangita Bose, Pratap Raychaudhuri — *cond-mat.supr-con / cond-mat.mes-hall*

The London penetration depth λ encodes the superfluid density and is essential for understanding superconducting order, yet its absolute extraction from microwave resonator data—which measures only relative frequency shifts—requires disentangling material and geometric contributions. This work presents a hybrid method combining superconducting microstrip resonator measurements with COMSOL Multiphysics finite-element electromagnetic simulations to extract absolute λ(T) of NbN thin films over a wide temperature range. By fabricating resonators from NbN films of multiple thicknesses, the geometric and material contributions are self-consistently separated, yielding the first microwave-based absolute penetration depth without relying on surface impedance fitting assumptions. The technique is further extended to lower-Tc materials through a flip-film geometry where the sample is placed atop the NbN resonator separated by a thin Mylar spacer, shifting the resonant frequency in proportion to λ changes in the sample. This method has immediate application to quantum device engineering: accurate λ values directly determine kinetic inductance, resonator frequency, and qubit design parameters in superconducting quantum circuits and detectors.

### Magnon polaritons in a van der Waals ferromagnet coupled to a superconducting resonator
[2605.12298](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12298) — Alvaro Bermejillo-Seco et al. — *cond-mat.mes-hall*

Coupling 2D van der Waals magnetic materials to superconducting microwave quantum circuits is central to quantum magnonics, but the drastically reduced spin density of atomically thin flakes makes strong magnon–photon coupling far more challenging than in bulk magnets. This work demonstrates magnon–photon hybridization in exfoliated flakes of the van der Waals ferromagnet Cr₂Ge₂Te₆ (down to 30 nm thickness) coupled to a specially designed low-impedance superconducting resonator that concentrates the microwave magnetic field at the flake location. The resulting magnon polaritons—hybrid cavity-photon–magnon excitations—are unambiguously identified by reproducible avoided crossings measured across six independent devices, confirming the robustness of the coupling at the 2D limit. The coupling strength overcomes the reduced magnetic volume and increased dissipation of the thin-flake geometry, enabled by the resonator design optimizing field concentration. These results establish a scalable platform for integrating 2D magnetic materials with superconducting quantum circuits for microwave quantum transduction, on-chip spin-wave manipulation, and hybrid quantum memory applications.

### Electrically driven Rabi dynamics of magnetic-field-induced corner states in a 2D topological insulator
[2605.28499](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28499) — D. V. Khomitsky et al. — *cond-mat.mes-hall*

HgTe/CdHgTe quantum wells realize 2D topological insulators with helical edge states, and this work proposes a geometry where in-plane magnetic field gaps the helical edges at two kink locations, creating localized corner-state modes. At a double-kink structure, two such corner states form a well-defined two-level system (topological qubit) whose transition frequency falls in the 20–40 GHz microwave range for realistic HgTe parameters. Electrical driving via ac gate voltages couples to the corner states through their charge distribution, enabling electrically driven Rabi oscillations at GHz frequencies without the need for magnetic rf fields. This is a concrete microwave quantum information proposal using topological boundary states of a 2D topological insulator, combining the topological protection of the corner states with the established toolbox of superconducting microwave qubit control at GHz frequencies.
