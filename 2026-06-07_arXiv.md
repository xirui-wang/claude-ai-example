## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [2606.03710] Mechanochemical Nano-Writing of an Atomically Thin Metal
Zhang et al. demonstrate that localized mechanical force from an AFM tip can drive solid-state chemical reactions in van der Waals stacks at near-room temperature — a process normally requiring ~200 °C. Applied to bilayer MoTe₂ encapsulated adjacent to a Pd source, force accelerates Pd intercalation exponentially (Eyring stress-assisted kinetics), synthesizing the atomically thin superconductor Pd₇MoTe₂ with ~50 nm lateral resolution. The resulting material is verified to be superconducting, and the patterning resolution is sufficient for device-scale nanofabrication. This mechanochemical route opens a new strategy for synthesizing and patterning functional 2D materials — including superconductors — that cannot be produced by conventional thermal processing, with direct relevance to quantum circuit fabrication in 2D systems.

### [2606.02131] Moire-Engineered Excitonic Landscape and Phonon-Mediated Recombination in Twisted WSe₂ Bilayers
Thapa et al. report low-temperature photoluminescence of hBN-encapsulated twisted bilayer WSe₂/WSe₂, probing how the moiré superlattice potential reshapes the excitonic landscape. The spectra reveal strong interlayer excitonic emission driven by moiré confinement and distinct phonon-assisted recombination sidebands, while localized defect-bound excitons — dominant in untwisted bilayers — are strongly suppressed by twisting. The twist angle thus provides a clean tuning knob for switching between different excitonic channels. These results establish twisted TMD homostructures as versatile platforms for engineering photonic and excitonic properties at the 2D limit, with potential applications in moiré-based light-emitting devices.

### [2606.02338] Ferroelectric Hysteresis in Singly Aligned Graphene-hBN Moiré Superlattices
Tu et al. demonstrate, for the first time, a ferroelectric response in singly aligned single-layer graphene (SLG) on hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) moiré superlattices — extending moiré ferroelectricity beyond bilayer graphene systems. Hall measurements pinpoint the origin of the hysteretic polarization to anomalous charge screening arising from the moiré-flat-band structure, yielding quantitative estimates of the spontaneous polarization. The absence of a structural polar distortion makes this a purely electronic (band-driven) ferroelectricity in a non-centrosymmetric SLG/hBN moiré unit cell. These findings broaden the family of 2D ferroelectric platforms and suggest potential for non-volatile graphene-based memory devices exploiting moiré-flat-band physics.

### [2606.05356] Fermiology and the Candidate Chiral Superconductor in Rhombohedral Tetralayer Graphene
Kalantre et al. perform systematic quantum oscillation measurements across the phase diagram of rhombohedral tetralayer graphene, directly imaging the Fermi surface in the time-reversal-symmetry-broken normal state from which superconductivity emerges. Knowing whether this Fermi surface is compatible with a chiral pairing symmetry (winding order parameter in k-space) is the decisive test for topological superconductivity. The measurements reveal the Fermi surface topology and its evolution with carrier density, providing the experimentally constrained Fermiology needed to evaluate proposed chiral superconducting mechanisms. This work is a critical step toward establishing rhombohedral multilayer graphene as a platform for intrinsic topological superconductivity without magnetic fields or heterostructure engineering.

### [2606.03663] Enhanced Superconductivity in Atomically Thin Noble Metals: From Quantum Confinement to Interface-Induced Lifshitz Transition
Zhang et al. address a longstanding puzzle — bulk Cu, Ag, and Au are not superconductors — using first-principles calculations to show that quantum confinement in atomically thin films of these metals enhances the density of states and softens phonons sufficiently to induce superconductivity: trilayer Cu reaches T_c ≈ 0.78 K and pentalayer Au ≈ 0.63 K. Interface engineering provides an additional lever: in h-BN/Cu(111) heterostructures, the interfacial stacking configuration triggers a Lifshitz transition that further elevates T_c. The unified framework linking confinement and interface effects in noble metals opens a new materials design space, particularly relevant to thin-film and 2D superconducting quantum circuits where noble metals are widely used as contacts and interconnects.

### [2606.05813] Strongly Nonlinear Regime of Josephson Transmission Lines Revealed by Two-Tone Spectroscopy
Averkin et al. experimentally and theoretically characterize the off-resonant, strongly nonlinear regime of a Josephson transmission line (JTL) — a key element for traveling-wave parametric amplifiers and quantum-limited microwave amplification. Using a weak probe tone while sweeping a strong pump microwave signal, they measure large phase-length variations with pronounced oscillations that cannot be explained by perturbative (weakly nonlinear) treatments. A derived nonlinear wave equation for the JTL correctly reproduces this oscillatory behavior through the power-dependent Josephson inductances, identifying a new strongly nonlinear operating regime. These results are directly relevant to optimizing JTL-based traveling-wave parametric amplifiers (TWPAs) and other quantum microwave devices that operate at high microwave powers.
