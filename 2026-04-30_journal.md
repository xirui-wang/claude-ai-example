## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Super-moiré spin textures in twisted two-dimensional antiferromagnets
**Journal:** Nature Nanotechnology 21, 359–365 (2026) | **Link:** [s41565-025-02103-y](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-025-02103-y)

Twisting van der Waals antiferromagnets creates moiré magnetic landscapes whose spatial scales can vastly exceed the twist-angle-defined moiré wavelength. Using scanning nitrogen-vacancy (NV) magnetometry on twisted double-bilayer CrI₃ devices, the authors observe long-range magnetic textures spanning multiple moiré unit cells—a "super-moiré" magnetic state. Counterintuitively, the super-moiré texture size increases with twist angle (opposite to the moiré period), reaching ~300 nm in 1.1° devices—roughly an order of magnitude larger than the moiré wavelength. The magnetic field maps are consistent with the formation of antiferromagnetic Néel-type skyrmions spanning multiple moiré cells. This geometry-driven, current-free, lithography-free skyrmion nucleation establishes twist angle as a clean control parameter for topological spin textures, with direct relevance to low-power spintronic applications.

### Excitonic negative refraction mediated by magnetic orders in 2D magnet CrSBr
**Journal:** Nature Nanotechnology | **Link:** [s41565-025-02118-5](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-025-02118-5) | **Authors:** Zhang Xiang; Liu Xiaoze; Chen Zuxin et al.

CrSBr is a layered 2D antiferromagnet with spectrally narrow, strongly bound excitons whose resonance energies and oscillator strengths are intimately tied to the magnetic order. In the magnetically ordered phase, exchange-enhanced anisotropic excitonic response drives the real part of the dielectric constant negative along specific crystal axes, producing a hyperbolic optical iso-frequency contour that supports negative refraction of excitonic polaritons—a phenomenon observed here for the first time in a natural material without metamaterial engineering. By integrating a precisely cleaved CrSBr slab on a photonic chip, the authors demonstrate an on-chip excitonic hyperlens that focuses polariton modes to sub-wavelength spots via curved trajectories governed by the magnetic order. The device response is reconfigurable by modest external magnetic fields, pointing toward magnetically tunable, ultra-compact nanophotonic elements from 2D quantum magnets.

### Two-dimensional crystalline hard masks for high-aspect-ratio nanofabrication
**Journal:** Nature Materials | **Link:** [s41563-026-02524-7](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-026-02524-7) | **Published:** April 16, 2026

The continued scaling of three-dimensional semiconductor devices demands hard-mask materials with extraordinary plasma resistance and atomic-scale surface flatness. This work demonstrates that van der Waals metal oxyhalides—CrOCl and FeOCl—far outperform conventional hard-mask materials due to their strongly bonded, self-terminating crystalline surfaces. CrOCl achieves etch rates as low as ~2.4 nm min⁻¹ in SF₆/O₂ plasma with >200:1 selectivity over silicon—a ~30× improvement over Si₃N₄ and ~20× over TiN under identical conditions. Remarkably, plasma exposure induces surface smoothening in CrOCl rather than roughening, maintaining sub-nanometre roughness throughout etching. The masks can be chemically patterned with Cl₂ plasma and mechanically transferred onto diverse substrates including perovskite oxides, polymers, glasses, and monolayer 2D semiconductors, establishing ultrathin 2D crystalline oxyhalides as a versatile platform for next-generation semiconductor nanopatterning.
