## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [2606.08237] Simultaneous nanoscale imaging of local conductivity and chemical potential in a quantum Hall isospin ferromagnet *(2D Materials + Microwave Engineering)*

This paper introduces **scanning conductivity and chemical potential microscopy (SCCM)**, a multi-modal near-field technique that combines **scanning microwave impedance microscopy (sMIM)** — which maps local AC conductivity — with **Kelvin probe force microscopy (KPFM)** — which maps local electrostatic potential — in a single scan. By integrating both capabilities, SCCM simultaneously reveals both the dissipative (transport) and thermodynamic (energetic) properties of quantum materials at the nanoscale. The technique is first validated on the well-understood quantum Hall states in **bilayer graphene**, where it reproduces the expected Landau level energy spectrum and maps the conductivity landscape at the integer filling factors. The authors then apply SCCM to **marginally twisted double bilayer graphene**, uncovering a cascade of quantum Hall isospin ferromagnetic states driven by the interplay of spin, valley, orbital, and layer degrees of freedom. This work represents a significant methodological advance for probing strongly correlated 2D electron systems, enabling direct comparison of local compressibility and dissipation in the same correlated state.

### [2606.25861] Sensitivity-optimal coplanar waveguide design for broadband magnetic resonance spectroscopy: a Beer–Lambert framework *(Microwave Engineering)*

Coplanar waveguide (CPW) transmission spectroscopy is a versatile microwave technique for probing spin dynamics, ferromagnetic resonance (FMR), and complex AC conductivity across a broad range of condensed-matter samples. Despite widespread use, no systematic framework had previously connected CPW geometry to measurement sensitivity for fixed sample volume and concentration. This paper shows that the shared geometric scaling of **sample-to-waveguide coupling** and **conductor Ohmic loss** maps the CPW design problem onto the classical **Beer–Lambert optimization** of optical spectrophotometry. The result is a **universal one-parameter solution** whose optimal geometry depends only on the sample's aspect ratio and the dominant noise source (thermal or amplifier noise), not on sample material properties, frequency, or system losses. This framework gives practitioners a principled, quantitative route to designing sensitivity-optimized CPW circuits for broadband microwave spectroscopy experiments.

### [2606.26632] Floquet-Engineered Chern Insulator in two-dimensional d_{x²−y²}-Wave Altermagnets *(2D Materials)*

Altermagnets are a recently identified class of antiferromagnets that exhibit spin splitting in momentum space without net magnetization, and their 2D realizations offer promising platforms for spintronics. This work demonstrates that **periodic Floquet driving** of a 2D **d_{x²−y²}-wave altermagnet** can engineer a non-trivial **Chern insulator** phase with topologically protected edge states, even in the absence of spin-orbit coupling. The study maps out the Floquet phase diagram as a function of drive amplitude and frequency, identifying parameter windows where the Chern number changes and where topological edge modes emerge. The results, published in Physical Review B 113, 155439 (2026), establish Floquet engineering as a tool for generating topological order in the new family of altermagnetic 2D materials.

### [2606.05093] Exchange-mediated exciton splitting and linear dichroism in monolayer TMD induced by ferroelectric substrates *(2D Materials)*

Monolayer transition-metal dichalcogenides (TMDs) such as MoSe₂ and WSe₂ host tightly bound excitons at the K/K′ valleys, making them attractive for valleytronics. This work predicts that placing a monolayer TMD on a **ferroelectric substrate** induces **exchange-mediated splitting** between two low-energy exciton states via proximity to the substrate's electric dipole order, breaking the valley degeneracy without an external magnetic field. The resulting symmetry lowering produces **linear dichroism** — anisotropic optical absorption — that can be tuned by switching the ferroelectric polarization direction. This mechanism establishes a framework for designing electrically switchable two-level excitonic systems in TMD heterostructures, with potential for optically addressable quantum bits and non-volatile optical memory in 2D material stacks.
