# Condensed Matter Journal Weekly Summary — 2026-06-11

> Covering the week of 2026-06-09 through 2026-06-11 across six journals.

## Condensed Matter Papers This Week

| Journal | Title | Major Authors | Summary |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Nature Physics** | [Spatially anisotropic Kondo resonance coupled with the superconducting gap in a kagome metal](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-026-03292-6) | Ji group, Renmin University et al. | Dilute Cr magnetic impurities in the kagome superconductor CsV₃Sb₅ generate spatially anisotropic Kondo resonances intertwined with the superconducting gap, while simultaneously weakening charge-density-wave order, revealing a rich interplay of magnetism, Kondo physics, and superconductivity. |
| **Nature Physics** | [Symmetry-broken Kondo screening and zero-energy mode in a kagome superconductor](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-026-03223-5) | — | Observes symmetry-broken Kondo screening and a zero-energy spectroscopic mode in a kagome superconductor, providing evidence for unconventional impurity bound states with potential topological character. |
| **Nature Communications** | [Realization of fermionic Laughlin state on a quantum processor](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-72769-y) | — (IonQ Aria-1 collaboration) | A ν = 1/3 fermionic Laughlin state was realized on a 16-qubit trapped-ion quantum processor using a Hamiltonian variational ansatz with 369 two-qubit gates; symmetry-verification error mitigation confirmed its topological signatures—correlation hole and chiral edge modes—opening a scalable route to simulating strongly correlated topological quantum matter. |
| **Nature Communications** | [Synchronized quantum dance of excitons and phonons in perovskite nanocrystals](https://phys.org/news/2026-06-physicists-synchronized-quantum-excitons-phonons.html) | Trifonov et al. | Direct observation of coherent exciton-phonon coupling in lead-halide perovskite nanocrystals at 2 K, manifested as pronounced quantum beats with long coherence times, establishing a coupled light-matter quantum state beyond conventional Frenkel exciton physics. |
| **Nature Communications** | [Carrier density modulation of a charge-density-wave condensate by electrical gating in TaS₃ nanowires](https://www.nature.com/subjects/condensed-matter-physics/ncomms) | Hao Chen, Jumin Shi, X. J. Zhou et al. | Electrical gating of orthorhombic tantalum trisulfide nanowires directly and strongly modulates the carrier density of the charge-density-wave condensate, demonstrating gate-tunable CDW order in a quasi-1D material. |
| **PRL** | [Topological Transition and Emergence of Elasticity of Dislocation in Skyrmion Lattice: Beyond Kittel's Magnetic-Polar Analogy](https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.136.236703) | Kohta Kasai, Akihiro Uematsu, Takahiro Shimada et al. | Magnetic skyrmion dislocations develop a core-split structure via skyrmion elongation of up to 180%, reaching a topological transition from a single skyrmion to two half-skyrmions, yet the long-range strain field rigorously obeys conventional Volterra elasticity theory; the Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interaction drives the large core deformation. |
