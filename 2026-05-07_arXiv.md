## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Gossamer Superconductivity in Moiré WSe₂ Bilayer
**arXiv:** [2605.03766](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03766) — Hui-Ke Jin, Guangyue Ji, Zhan Wang, Jie Wang, Fu-Chun Zhang

Moiré transition-metal dichalcogenide bilayers have emerged as a uniquely controllable platform for simulating Hubbard physics, and recent experiments have confirmed robust superconductivity in twisted WSe₂ at half-filling with zero displacement field. This work maps the system onto an effective extended single-orbital Hubbard model on the triangular lattice and applies renormalized mean-field theory to explore the strong-coupling phase diagram. The key finding is that a moderate on-site Coulomb repulsion partially suppresses charge fluctuations while leaving a finite density of mobile doublons and holes, giving rise to "gossamer" superconductivity—a pairing state in which the condensate coexists with strong Mott-like correlations. This mechanism is distinct from conventional phonon-mediated pairing and places twisted WSe₂ alongside cuprates and heavy-fermion compounds as a test bed for strongly correlated superconductivity. The results also predict competing charge-ordered phases near integer filling that could be probed by scanning tunnelling spectroscopy.

### Polarization-Controlled Effective Rabi Dynamics in Driven Graphene: A Floquet-Magnus Approach
**arXiv:** [2605.00325](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00325) — V. G. Ibarra-Sierra, J. L. Cardoso et al.

This paper applies high-frequency Floquet-Magnus perturbation theory to monolayer graphene driven by a circularly or linearly polarized laser field, demonstrating that the polarization state of the drive directly controls effective Rabi-like oscillations between pairs of Dirac-cone eigenstates. For circular polarization the effective Hamiltonian acquires a topological gap (Haldane-like mass term), while for linear polarization an anisotropic Rabi coupling mixes states across the two valleys, enabling valley-selective population inversion. The approach yields closed-form analytical expressions for the Rabi frequency, detuning, and decay envelope as functions of field amplitude, photon energy, and Fermi level, all benchmarked against full numerical Floquet calculations. These results suggest that femtosecond laser pulses with controlled polarization could be used to implement all-optical coherent control of the graphene electronic state, with potential applications in ultrafast graphene-based photonic switches and valley-Hall devices.
