## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [Morphology control and low-temperature magnetotransport in chiral 2D perovskite R-(MBA)₂PbI₄](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13664)
*(2D Materials — cond-mat.mtrl-sci)*

Two-dimensional chiral hybrid organic-inorganic perovskites such as R/S-(MBA)₂PbI₄ are considered leading candidates for chirality-dependent spin and charge transport, but their notoriously high in-plane resistance had prevented electrical characterization. This work by Aslam, Hautzinger, Beard, Singh et al. overcomes the bottleneck by systematically engineering the thin-film morphology of R-(MBA)₂PbI₄ through controlled crystallization conditions, producing films with sufficient lateral continuity for reliable ohmic contact formation. Low-temperature magnetotransport measurements on the resulting lateral devices reveal clear signatures of chirality-dependent conduction, opening the door to spintronic exploitation of this material class. The approach demonstrates that morphology control—rather than materials chemistry—is the critical variable for device integration of 2D chiral perovskites. This is the first demonstration of robust lateral electrical measurements in a chiral 2D perovskite, establishing a platform for probing chiral-induced spin selectivity at low temperatures.

### [Proximity-induced unconventional superconductivity and chiral topological phases in twisted graphene/NbSe₂ van der Waals heterostructure](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12104)
*(2D Materials — cond-mat.supr-con)*

This paper by Hložný and Milivojević addresses proximity-induced superconductivity in a twisted graphene/NbSe₂ van der Waals heterostructure using the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) formalism. At a twist angle of 23.4°, the common symmetry of the graphene/NbSe₂ system is reduced to C₃, and the proximity-induced pairing inherits the unconventional character of NbSe₂. The authors extract normal-state graphene parameters from ab initio calculations and self-consistently solve the BdG equations, mapping out a rich phase diagram of chiral topological superconducting phases characterized by nonzero Chern numbers C ∈ {−4, −2, 2, 4}. Such chiral phases host protected chiral Majorana edge modes relevant to topological quantum computation. The work demonstrates that the twist angle in 2D van der Waals heterostructures is a powerful knob for engineering topological superconducting order, complementing recent experimental realizations of proximity superconductivity in graphene-based devices.

### [Engineering electrically-switchable quantum anomalous Hall states by spin-orbit coupling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13651)
*(2D Materials — cond-mat.mes-hall / cond-mat.mtrl-sci)*

This experimental study by Qin, Wang, Kim, Watanabe, Taniguchi, Simon, Parameswaran, and Polshyn demonstrates that proximity coupling to WSe₂ provides an engineered route to nonvolatile electrical switching of quantum anomalous Hall (QAH) states in twisted monolayer-bilayer graphene. While prior QAH switching relied on the intrinsic magnetic energy landscape of moiré systems, the authors show that WSe₂-induced spin-orbit coupling (SOC) modifies the competing magnetization states through spin-valley locking, reshaping the energy barriers involved in switching without requiring an external magnetic field reset. The resulting devices display robust nonvolatile QAH switching controllable entirely by gate voltage, a key requirement for topological electronics based on chiral edge-state transport. This approach generalizes to other graphene moiré systems and opens a pathway toward voltage-programmable topological transistors. The result also highlights proximity-induced SOC as a versatile design parameter for controlling magnetic textures in 2D moiré materials.

### [Dynamical Control of Superconductivity in Superconductor-Ferromagnet Bilayers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12544)
*(Microwave Engineering — cond-mat.supr-con)*

Pimenov, Poniatowski, Bøttcher, Yacoby, and Chowdhury study a ferromagnetic metal proximitized by a fully-gapped s-wave superconductor and coupled to a **microwave resonator**, making this a direct microwave-engineering problem in the superconducting context. In equilibrium the proximity effect produces a nodal p-wave gap in the ferromagnet. When the magnon and photon frequencies are made comparable and an external microwave drive is applied, the hybridized driven magnon-polaritons dynamically gap out the nodal points, inducing a non-equilibrium crossover to a fully gapped (pₓ+ipᵧ)-superconducting state. This represents an on-demand, microwave-controlled topological transition in a solid-state device: the system switches between a topologically trivial nodal phase and a topologically non-trivial gapped phase by tuning microwave drive parameters. The work has implications for quantum information platforms where superconductor-ferromagnet bilayers integrated with microwave resonators are already fabricated for circuit-QED detection, suggesting that such existing architectures could be repurposed to dynamically engineer the pairing symmetry.
