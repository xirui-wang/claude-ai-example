## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [Breaking the Trade-off: Bulk 2D Ising Superconductivity in (BaS)₁/₃TaS₂](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07336)
*2D Materials — Ziyi Zhu et al., arXiv:2605.07336*

Two-dimensional Ising superconductors, in which strong spin-orbit coupling locks electron spins to the out-of-plane axis, exhibit extraordinary resilience to in-plane magnetic fields far exceeding the Pauli paramagnetic limit. However, realizing this physics in bulk (rather than atomically thin exfoliated flakes) has been hampered by a fundamental conflict: increasing the interlayer spacing to preserve the 2D character and local inversion symmetry breaking generally reduces the superconducting transition temperature. Zhu et al. resolve this trade-off by synthesizing (BaS)₁/₃TaS₂, a new van der Waals compound in which unusual Ba–S–S–Ba chain units are intercalated between TaS₂ bilayers, achieving a giant inter-bilayer spacing of 12.75 Å—more than three times that of pristine 2H-TaS₂—while simultaneously breaking the bulk c-axis mirror symmetry, which allows local inversion symmetry breaking within each TaS₂ layer to dominate. The resulting material is confirmed as a bulk 2D Ising superconductor with both high Tc and strong 2D character, demonstrated by in-plane upper critical fields far above the Pauli limit. This work establishes chain intercalation as a general strategy for engineering 2D superconductors with simultaneously enhanced Tc and preserved Ising character, with potential relevance to topological superconductivity and quantum information applications in van der Waals systems.

### [Gossamer Superconductivity in Moiré WSe₂ Bilayer](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03766)
*2D Materials — Hui-Ke Jin et al., arXiv:2605.03766*

Twisted bilayer WSe₂ has emerged as a leading moiré platform hosting both Mott insulating and superconducting states at twist angles near 5°, but the microscopic pairing mechanism has remained debated. Jin et al. propose a "gossamer" superconductivity scenario, wherein the system is mapped onto an extended single-orbital Hubbard model on an effective triangular lattice and treated with renormalized mean-field theory (RMFT). A key finding is that moderate but not extreme Coulomb repulsion only partially suppresses charge fluctuations, leaving a finite density of itinerant doubly-occupied sites (doublons) and holes that can condense into Cooper pairs—a mechanism reminiscent of Zhang's gossamer superconductivity in doped Mott insulators. The phase diagram from RMFT reproduces the experimentally observed narrow superconducting dome at half-filling and zero displacement field, providing a unified picture of the strongly correlated insulating parent state and the adjacent superconductivity. This work deepens the analogy between moiré TMD bilayers and cuprate high-temperature superconductors and suggests that doublon-mediated pairing may be a robust mechanism in triangular-lattice moiré systems.

### [Multilayer Model for Coatings with Arbitrary Layers for Superconducting Radio-Frequency Applications](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07958)
*Microwave Engineering — Aaron Gobeyn, Wolfgang Ackermann, Herbert De Gersem (TU Darmstadt), arXiv:2605.07958*

Superconducting radio-frequency (SRF) cavities for particle accelerators are traditionally fabricated from bulk niobium, but replacing the interior with thin-film multilayer coatings of higher-Tc materials (e.g., NbTiN, Nb₃Sn) in a superconductor-insulator-superconductor (SIS) architecture promises to raise the maximum accelerating gradient by delaying vortex penetration. Gobeyn et al. generalize the existing SIS model in two important ways: first, the framework is extended from two-layer stacks to arbitrary sequences of superconducting, normal-conducting, and insulating layers; second, ohmic and dielectric losses—previously neglected—are fully incorporated into the field penetration and energy dissipation calculations. Applied to (SI)ⁿS stacks, the model shows that the n=1 configuration (one superconducting coating layer) optimizes performance, and that coating layers can be thinned below their penetration depth with only minor degradation, relaxing deposition requirements. A virtual-layer formalism is introduced to model interdiffusion and non-abrupt boundaries in superconductor-superconductor bilayers (SS), revealing that thicker transition zones degrade the achievable peak surface field. These results provide actionable design rules for next-generation SRF coatings in accelerator cavities.

### [Numerical Quality Factor Statistics for SRF Cavities with Spatially Inhomogeneous Multilayer Coatings](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05505)
*Microwave Engineering — Aaron Gobeyn, Wolfgang Ackermann, Herbert De Gersem (TU Darmstadt), arXiv:2605.05505*

Coating SRF cavities with SIS multilayer structures is complicated by the inherently non-flat cavity geometry: physical vapor deposition over curved surfaces inevitably produces spatially varying coating thicknesses, which modulate both the local screening length and dissipation. Gobeyn et al. address this by representing thickness inhomogeneity as a Gaussian random field generated by solving a stochastic partial differential equation parameterized by a correlation length scale, allowing systematic variation from nearly uniform to highly patchy coatings. The random thickness field is incorporated as a spatially varying boundary condition in the cavity microwave eigenvalue problem, and a Monte Carlo ensemble of realizations is used to compute statistical distributions of resonant frequency and quality factor Q. Key results show that correlated (large-scale) thickness variations are more damaging to Q than uncorrelated fluctuations of the same variance, providing clear tolerancing targets for coating process development. Together with the companion paper (arXiv:2605.07958), this work supplies both the physical model and the statistical engineering framework needed to optimize SRF multilayer coatings for real accelerator cavities.
