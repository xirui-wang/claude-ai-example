### [2604.11739] Topological Kondo Insulator from Spin Loop Currents
**ArXiv:** [2604.11739](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11739) | **Category:** cond-mat.str-el

This theoretical study establishes that interacting electrons in AB-stacked MoTe₂/WSe₂ moiré bilayers realize a topological Kondo insulator at hole filling ν=2 per moiré unit cell—requiring no external magnetic field or strain engineering. The key mechanism involves non-local electron-electron interactions that allow intrinsic, quantum-geometry-induced spin loop currents to self-consistently renormalize the effective band structure, lifting accidental degeneracies and opening a full topological gap in the spectrum. Real-frequency dynamical mean-field theory is used to capture Kondo correlations, while Hartree-Fock handles the non-local interaction terms, providing a fully self-consistent many-body treatment beyond static mean field. This finding positions MoTe₂/WSe₂ moiré heterostructures as an experimentally accessible platform for topological heavy-fermion physics, linking van der Waals 2D materials engineering with exotic correlated topological phases with potential implications for fault-tolerant quantum computing.

### [2604.11361] Ultrafast Ghost Hall States in a 2D Altermagnet
**ArXiv:** [2604.11361](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11361) | **Category:** cond-mat.mes-hall / cond-mat.mtrl-sci  
**Authors:** Ruikai Wu, Deepika Gill, Sangeeta Sharma, Sam Shallcross

Two-dimensional altermagnets host inequivalent valleys with opposite spin character; this work shows that femtosecond linear-polarized light pulses can selectively populate one valley, with the excited valley determined by the polarization orientation—enabling all-optical, ultrafast valley switching. The selective excitation gives rise to two distinct regimes: (i) a valleytronics regime generating nearly 100% spin-polarized valley currents, and (ii) a "ghost Hall" regime in which spin and charge currents flow orthogonally without any Hall mechanism, constituting a new transport state unique to altermagnets. These effects occur on femtosecond timescales, offering orders-of-magnitude speed advantages over conventional spintronic switching. The findings open a route to ultrafast, purely optical spin-valley control in 2D altermagnet thin films with applications in next-generation spintronic devices operating at THz frequencies.

### [2604.09050] Tantalum-Encapsulated Niobium Superconducting Resonators: High Q and Temporal Stability
**ArXiv:** [2604.09050](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09050) | **Category:** cond-mat.supr-con  
**Authors:** Anas Alkhazaleh, Juan Villegas, Florent Ravaux, Alexey Zharinov (Quantum Research Centre, TII Abu Dhabi)

Superconducting coplanar waveguide (CPW) resonators underpin qubit readout and coupling in quantum processors, and their internal quality factor Qi is fundamentally limited by two-level system (TLS) microwave loss at the NbOx surface oxide of niobium films. This work introduces in-situ Ta encapsulation: Nb is capped with a thin tantalum overlayer immediately after deposition, replacing the lossy Nb₂O₅/NbOx interface with a Ta-based oxide whose TLS density is substantially lower. Fresh Ta-capped devices reach Qi up to 2.4×10⁶ in the single-photon power regime, with a power dependence characteristic of TLS saturation at the metal-air interface. Critically, aging tests after six months show that Ta-encapsulated resonators degrade only moderately and still outperform freshly fabricated bare-Nb devices, indicating that the passivation layer provides lasting protection against re-oxidation. These results establish surface-engineering through in-situ metal capping as a practical, industry-compatible route to simultaneously achieving high initial quality factors and long-term stability in superconducting microwave circuits.

### [2604.08813] Coherence of Niobium Resonators via Fast-Sealing Microwave Cavity
**ArXiv:** [2604.08813](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08813) | **Category:** cond-mat.supr-con  
**Authors:** Chi Zhang, R. Germond, N. Janzen (Waterloo), A.-M. Valente-Feliciano (Jefferson Lab), M. Bal (Fermilab), A. Lupascu (Waterloo)

The dominant loss mechanism in niobium CPW resonators is TLS noise at the metal-air interface arising from rapid NbOx regrowth after wet-chemical oxide removal. This study develops a fast-sealing microwave cavity that allows devices to be placed under vacuum within five minutes of HF surface treatment, preventing re-oxidation before measurement. Niobium coplanar stripline resonators sealed using this method achieve internal quality factors exceeding 10⁶ at single-photon power—a factor of several improvement over unprotected devices. Re-exposure to air causes systematic resonance frequency shifts and Qi degradation that are quantitatively consistent with NbOx regrowth, validating the causal mechanism. The fast-sealing approach is cryostat-compatible and requires no changes to materials or fabrication, providing a straightforward experimental protocol to isolate and eliminate metal-air TLS loss in Nb-based superconducting quantum devices.
