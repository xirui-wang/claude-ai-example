## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [2604.12264] Polymer-free van der Waals assembly of 2D material heterostructures using muscovite crystals

Current polymer-based transfer methods for vdW heterostructure fabrication suffer from organic contamination, residue-induced strain, and poor determinism. This paper addresses those problems by replacing polymer stamps with thin muscovite (natural mica) crystals whose surface adhesion can be dynamically tuned by temperature — hot mica releases flakes while cooled mica picks them up deterministically. Because muscovite is inorganic and crystalline, the heterostructure interfaces it assembles are pristine and strain-free compared to PC or PDMS-handled samples. The technique is validated on demanding architectures including moiré superlattices, suspended membranes, and structures with exposed conductive layers, and is fully compatible with existing lithography workflows.

### [2604.11595] Pt-wedge squeegee cleaning of two-dimensional materials and heterostructures

Bubble and blister formation at 2D material interfaces is a long-standing bottleneck in device fabrication. This work introduces an AFM-based mechanical cleaning approach where a platinum wedge is deposited on a standard cantilever via focused ion beam, converting it into a squeegee tool. The Pt-wedge geometry dramatically increases the contact footprint, raising the cleaning rate from 0.01 μm²/s (pointy tip) to 3 μm²/s — a 300-fold improvement in throughput. WS₂ monolayers on h-BN cleaned by this method show sharper room-temperature photoluminescence emission and improved electrical contacts to gold electrodes, while h-BN-encapsulated heterostructures can also be rapidly cleaned. This technique is practical and broadly applicable across 2D material platforms.

### [2604.12824] All optical ultrafast pure spin current in the altermagnet Cr₂SO

Two-dimensional d-wave altermagnets like Cr₂SO are highly attractive for spintronics because they have very low spin-orbit coupling; however, this same property — combined with the material's magnetic point group symmetry — would normally prevent the generation of pure spin currents. This paper resolves the paradox by showing that infrared light couples linearly to spin-opposite valleys due to a valley selection rule, and that a simultaneously applied THz pulse imparts a finite momentum shift to the resulting spin excitation, breaking the cancellation between opposite-valley contributions and producing a nearly 100% spin-polarized current. The result is an entirely all-optical, ultrafast route to pure spin current generation in an environment with minimal intrinsic spin mixing, opening practical pathways for lightwave-controlled spintronic devices using d-wave 2D altermagnets.

### [2604.11738] Reduced pair breaking from extended disorder in unconventional superconductors: implications to 4Hb-TaS₂

Unconventional superconductors are expected to be strongly suppressed by non-magnetic disorder (Abrikosov–Gorkov theory), yet layered 2D materials such as the transition metal dichalcogenide 4Hb-TaS₂ exhibit clear signatures of unconventional pairing despite high resistivities. This paper provides a theoretical resolution: when impurities are spatially extended rather than point-like, the pair-breaking rate is parametrically suppressed (Γτ_D ~ 1/3) over a wide range of parameters, because the momentum structure of the extended potential partially matches the gap function and cancels pair-breaking matrix elements. The transport scattering rate is unaffected, meaning conventional resistivity measurements overestimate disorder's impact on pairing. This mechanism naturally explains the robustness of unconventional pairing in high-resistivity 2D superconductors and is broadly applicable to other layered materials with extended defects.
