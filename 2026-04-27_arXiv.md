## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [Nickel intercalation in epitaxial graphene on SiC(0001): a novel platform for engineering two-dimensional heterostructures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21739)

Two-dimensional magnetic materials integrated with graphene are a compelling platform for spintronic devices, yet 2D Ni had remained unexplored due to synthesis limitations. This work demonstrates scalable intercalation of Ni beneath epitaxial graphene on SiC(0001): chemically synthesized Ni nanoparticles (~10 nm) are deposited at room temperature via immersion in colloidal solution, then annealed at 650 °C to drive Ni beneath the graphene/buffer-layer interface, yielding well-ordered Ni islands whose morphology is controlled by annealing conditions. STM and ARPES confirm a preserved graphene band structure, while DFT calculations predict thermodynamically stable 2D nanostructures with an average magnetic moment of 0.9 μB/atom and robust interfacial magnetism stable under ambient conditions. The method provides a reproducible, scalable pathway to 2D magnetic graphene heterostructures with implications for spin-filtering, exchange-proximity coupling, and on-chip spintronic integration.

### [Cryogenic shock exfoliation for ultrahigh mobility rhombohedral graphite nanoelectronics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21912)

Rhombohedral multilayer graphene (RMG) is an exceptionally tunable platform for correlated electron physics — with gate-controllable superconducting, magnetic, and topological phases — but its practical use has been limited by the scarcity of rhombohedral stacking in natural graphite and correspondingly low sample yields. This paper introduces "cryogenic shock exfoliation," which produces large-area rhombohedral graphene flakes that, combined with a low-pressure van der Waals assembly technique designed to preserve stacking order, yield highly uniform devices exceeding 1300 μm² at a fabrication success rate of 90%. Scanning nanoSQUID-on-tip imaging demonstrates spatially uniform spin magnetism across the full central 10 × 10 μm² device area, and transverse magnetic focusing measurements reveal a disorder mean free path exceeding 200 μm at low temperatures — benchmark quality for systematic correlated-phase studies. These advances lower the barrier to RMG device fabrication and should enable reproducible exploration of its rich phase diagram across a range of layer numbers and twist angles.

### [Electronic and Vibrational Properties of On-Surface Synthesized Gulf-Edged Chiral Graphene Nanoribbons](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21796)

Atomically precise graphene nanoribbons with engineered edge topologies are key targets for 1D quantum materials, yet the synthesis of gulf-edged variants has been hindered by side reactions during on-surface polymerization. This work introduces a new precursor design in which biphenyl substituents on a trisnaphthalene core impose conformational constraints that suppress undesired reactions, enabling the first on-surface synthesis of a gulf-edged chiral GNR [(4,2,7)-chGNR] as confirmed by non-contact atomic force microscopy. Scanning tunneling spectroscopy identifies the ribbon as a closed-shell semiconductor with a bandgap of approximately 1.8 eV, while Raman spectroscopy reveals a distinctive C-H out-of-plane vibration at the gulf edge that provides an unambiguous structural fingerprint for this edge topology. Despite the large bandgap and non-spin-polarized edges, ambient instability is observed — a finding that connects GNR stability to specific edge structural features and motivates encapsulation strategies for device integration.
