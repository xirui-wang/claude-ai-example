## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Coherent 2D–3D van der Waals perovskite epitaxial heterostructures [Nature Nanotechnology, s41565-026-02201-5]
This work establishes a strategy for growing three-dimensional (3D) methylammonium tin iodide (MASnI₃) perovskite single crystals directly on two-dimensional (2D) butylammonium tin iodide [(3T)₂SnI₄] template layers via cross-dimensional van der Waals epitaxy. The growth achieves atomically sharp, structurally coherent interfaces that can be resolved in real space by low-dose aberration-corrected transmission electron microscopy and ptychographic imaging, revealing well-ordered spacer ligands at the heterointerface and uniform interfacial passivation. A particularly striking feature is that topological defects (dislocations and screw dislocations) in the 2D template layer seed spiral 3D crystal growth, producing chiral heterostructures with pronounced and tunable chiroptical responses in the visible spectrum. The method is scalable to macroscopic single-crystal thin films, establishing a versatile and passivation-integrated platform for next-generation hybrid perovskite optoelectronics, including chirality-selective photodetectors and LEDs, where interface quality is the principal bottleneck.

### Anomalous ultrafast lithium-ion transport through boron nitride nanotube membranes [Nature Nanotechnology 21, 831–838 (2026)]
Researchers fabricate macroscopic membranes of vertically aligned boron nitride nanotubes (BNNTs) with densities up to 10⁷ pores cm⁻² and systematically measure aqueous transport of Li⁺, Na⁺, and K⁺ through 3-nm and 12-nm-diameter charged BNNT channels. They observe that Li⁺ transport is anomalously fast—exceeding Fickian diffusion predictions by up to 31-fold—and is highly selective over the heavier alkali-metal cations. This anomalous selectivity and speed is fundamentally distinct from transport in carbon nanotubes of comparable diameter or in two-dimensional hexagonal boron nitride nanochannels, and is attributed to the polar B–N bond environment that electrostatically stabilizes the smaller, harder Li⁺ ion uniquely well. The findings demonstrate a one-dimensional nanochannel material platform that combines ultrahigh selectivity with ultrafast throughput—two properties that normally trade off—with immediate practical applications in osmotic "blue energy" harvesting from river–sea salinity gradients and in scalable membrane-based lithium extraction for battery supply chains.

### Magnonic spontaneous oscillation induced by parametric pumping [Nature Communications, doi:10.1038/s41467-026-71916-9]
The authors study a 100-nm-thick yttrium iron garnet (YIG) thin-film stripe with two parallel coplanar waveguides (CPW) lithographically patterned on its surface, one for parametric microwave pumping at twice the target spin-wave frequency and one for probing the spin-wave response. When the parametric pump exceeds a threshold amplitude, spontaneous magnonic auto-oscillation sets in, producing ultrasharp spin-wave modes with quality (Q) factors exceeding 200,000—among the highest demonstrated for solid-state spin-wave devices. These spontaneous modes behave as non-linear auto-oscillators that can phase-lock to a weak external microwave reference, enabling injection locking with phase noise suppression, and simultaneously function as magnon parametric amplifiers that provide gain determined by the oscillation amplitude to a weak co-propagating probe signal. The demonstration establishes parametric pumping in YIG films as a highly controllable route to nonlinear spin-wave dynamics, with direct relevance to magnon-based microwave signal processing (reconfigurable filters, amplifiers, and frequency converters), synchronization physics, and microwave-to-magnon quantum transduction in hybrid quantum systems.
