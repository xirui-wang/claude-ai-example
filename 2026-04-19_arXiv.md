## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [Mode-Resolved Multiband Ballistic Transport and Conductance Thresholds in Bilayer Graphene Junctions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07504)
**arXiv:2604.07504 — Dan-Na Liu, Pierre A. Pantaleón (IMDEA Nanoscience)**

This work presents a full four-band analysis of ballistic electron transport in bilayer graphene (BLG) junctions, revealing how electrostatic gating, interlayer bias, and homogeneous strain provide complementary, tunable control over electron transmission. In the absence of strain, symmetry constraints suppress transmission at specific incidence angles (pseudo-spin selection rules), while an interlayer bias lifts this suppression through mode mixing and opens a transport gap that is continuously adjustable. Going beyond the two-band approximation, the authors identify a new conductance threshold marking the onset of propagation in the upper Hamiltonian band inside the barrier, producing a step-like slope change in the total conductance. This multiband fingerprint serves as a direct, experimentally accessible transport signature of BLG's interlayer coupling and band structure, detectable in low-temperature conductance spectroscopy. The results are significant because they establish tunable BLG junctions as a model system for probing multiband effects and engineering transmission profiles for valleytronics and quantum transport applications.

### [Hybrid-2D Excitonic Metasurfaces for Complex Amplitude Modulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07619)
**arXiv:2604.07619 — Tom Hoekstra, Mark L. Brongersma, Jorik van de Groep**

This paper introduces a hybrid metasurface platform that integrates gate-tunable monolayer WS₂ into a nanophotonic resonator structure to achieve simultaneous, independent control of amplitude and phase of reflected visible light. Using an inverse-design pipeline, the authors numerically demonstrate that the strong, gate-tunable excitonic response of WS₂ allows the metasurface to sweep the full 0–2π phase range while independently modulating amplitude, something difficult to achieve with conventional electro-optic metasurfaces. Adding a second tunable monolayer decouples the amplitude and phase degrees of freedom, enabling programmable complex-amplitude wavefront shaping. As a proof of concept, they design a reconfigurable beam-steering metadevice operating in the visible. This platform represents a new route toward electrically programmable, 2D-material-based flat optics with applications in holography, LiDAR, and quantum optics.

### [Acoustically-driven magnons in CrSBr bilayers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12866)
**arXiv:2604.12866 — A. Shubnic, I. Chestnov, I. Lobanov, V. Uzdin, I. Iorsh, I. A. Shelykh**

CrSBr is an air-stable van der Waals magnetic semiconductor in which the interlayer antiferromagnetic exchange coupling is highly sensitive to interlayer distance and thus to strain. This work theoretically demonstrates that this strong magnetoelastic coupling allows acoustic phonon waves to resonantly drive magnon excitations in CrSBr bilayers when the acoustic frequency matches the antiferromagnetic resonance. The resonant generation efficiency and frequency can be tuned continuously with an external magnetic field, offering a new handle for magnonics in 2D systems. The authors derive the magnon-phonon coupling Hamiltonian from first principles and analyze the parametric excitation regime. This study establishes CrSBr as a promising platform for acoustic spintronics and magnon-based information processing in ultrathin van der Waals devices.

### [Microwave-to-optical transduction using magnon–exciton coupling in a layered antiferromagnet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03441)
**arXiv:2604.03441 — Pratap Chandra Adak et al.**

Efficient microwave-to-optical quantum transduction is a key challenge for hybrid quantum networks, where superconducting qubits (microwave frequency) must be linked to optical fiber channels. This work demonstrates a coherent transduction scheme based on the van der Waals antiferromagnet CrSBr, in which microwave signals drive the antiferromagnetic resonance (magnons), whose oscillation is imprinted on a reflected optical probe via strong magnon–exciton coupling near the material's excitonic resonances. The resulting optical sidebands at microwave offset frequencies are resonantly enhanced when the probe wavelength is tuned to excitonic transitions of CrSBr, yielding a significant enhancement in transduction efficiency. This mechanism exploits both the strong light–matter coupling of 2D excitonic materials and the microwave-accessible magnon modes of antiferromagnets, offering a compact and integrable platform. The results open a new route toward 2D-material-based quantum transducers that avoid the thermal noise and bandwidth limitations of conventional electro-optomechanical approaches.

### [Observation of Tunable Superradiant Frequency Combs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14601)
**arXiv:2604.14601 — (Kavli Institute preprint)**

This paper reports a dynamical phase transition in a driven rare-earth ion spin ensemble coupled to an on-chip superconducting microwave resonator, from continuous-wave superradiant emission to a periodic pulsed regime — effectively a superradiant frequency comb. The authors develop a driven-dissipative cavity-QED model explaining how coherence growth, disorder, and dissipation interplay to stabilize periodic pulsed superradiance as a collective, phase-locked spin dynamics. Crucially, the system simultaneously generates phase-synchronized, dual-rail superradiant frequency combs in both the microwave and optical domains, bridging the two spectral windows. This periodic superradiant state is identified as a continuous time crystal, establishing a connection between many-body synchronization and time-crystalline order. The results demonstrate rare-earth spin–cavity systems as a new platform for quantum-limited microwave and optical frequency references with applications in quantum metrology and networking.

### [Twisted Bilayer Graphene Lifetimes At Integer Fillings: An Analytic Result](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14303)
**arXiv:2604.14303 — Haoyu Hu, Yuelin Shao, Lorenzo Crippa, Dumitru Călugăru, Giorgio Sangiovanni, Tim Wehling, Leonid I. Glazman, B. Andrei Bernevig**

Magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene (TBG) near integer fillings hosts strongly correlated quasiparticles whose dispersion and linewidth are increasingly experimentally accessible via ARPES and STM. This work derives analytic results for the single-particle self-energy of these excitations using the topological heavy-fermion (THF) model, treating the f–c hybridization and Hund coupling perturbatively around an exactly solvable decoupled limit where free Dirac fermions coexist with interacting Hubbard-band f electrons. The analytic self-energy captures the quasiparticle renormalization, lifetime broadening, and the crossover between coherent and incoherent spectral weight at integer fillings. These results provide direct benchmarks for spectroscopic experiments and clarify how both band topology and strong correlations jointly shape quasiparticle dynamics in TBG. The approach complements numerical methods and yields interpretable formulas for the dependence of linewidths on filling and coupling parameters.
