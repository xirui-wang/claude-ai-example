## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [Visualizing the impact of quenched disorder on 2D electron Wigner solids](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10654-w)
*(2D Materials — Nature)*

The Wigner solid — a crystalline phase of electrons driven by strong Coulomb repulsion — has long been predicted in two dimensions but its microscopic structure in the presence of inevitable disorder has been inaccessible experimentally. This work uses gate-tunable bilayer MoSe₂ devices as an ultraclean 2D platform and combines atomically resolved STM with neural-quantum-state (NQS) quantum Monte Carlo simulations to image the evolution of the Wigner solid as a function of electron density and disorder strength. Two distinct disorder regimes are identified: a weak-disorder regime where the Wigner crystal retains long-range crystalline order punctuated by topological defects, and a strong-disorder regime where short-range order collapses into a glassy state. The combined experiment-theory approach establishes a quantitative framework for understanding the competition between electron–electron interactions and electron–disorder scattering in 2D correlated systems, with implications for quantum computing platforms based on electrostatically defined 2D electron systems.

### [Superconductivity from dual-surface carriers in rhombohedral graphene](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-026-03277-5)
*(2D Materials — Nature Physics)*

Rhombohedral graphene (RG) stacks possess low-energy states localized on opposite surfaces due to staggered layer potentials, making them a natural platform for surface-coupled correlated phenomena. This work reports superconductivity in octalayer RG and hBN-aligned heptalayer RG arising from a charge-delocalized semimetallic normal state where valence- and conduction-band Fermi pockets are split across opposite crystal surfaces. In octalayer graphene, five apparently distinct superconducting regions appear symmetrically for each sign of an applied displacement field — a topology of the phase diagram not seen in any prior moiré or rhombohedral graphene system. In the moiré-aligned heptalayer sample, two superconducting domes emerge from a single sharp resistive feature associated with the semimetal-to-semiconductor crossover. The dual-surface carrier origin — contrasting with the interaction-driven flat-band mechanism of moiré graphene — establishes a fundamentally new class of graphene superconductor potentially accessible to BCS-like pairing of spatially separated surface carriers.

### [Strong coupling of a microwave photon to an electron on helium](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-026-03342-z)
*(Microwave Engineering — Nature Physics)*

Electrons trapped on the surface of superfluid helium possess long-lived motional and spin states, making them attractive qubit candidates with predicted coherence times far exceeding those of solid-state spins. This work realizes the strong coupling regime between a microwave cavity photon and the lateral (in-plane) motional degree of freedom of a single electron on helium, using a device that integrates an electrostatically defined quantum dot with a planar superconducting coplanar waveguide resonator. A vacuum Rabi coupling strength of g/2π = 118 MHz is achieved, exceeding both the motional decoherence rate and the resonator photon loss rate by substantial margins, placing the system firmly in the resolved-sideband strong-coupling regime. This milestone establishes the electron-on-helium platform as a viable route for microwave quantum electrodynamics and long-coherence-time qubits compatible with cryogenic superconducting circuit infrastructure.

### [Excitonic spin torque in a magnetic semiconductor](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-026-02643-1)
*(2D Materials — Nature Materials)*

Optical control of magnetic order offers ultrafast alternatives to current-driven spintronic switching, but the microscopic mechanisms linking photoexcited carriers to magnetic dynamics in 2D materials have remained unclear. This work demonstrates that photoexcited excitons in the van der Waals layered antiferromagnetic semiconductor CrSBr exert a coherent spin torque with both damping-like and anti-damping-like components on the AFM order parameter, driving it along a non-trivial trajectory in the magnetic energy landscape. Time-resolved magneto-optical measurements confirm that a single ultrashort optical pulse causes deterministic bidirectional switching between antiferromagnetic configurations, with DFT calculations establishing the exciton-mediated exchange mechanism. By demonstrating that exciton density and helicity control the direction and magnitude of the torque, this work establishes excitons as a practical, all-optical control knob for spintronic memory, quantum transducers, and optospintronic logic devices based on 2D magnetic materials.

### [Scaling nanoribbon transistors with monolayer transition metal dichalcogenides](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-026-02161-w)
*(2D Materials — Nature Nanotechnology)*

Continued CMOS scaling beyond silicon requires semiconductor channel materials that retain high gate electrostatics and carrier mobility at sub-10 nm body thickness, a requirement naturally met by monolayer transition metal dichalcogenides. This work demonstrates monolayer TMD nanoribbon field-effect transistors fabricated using anchored metal contacts and multipatterning lithography with channel widths scaled to 25 nm, the narrowest yet demonstrated for 2D semiconductor nanoribbons at this performance level. The anchored contact scheme reduces contact resistance compared to conventional evaporated metal contacts by improving interface quality at the ribbon edges. The resulting transistors exhibit high on-state current density competitive with silicon nanosheets at equivalent width, demonstrating that 2D TMD nanoribbons can meet the performance targets for gate-all-around nanosheet architectures at future technology nodes and positioning monolayer TMDs as credible CMOS scaling candidates.

### [Coherent 2D–3D van der Waals perovskite epitaxial heterostructures](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41565-026-02201-5)
*(2D Materials — Nature Nanotechnology)*

Van der Waals epitaxy conventionally involves growth of layered 2D materials on other 2D or weakly bonded substrates, but cross-dimensional integration of 3D crystalline materials on 2D templates has remained challenging due to lattice and bonding mismatch. This work introduces coherent van der Waals cross-dimensional epitaxy where 3D methylammonium tin iodide (MASnI₃) perovskite single crystals nucleate and grow directly on 2D layered perovskite (3T)₂SnI₄ templates, forming atomically sharp and structurally coherent heterointerfaces confirmed by aberration-corrected TEM and ptychographic phase imaging. Remarkably, the 3D crystal growth tolerates topological defects (screws and edge dislocations) in the 2D template, producing spiral 3D crystals with intrinsic chirality inherited from the defect topology. These chiral spiral structures exhibit pronounced chiroptical responses, opening applications in chiral photonic devices, enantioselective sensing, and 3D/2D hybrid perovskite optoelectronics.

### [Dissipation due to Bulk Localized Low-Energy Modes in Strongly Disordered Superconductors](https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/923y-49z5)
*(Microwave Engineering — Phys. Rev. Lett.)*

Strongly disordered superconductors — including TiN, InOₓ, and granular Al — are widely deployed as kinetic inductance detectors, microwave resonators, and superconducting qubits, but suffer from poorly understood low-temperature microwave dissipation that limits device coherence. This work derives a microscopic mechanism: spatially inhomogeneous superconducting gap magnitude in disordered films gives rise to bulk-localized collective modes — distinct from conventional Bogoliubov quasiparticles — that absorb microwave photons efficiently at sub-gap frequencies. These modes arise from rare regions where the gap is suppressed below the average, creating local two-level-system-like resonances that couple to the electromagnetic field. The theory predicts a characteristic temperature and frequency dependence of dissipation that differs from standard quasiparticle or two-level-system models, providing a roadmap for suppressing these modes via disorder engineering, interface passivation, or frequency management to improve coherence in superconducting quantum circuits.
