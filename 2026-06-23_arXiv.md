## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Charge imprinting biases topology of correlated insulator in hBN-aligned rhombohedral multilayer graphene ([2606.20377](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20377))

This paper uses self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations to study correlated insulating phases in rhombohedral multilayer graphene (RMG) aligned with hexagonal boron nitride (hBN), a paradigmatic 2D van der Waals heterostructure. The authors systematically scan layer number, twist angle, displacement field, filling, and hBN alignment, finding that correlated insulators are most robust at small twist angles and intermediate layer numbers (N≃6), where bandwidth suppression from the moiré potential is balanced by the layer delocalization of the active conduction electrons. The central finding is the concept of "charge imprinting" under moiré-distant conditions at filling ν=1: even though conduction electrons reside far from the hBN interface, the hBN moiré potential shapes the valence-band charge texture near the interface, which then acts as a remote electrostatic template that imprints a charge texture onto the distant conduction electrons through long-range Coulomb interactions. This charge imprinting strongly biases the topological character of the correlated insulating state, determining whether it is a topologically trivial or quantum anomalous Hall insulator. The work establishes hBN alignment as a topological knob in RMG/hBN heterostructures, with immediate relevance to the engineered control of quantum anomalous Hall phases in moiré 2D materials.

### Anomalous magneto-optical response at RuO₂/WSe₂ van der Waals interface ([2606.20262](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20262))

This study probes weak interfacial magnetic states at the surface of (001)-oriented RuO₂ films—a controversial altermagnetic candidate material—using the magnetic proximity effect (MPE) in a van der Waals heterostructure formed by placing monolayer WSe₂ atop RuO₂. Temperature-dependent magneto-optical spectroscopy of the WSe₂ exciton resonance reveals an anomalous excitonic energy shift and a deviation from conventional Varshni (electron-phonon) behavior below 55 K that are absent in an encapsulated WSe₂ control sample; strikingly, the sign of this anomalous shift reverses upon field cooling in opposite magnetic field polarities, confirming its magnetic origin. Polarization-resolved measurements further reveal a nearly field-independent and fluctuating valley splitting in WSe₂/RuO₂, in contrast to the linear Zeeman splitting expected and observed in bare WSe₂, pointing to strongly fluctuating or disordered interfacial magnetic moments. This work demonstrates that monolayer WSe₂ acts as a sensitive excitonic magnetometer for the weak and debated surface magnetism of altermagnetic candidates, and contributes important evidence to the ongoing controversy about whether RuO₂ is a true altermagnet with a spin-split band structure. More broadly, it establishes polarization-resolved magneto-optical spectroscopy in 2D van der Waals heterostructures as a powerful tool for detecting subtle interfacial magnetism at the atomic scale.

### Proximity-induced unconventional superconductivity and chiral topological phases in twisted graphene/NbSe₂ van der Waals heterostructure ([2606.12104](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12104))

This theoretical work investigates the interplay between the moiré superlattice of twisted graphene and the conventional s-wave superconductor NbSe₂ in a van der Waals heterostructure, showing that the moiré modulation transforms proximity-induced pairing into an unconventional chiral topological superconductor phase. The twist angle controls the moiré wavelength and the low-energy flat-band structure of graphene, and when combined with the spin-orbit coupling inherited from NbSe₂ and proximity-induced s-wave pairing, the system enters a chiral topological superconducting phase characterized by a nonzero Chern number. This topological phase hosts chiral Majorana edge modes at sample boundaries, which are robust against time-reversal-breaking disorder and are of direct interest for topological quantum computing without external magnetic fields. The study maps out the phase diagram as a function of twist angle, interlayer coupling, and chemical potential, identifying parameter windows accessible in current experiments. This work highlights the power of moiré engineering in 2D heterostructures as a tool for synthesizing topological superconductors from entirely conventional building blocks.

### Monolithic hybrid quantum dot devices in superconducting twisted bilayer graphene ([2606.14685](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14685))

Gate-tunable superconductivity in magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene (MATBG) has enabled the realization of superconducting quantum devices within a single 2D crystal, without the need for external superconducting electrodes or multi-material assembly. This paper reports the monolithic fabrication of Josephson junctions and quantum dot devices entirely within MATBG, exploiting spatially localized gate-tunable regions to define correlated-electron quantum dots coupled directly to the intrinsic superconductor. The correlated nature of the MATBG superconductor—arising from the same moiré flat bands responsible for its strong-correlation physics—leads to unconventional coupling between quantum dot bound states and the superconducting condensate, providing a platform to study Yu-Shiba-Rusinov states and correlated Andreev bound states in a purely 2D setting. The monolithic approach eliminates interface resistances and fabrication complexity while preserving crystal coherence, representing a significant advance in quantum device engineering within moiré 2D materials. This platform has direct potential applications in topological qubit architectures based on moiré flat-band superconductors coupled to spin-polarized quantum dots.
