## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Chern number reversal and emergent superconductivity in rhombohedral graphene induced by in-plane magnetic fields — [2604.27788](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27788)

*(X. Zan et al., submitted April 30, 2026; cond-mat.mes-hall, cond-mat.str-el, cond-mat.supr-con)*

This experimental study investigates hBN-aligned eight-layer rhombohedral graphene moiré superlattices, a tunable 2D van der Waals platform that simultaneously hosts a robust quantum anomalous Hall (QAH) state and three unconventional superconducting phases. For electron-doped carriers away from the moiré potential, the team demonstrates QAH Chern number reversal driven by either displacement fields or in-plane magnetic fields, with an isotropic in-plane field response pointing to a joint role of orbital magnetism and spin-orbit coupling. For hole-doped carriers near the moiré superlattice, three superconducting phases exhibit strikingly different in-plane field responses: one is weakly enhanced, one strongly suppressed, and a third is exclusively induced by an in-plane magnetic field. The emergence of superconductivity under in-plane fields—where orbital pair-breaking is minimized—provides compelling evidence for spin-triplet Cooper pairing in this graphene system. These findings establish multilayer rhombohedral graphene as a rich platform for engineering correlated topological and unconventional superconducting phases purely through electrostatic and magnetic control, with direct implications for topological quantum computation.
