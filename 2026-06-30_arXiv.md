## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Moiré Phonons and Emergent Exciton-Phonon Coupling in a Moiré Heterobilayer ([2606.28185](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28185))
**Authors:** Can B. Uzundal, Woochang Kim, Zhiyuan Cui, Yuxuan Wei, Zheyu Lu, Qixin Feng, Francis L. Hong, Indrajit Maity, Takashi Taniguchi, Kenji Watanabe, Manish Jain, Mit H. Naik, Yoseob Yoon, Michael F. Crommie, Steven G. Louie, Feng Wang

Angle-aligned WS₂/WSe₂ moiré heterobilayers serve as a platform to investigate emergent lattice-excitonic coupling at the moiré wavelength scale. Using a broadband terahertz phonon transducer, the authors coherently launch moiré phonons that resonantly perturb moiré-trapped exciton states, revealing layer-selective coupling absent in the isolated monolayers. A driven oscillator model captures three distinct moiré phonon resonances, each with characteristic coupling strength reflecting the interlayer potential modulation at the moiré scale, and first-principles calculations confirm many such modes in the moiré unit cell. This work establishes THz phonon spectroscopy as a probe of emergent lattice-excitonic physics in van der Waals moiré systems and opens routes to phonon-mediated control of correlated exciton states.

### Superconducting cavity probes sliding ferroelectricity in small-angle twisted WSe₂ ([2606.22140](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22140))
**Authors:** Krishnendu Maji, Supriya Mandal, Sriram H., Rishiraj Rajkhowa, Malhar Date, Sayani Pal, Meghan P. Patankar, Kenji Watanabe, Takashi Taniguchi, Vibhor Singh, Mandar M. Deshmukh *(Nano Letters 2026)*

Sliding ferroelectricity in parallel-stacked WSe₂ bilayers is probed at high frequencies by embedding the van der Waals stack in a half-wave superconducting coplanar waveguide (CPW) cavity operating at GHz frequencies. Gate-dependent measurements reveal hysteresis in both the resonance frequency (related to capacitance) and the quality factor of the cavity, directly confirming ferroelectric polarization switching in the 2D system. Relaxation dynamics visible in the microwave response further reveal domain-wall motion kinetics during ferroelectric switching—information inaccessible to DC measurements. This approach establishes superconducting microwave circuits as powerful, non-invasive tools for characterizing 2D ferroelectric materials and tracking domain dynamics.

### High-Performance Nanophononic Resonators in Self-Suspended WSe₂ Domes and Drums ([2606.25946](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25946))
**Authors:** Jens-Christian Drawer et al.

Van der Waals WSe₂ membranes are patterned into self-suspended nano-dome and nano-drum geometries that serve as scalable nanoacoustic resonator platforms for the GHz–THz frequency range. The nano-dome geometry enables simultaneous generation of hundreds of high-quality resonators from a single lithographic step, exploiting the atomically flat interfaces of the 2D material to minimize phonon scattering losses. Self-supporting nano-drums achieve record-high resonance frequencies for 2D-semiconductor acoustic transducers, exceeding 1 THz, demonstrating van der Waals materials as practical components in the sub-THz to THz regime. These results position monolayer TMD resonators as candidates for nanoacoustic signal processing and phononic quantum transduction in hybrid quantum systems.

### Emergence of millimeter-wave resonances in self-assembled ferroelectric metamaterials ([2606.27616](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27616))
**Authors:** Florian Bergmann, Peter Meisenheimer, Aiden Ross, Marvin Schewe, Fernando Gómez-Ortiz, Kaiwen Yang, Xinyan Li, Thomas J. Lee, Lucas Caretta, Javier Junquera, Jason J. Gorman, Long-Qing Chen, Ramamoorthy Ramesh, Nathan D. et al.

Solid-state materials rarely exhibit intrinsic resonances in the millimeter-wave regime (30–300 GHz), but self-assembled periodic arrays of ferroelectric nanodomains form phononic metamaterials with designable resonant properties at these frequencies. Phase-field simulations of complex polar textures—such as flux-closure domains and polar vortices in PbZrTiO₃/SrTiO₃ superlattices—predict emergent "domain breathing modes," and state-of-the-art millimeter-wave characterization confirms resonance signatures up to hundreds of GHz. This bridges the gap between conventional GHz electronics and THz photonics, suggesting ferroelectric domain engineering as a platform for frequency-agile sub-THz metamaterials. The discovery has potential implications for next-generation 5G/6G millimeter-wave communication, programmable filters, and sensing systems operating in the W-band and beyond.

### Open Nanoacoustic Resonators Based on SrTiO₃/YBa₂Cu₃O₇₋ₓ Superlattices ([2606.27945](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27945))
**Authors:** (oxide superlattice / nanoacoustic group)

Oxide heterostructures of ferroelectric SrTiO₃ (STO) and high-temperature superconducting YBa₂Cu₃O₇₋ₓ (YBCO) are engineered as phononic distributed Bragg reflectors supporting sub-THz acoustic cavity modes. A metallic Ni transducer layer enables efficient ultrafast optical generation and detection of coherent phonons via time-domain Brillouin scattering, with transient reflectivity measurements revealing a well-defined cavity resonance in agreement with transfer-matrix acoustic modeling. This work demonstrates phonon confinement in a multifunctional oxide architecture combining ferroelectric and superconducting components in a single resonator platform. The simultaneous presence of superconducting and ferroelectric functionalities suggests opportunities for microwave-acoustic hybrid quantum systems at millikelvin temperatures where YBCO is in its superconducting state.

### Strong coupling between propagating spin wave and microwave photons in a superconducting resonator ([2606.27279](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27279))
**Authors:** (magnomechanics group)

A superconducting coplanar resonator fabricated directly on yttrium iron garnet (YIG) thin films grown on rare-earth-free Y₃Sc₂Ga₃O₁₂ substrates achieves strong magnon–photon coupling exceeding both the magnon linewidth and the photon linewidth—the hallmark of the strong coupling regime. Both Damon–Eshbach surface modes and backward-volume spin wave modes couple efficiently to the resonator's microwave photons, enabling full control of the spin-wave coupling geometry by varying field orientation. Nonreciprocal spin-wave radiation is observed in the Damon–Eshbach configuration, a feature relevant for directional microwave emission. This platform combining rare-earth-free YIG with superconducting resonators is attractive for quantum-magnonics applications including microwave-to-magnon quantum transducers.

### Confined exciton polaron in MoS₂ on twisted-hBN ([2606.27770](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27770))
**Authors:** Garima Gupta et al.

Spontaneous ferroelectric domain walls forming at the AA-stacked interface of twisted hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) generate spatially varying in-plane electric fields that simultaneously confine 2D excitons in MoS₂ monolayers and deplete the free charge carriers that normally bind to form trions. This dual electrostatic action creates a distinct "confined exciton polaron" quasiparticle at the domain wall without requiring any external voltage. The approach provides a gate-free, substrate-defined route to spatially address individual excitons in TMD monolayers, leveraging the self-organized ferroelectric texture of twisted hBN as a built-in electrostatic confinement template. These results establish twisted hBN as a versatile platform for engineering novel excitonic states in 2D semiconductors beyond conventional gate-defined devices.
