## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### On-chip superconducting GHz RF reflectometry of the capacitance response in bilayer graphene ([2606.14431](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14431))
An hBN-encapsulated bilayer graphene device is monolithically integrated with a Nb lumped-element LC resonator and driven near 4.25 GHz to perform radio-frequency reflectometry of the electronic compressibility. A perpendicular displacement field opens a tunable band gap in the dual-gated bilayer graphene, simultaneously modifying channel conductance and quantum capacitance; the gate-dependent resonance-frequency shift is converted to effective capacitance through an equivalent-circuit model. This approach directly probes the interplay of band-gap formation and electronic screening at GHz frequencies without invasive contacts, offering a high-sensitivity platform for studying correlated and topological phases in 2D materials via microwave spectroscopy. The experiment demonstrates that bilayer graphene is a viable host for on-chip microwave circuit quantum electrodynamics, with implications for graphene-based dispersive readout of quantum dot and spin-qubit architectures.

### Monolithic hybrid quantum dot devices in superconducting twisted bilayer graphene ([2606.14685](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14685))
Magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene (MATBG) is used as a monolithic platform where electrostatic gating simultaneously defines superconducting leads and quantum-dot confinement within a single crystal, eliminating the semiconductor-superconductor interfaces that typically introduce disorder in hybrid systems. Two device types are realized: a superconducting island exhibiting 2e-periodic Coulomb blockade (indicating a gap protected from quasiparticle poisoning) and a quantum dot proximitized by a MATBG superconducting lead. The moiré flat-band origin of superconductivity makes the pairing susceptible to electric-field tuning, enabling a reconfigurable, scalable hybrid architecture. This work opens a path toward topological qubit implementations and Andreev spectroscopy of strongly correlated, gate-tunable superconductors entirely within a 2D material.

### Engineering electrically-switchable quantum anomalous Hall states by spin-orbit coupling ([2606.13651](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13651))
Proximity coupling of twisted monolayer–bilayer graphene to WSe₂ introduces spin-valley-locking spin-orbit coupling that reshapes the competing magnetic ground states responsible for nonvolatile electrical switching of the quantum anomalous Hall (QAH) effect. By locking spin to valley quantum numbers, the proximity-induced SOC modifies the relative stability and coercive fields of the competing magnetization states, enabling deterministic gate-controlled switching of the QAH edge conductance without requiring magnetic field. The findings establish proximity-induced SOC as a new engineering lever for topological electronics, offering a pathway to low-power reconfigurable chiral-edge-state devices. The platform generalizes to other moiré graphene systems where competing magnetic orders compete at charge neutrality.

### Proximity-induced unconventional superconductivity and chiral topological phases in twisted graphene/NbSe₂ ([2606.12104](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12104))
The Bogoliubov–de Gennes formalism is applied to a graphene/NbSe₂ van der Waals heterostructure at a twist angle of 23.4°, where the reduced common symmetry (C₃) and proximity-induced spin-orbit coupling from NbSe₂ are extracted from ab initio calculations. The phase diagram is found to host chiral topological superconducting phases characterized by Chern numbers C ∈ {−4,−2,2,4}, with the twist-induced symmetry reduction stabilizing chiral pairing components that are absent in aligned heterostructures. The topological phases are expected to support chiral Majorana edge modes, making graphene/NbSe₂ a promising platform for topological quantum computing without the need for magnetic fields or proximity to magnetic materials. The twist angle thus provides an additional tuning parameter for engineering the topological gap and Chern number in 2D heterostructure superconductors.

### Possible high temperature superconductivity induced by coupling to proximate hyperbolic photon modes ([2606.16674](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16674))
When a metal is placed directly on hexagonal boron nitride (hBN), its conduction electrons couple to the hyperbolic phonon-polariton modes—electromagnetic modes confined to an unusually large volume in momentum space due to the sign-changing dielectric function of hBN. The longitudinal nature of these modes produces a coupling driven by time-dependent charge fluctuations that grows strongly with the energy difference between initial and final states, yielding a dimensionless coupling constant λ₀ that is identified as the key parameter controlling both Fermi velocity renormalization and possible superconducting instabilities. The authors find that within the hyperbolic frequency window, the zero-point electric-field fluctuations can reach ~MV/cm, comparable to those used in THz pump-probe experiments, suggesting that proximity to hBN could drive high-temperature superconductivity in 2D metals or few-layer films. A companion paper (arXiv:2606.16665) by Lee examines the related effect on metal-insulator transitions, suggesting a broad set of proximity-induced correlated-electron phenomena accessible via hBN heterostructures.
