## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [Electrically and Magnetically Tunable Charge-Density-Wave Transport in Quasi-2D h-BN/1T-TaS₂ Thin-Film Heterostructures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.27731)
**arXiv:2603.27731** | Jonas O. Brown, Maedeh Taheri, Nicholas R. Sesing, Tina T. Salguero, Alexander A. Balandin

This study investigates collective electronic transport in quasi-two-dimensional 1T-TaS₂ thin films encapsulated in hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN), using dual top- and bottom-gate device architectures. The authors demonstrate that perpendicular electric field gating produces a non-monotonic shift in the charge-density-wave (CDW) depinning threshold voltage — a behavior qualitatively distinct from quasi-1D CDW systems — attributed to competition between gate-induced carrier doping and domain-boundary pinning in the nearly commensurate (NC) CDW phase. Strikingly, an applied perpendicular magnetic field increases the depinning threshold and can drive the NC-to-incommensurate (IC) CDW phase transition at accessible field strengths. The ability to independently control both the CDW phase boundary and the collective transport threshold by electric and magnetic fields in a single 2D heterostructure device represents a significant advance for reconfigurable CDW-based logic and memory elements.

### [Oxygen as a dual function regulator in MoS₂ CVD synthesis: enhancing precursor evaporation while modulating reaction kinetics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28461)
**arXiv:2603.28461** | Keerthana S. Kumar, Akshay Singh et al.

Using a combination of ab initio molecular dynamics (AIMD), density functional theory (DFT), and computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulations, this work unravels the dual role of oxygen during chemical vapor deposition (O-CVD) of monolayer MoS₂, a prototypical 2D transition metal dichalcogenide. AIMD calculations show that oxygen promotes MoO₃ sublimation by forming volatile Mo₃O₉ clusters, increasing precursor supply to the growth substrate; simultaneously, DFT reveals that sulfur oxides (SOₓ) formed downstream sterically hinder the formation of reactive MoS₆ intermediate complexes, slowing nucleation kinetics. The interplay of these two effects means that maintaining a low S:O₂ ratio near the MoO₃ source during nucleation and a high S:O₂ ratio at the substrate during lateral growth is the key to producing large-area, high-quality monolayer films. Based on these mechanistic insights the authors construct a kinetic phase diagram for O-CVD that provides quantitative guidance for defect-controlled, scalable MoS₂ synthesis — directly applicable to optoelectronic and quantum technology device fabrication.

### [Magnetism and magnetoelastic effect in 2D van der Waals multiferroic CuCrP₂S₆](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25817)
**arXiv:2603.25817** | Jiasen Guo et al.

This work presents a comprehensive single-crystal neutron diffraction study of the van der Waals layered multiferroic CuCrP₂S₆, resolving a long-standing ambiguity about its magnetic ground state. The authors establish that the ordered Cr³⁺ moments align along the crystallographic b-axis in an A-type antiferromagnetic configuration, with a spin-flop transition occurring for fields applied along the same direction and a smooth rotation to a ferromagnetically polarized state for fields applied along a. Beyond the magnetic structure, the study uncovers a magnetoelastic coupling effect in which the interlayer spacing is directly coupled to the magnetic order parameter, identified through correlated changes in lattice parameters and magnetic Bragg intensities. These findings suggest that out-of-plane mechanical strain can serve as an effective external knob for tuning both the magnetic ordering temperature and spin configuration in CuCrP₂S₆ and related van der Waals magnets, with implications for strain-engineered multiferroic heterostructures and flexoelectric control of magnetoelectric coupling.

### [Geometric superfluid stiffness of Kekulé superconductivity in magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24766)
**arXiv:2603.24766** | Ke Wang, Qijin Chen, Rufus Boyack, K. Levin

Motivated by scanning tunneling spectroscopy reports of Kekulé bond-order modulations in magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene (MATBG), this theoretical study proposes that superconductivity in this system takes the form of an intra-valley finite-momentum pair-density wave (PDW) that intrinsically carries the Kekulé wavevector. The PDW pairing generates a Bogoliubov Fermi surface — yielding finite zero-bias conductance consistent with tunneling experiments — while simultaneously endowing the geometric superfluid stiffness with a characteristic T² low-temperature suppression arising from the same gapless quasiparticles. This work demonstrates that quantum geometry (Berry curvature contributions) is essential to the full superfluid response in the flat-band limit, adding a new dimension to the physics of moiré superconductors beyond conventional BCS pairing. The authors predict that density or displacement-field tuning of MATBG should produce correlated changes in tunneling conductance and superfluid stiffness, providing a clear and experimentally testable signature of PDW Kekulé order.

### [Magnetic flux distribution, quasiparticle spectroscopy, and quality factors in Nb films for superconducting qubits](https://arxiv.org/list/cond-mat.supr-con/recent)
**arXiv: (2026-03-31 submission)** | Multiple authors

This work combines three complementary microwave and nanoscale measurement techniques — scanning SQUID magnetometry for magnetic flux imaging, tunneling spectroscopy for subgap quasiparticle density-of-states, and cryogenic microwave resonator measurements for quality factor characterization — applied to Nb thin films used as the base material for superconducting qubits. The study maps the spatial distribution of trapped magnetic vortices as a function of field-cooling history and film thickness, correlating vortex density with measured increases in the microwave loss rate (reduced internal quality factors Q_i). Tunneling spectroscopy further identifies subgap Andreev-like states whose energy and spatial distribution track with vortex locations and grain boundary structure, suggesting that both vortex-bound states and grain-boundary proximitized regions are significant sources of quasiparticle-induced decoherence. The results provide direct nanoscale-to-microwave-performance correlations that can guide Nb film deposition optimization — including substrate temperature, sputtering pressure, and post-deposition annealing — for next-generation superconducting qubit circuits.
