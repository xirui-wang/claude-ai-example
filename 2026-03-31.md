## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [2603.25817] Magnetism and magnetoelastic effect in 2D van der Waals multiferroic CuCrP₂S₆
*Jiasen Guo et al. (using Spallation Neutron Source, Oak Ridge National Laboratory)*

CuCrP₂S₆ is a layered van der Waals material hosting both magnetic and electric orders, making it a prime candidate for low-dimensional magnetoelectric physics. Using single-crystal neutron diffraction, this study unambiguously establishes that the ordered Cr moments align along the *b* axis in an A-type antiferromagnetic configuration, resolving a longstanding controversy over the ground-state spin direction. Application of magnetic field along *b* produces a spin-flop transition, while fields along *a* smoothly rotate spins into a fully polarized ferromagnetic state. Crucially, the work reveals a magnetoelastic coupling that links the interlayer *c*-axis spacing directly to the spin canting angle, so that out-of-plane compressive or tensile strain can continuously tune both the ordering temperature and spin configuration. These findings provide a quantitative basis for exploiting strain-mediated control of magnetism and polarization simultaneously—via the flexoelectric effect or substrate-induced strain—in this and related 2D vdW multiferroics, with implications for strain-tunable magneto-optical and magneto-electric devices.

### [2603.25807] Imaging the Meissner effect and local superfluid stiffness in a graphene superconductor
*Ruoxi Zhang et al.*

Rhombohedral-stacked multilayer graphene has recently emerged as a highly tunable 2D platform for correlated superconductivity, but local probes of the pairing state have been scarce. This paper uses scanning magnetometry sensitive to nanotesla-scale fringe fields to directly image the static Meissner effect in a few-micron rhombohedral graphene flake, detecting a diamagnetic response that screens ~100 ppm of the applied field—small because the sample is extremely thin. By tracking the spatial evolution of vortex entry and exit, the authors map the local superfluid stiffness ρ_s with sub-micron resolution. The temperature dependence of ρ_s is found to be incompatible with conventional isotropic BCS theory, and the zero-temperature stiffness scales linearly with *T*c—a hallmark of unconventional or geometrically enhanced pairing. Furthermore, the experiment shows that superconductivity nucleates in the midst of a continuous quantum phase transition into a canted spin-ferromagnetic phase, directly linking the competing orders and providing new constraints for theoretical models of correlated 2D graphene superconductors.

### [2603.24766] Geometric superfluid stiffness of Kekulé superconductivity in magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene
*Ke Wang, Qijin Chen, Rufus Boyack, K. Levin*

Magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene (TBG) is a paradigmatic 2D correlated system in which flat bands lead to strongly enhanced interaction effects and a rich phase diagram including Kekulé-distorted superconducting states. This theoretical work computes the full superfluid stiffness tensor for Kekulé bond-ordered superconductivity in TBG, explicitly separating conventional kinetic contributions from quantum geometric (quantum metric) contributions arising from the non-trivial band topology of the moiré flat bands. The quantum geometric term is found to be the dominant contribution to the in-plane stiffness in the flat-band limit, analogous to results in other moiré systems but with the added complexity of Kekulé order breaking the three-fold rotational symmetry. These results predict an anisotropic superfluid response and set quantitative bounds on the minimum achievable *T*c as a function of carrier density and twist angle, providing directly testable predictions for microwave or THz conductivity measurements on TBG devices.

### [2603.24230] A material-agnostic platform to probe spin-phonon interactions using high-overtone bulk acoustic wave resonators
*Q. Greffe, A. Hugot, S. Zhang, Jeremie Viennot (corresponding) et al.*

High-overtone bulk acoustic wave resonators (HBARs) operating at gigahertz frequencies are powerful microwave-phonon transducers that can be coupled to spin systems for quantum sensing and quantum information applications. This work introduces a material-agnostic integration method in which thin-film lithium niobate transducers are visco-elastically transferred onto arbitrary crystal hosts, allowing the HBAR phonon modes to be brought into resonance with electron spin transitions (Larmor frequency tuned by a static magnetic field) at millikelvin temperatures. The spin-phonon coupling anisotropy and strength are extracted from acoustic dispersion and dissipation data without requiring any host-specific lithographic processing. Demonstrations in Er³⁺-doped CaWO₄ and Y₂SiO₅ achieve spin-phonon cooperativities up to 0.5, approaching the strong-coupling regime. This versatile platform accelerates the discovery of crystal hosts with enhanced spin-phonon coupling for microwave-to-optical quantum transduction and for the engineering of phonon-mediated qubit-qubit interactions in solid-state quantum processors.
