## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Strain-Enhanced Properties of the 2D CrSi₂N₄ Monolayer
The CrSi₂N₄ monolayer is a multifunctional 2D material combining transition metal and main-group elements within a MA₂Z₄ structure. This work performs comprehensive first-principles calculations showing that biaxial strain can simultaneously tune the hydrogen evolution reaction (HER) activity—by optimizing the H adsorption energy—alongside electrical conductivity, optical absorption, and thermoelectric figure-of-merit. The multifunctional nature of the monolayer makes it a candidate for integrated energy-harvesting and electrocatalytic platforms at the nanoscale. The results highlight how 2D semiconductors with strong spin-orbit elements (Cr) offer large degrees of freedom for strain-engineering compared to conventional TMDs.

### Interplay of Valley, Orbital, Spin, and Layer Degrees of Freedom in Ta₂CS₂ MXene
Ta₂CS₂ is a 2D MXene with an intrinsically low crystal symmetry, giving rise to a rich entanglement of valley, orbital, spin, and layer degrees of freedom in its band structure. This first-principles study maps out how the competing energy scales of spin-orbit coupling, inter-layer hopping, and valley splitting produce multiple topological and magnetic phases as a function of gating and strain. The strong SOC of the heavy Ta atoms leads to large spin Hall conductivities, suggesting Ta₂CS₂ as a promising platform for all-electrical spin manipulation. The work establishes MXenes as a family where these four quantum numbers can be simultaneously tuned, complementing TMD-based spintronics.

### Observation of a Mott Quantum Spin Hall Insulator in Twisted WSe₂ [2605.17335](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17335)
This experimental work reports the observation of a quantum spin Hall (QSH) phase that is stabilized by Mott correlations rather than by single-particle band topology in a 2.29° twisted WSe₂ device. At half-filling of the second moiré band, transport measurements reveal nearly quantized helical edge conductance (~e²/h) that is robust against perpendicular electric fields, out-of-plane magnetic fields up to several tesla, and temperatures below 5 K. This behavior, interpreted as a Mott-insulating bulk with topologically protected edges, demonstrates that strong electron-electron interactions in moiré systems can generate emergent topological protection distinct from band-topological mechanisms. The finding opens a new route to engineering correlated topological phases in 2D van der Waals moiré heterostructures.

### Reconfigurable Chiral Superconductivity in Rhombohedral Pentalayer Graphene [2605.13303](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13303)
Using a nanoscale SQUID-on-tip (SOT) magnetometer, this work images isospin-polarized ferromagnetic domains in rhombohedral pentalayer graphene and demonstrates that chiral superconductivity spontaneously breaks time-reversal symmetry in a manner that can be reconfigured between domains. The SOT provides sub-100 nm spatial resolution, revealing that the superconducting order parameter chirality tracks the underlying isospin domain walls. This spatial correlation between ferromagnetic and superconducting order demonstrates a rare coexistence phase and establishes domain-wall-bound chiral edge modes. The reconfigurability of the chiral ground state by electrostatic gating highlights rhombohedral few-layer graphene as a clean, gate-tunable platform for topological superconductivity research.
