## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Magnon polaritons in a van der Waals ferromagnet coupled to a superconducting resonator — [2605.12298](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12298)

*Álvaro Bermejillo-Seco, Luuk J. van der Goot, Matteo Arfini, Yaroslav M. Blanter, Gary A. Steele, Herre S. J. van der Zant — TU Delft — submitted 12 May 2026 — cond-mat.mes-hall*

This paper addresses a long-standing challenge in 2D materials and quantum microwave engineering: demonstrating strong magnon–photon coupling in an atomically thin van der Waals magnetic material. The authors couple exfoliated flakes of the 2D ferromagnet Cr₂Ge₂Te₆ (CGT)—with thicknesses ranging down to 30 nm—to a purpose-designed low-impedance superconducting coplanar resonator operating in the microwave regime. Magnon polaritons are evidenced by clear avoided crossings in the microwave transmission spectrum, reproduced consistently across six separate devices, ruling out sample-specific artefacts. The vacuum coupling strength is found to scale as the square root of the CGT flake thickness, in agreement with theory for volume-limited magnetic systems; extrapolation of this trend indicates that the strong-coupling regime is achievable even at the monolayer limit. The result establishes van der Waals ferromagnets as viable elements in hybrid quantum circuits and opens a route toward integrating 2D magnonic functionalities—including tunable magnon-based transducers and quantum memories—with superconducting qubit architectures.
