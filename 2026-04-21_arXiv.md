## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [Facet-dependent Chemical Kinetics Governed Growth of Twisted Graphene Layers with Pre-designed Angles](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15639)
*arXiv:2604.15639 — Chaowu Xue et al. — cond-mat.mtrl-sci*

Twisted graphene layers (TGLs) provide a versatile platform for studying flat-band correlated physics, but scalable synthesis with precise twist-angle control has remained elusive. This work identifies that different Pt grain facets exhibit different graphene nucleation activity, governed by the area ratio of exposed (110) facets during graphene-induced surface reconstruction. By rationally pre-selecting substrate Pt grains with the desired crystallographic orientation, the authors demonstrate CVD growth of TGLs at pre-designed twist angles, including the magic angle (≈1.1°), using complementary in situ characterization methods. The methodology extends beyond graphene and is proposed as a generalizable framework for controlling the stacking geometry of other foldable 2D materials via dynamic substrate reconstruction. This result bridges fundamental kinetics of Pt-catalyzed CVD with practical angle-engineering of moiré quantum materials.

### [Identification and Structural Characterization of Twisted Atomically Thin Bilayer Materials by Deep Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15960)
*arXiv:2604.15960 — Haitao Yang et al. — cond-mat.mtrl-sci*

Rapid and accurate determination of twist angle in bilayer 2D materials is a bottleneck for scalable moiré-material research. This paper trains two convolutional neural network models on optical micrograph data: one for MoS₂ flake thickness segmentation and one for twist-angle prediction using a synthetic dataset of >10,000 images spanning hexagonal-to-triangular geometries. Deep learning predictions are validated experimentally by second-harmonic generation (SHG) and Raman spectroscopy, achieving reliable angle determination without time-consuming microscopy techniques. The approach is system-agnostic and generalizable to other 2D material bilayer systems, offering a practical high-throughput tool for twist-angle engineering. This work lowers a key experimental barrier for scalable production of moiré quantum devices.

### [New Frontiers in Quantum Science and Technology Using van der Waals Josephson Junctions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15276)
*arXiv:2604.15276 — Joydip Sarkar, Ayshi Mukherjee, Amit Basu, Ritajit Kundu, Arijit Kundu, Mandar M. Deshmukh — cond-mat.mes-hall*

Van der Waals 2D materials have enabled a new generation of Josephson junctions (JJs) with fundamentally richer physics than conventional oxide-barrier devices. This review surveys how vdW JJs provide direct control over device symmetry, enabling realization of Hamiltonians with broken time-reversal or inversion symmetry—including magnetic, topological, and spin-orbit-coupled weak links—that are inaccessible in epitaxial tunnel junctions. Key results include the observation of spin-triplet supercurrents in magnetic vdW barriers, gate-tunable 0-to-π transitions, and signatures of Andreev bound states in topological edge channels. The authors highlight open questions at the intersection of vdW heterostructure engineering and quantum information, including scalable qubit integration and microwave circuit coupling. This is a timely roadmap for the field as vdW JJ fabrication matures.

### [Sub-nm Range Momentum-Dependent Exciton Transfer from a 2D Semiconductor to Graphene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13445)
*arXiv:2604.13445 — (multiple authors) — cond-mat.mes-hall*

Understanding how excitons in 2D semiconductors decay into adjacent graphene is central to designing TMD/graphene optoelectronic devices. Using time-resolved PL spectroscopy with ~1 ps resolution on MoSe₂/few-layer graphene vdW heterostructures, the authors measure a universal ≈2.5 ps exciton transfer time at cryogenic temperature that is independent of graphene layer count. Crucially, inserting an hBN spacer of just 1 nm thickness completely suppresses the transfer, identifying charge tunneling—not Förster dipolar coupling—as the dominant mechanism for bright excitons with near-zero center-of-mass momentum. Finite-momentum "hot" excitons may additionally relax through Förster-type channels, but these do not significantly contribute for the bright exciton population. These results quantify the ultra-short-range nature of the coupling and set design rules for hBN-spacer thickness in TMD/graphene photodetectors and light emitters.

### [Digital Predistortion for Flux Control of Tunable Superconducting Qubits](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15895)
*arXiv:2604.15895 — Dharun Venkateswaran, Felice Francesco Tafuri, Yuanzheng Paul Tan, Bruno Aznar Martinez et al. — cond-mat.supr-con / quant-ph (submitted for EuMW 2026)*

Flux-tunable superconducting qubits require fast, distortion-free flux pulses to implement high-fidelity two-qubit entangling gates, but non-ideal control electronics and cryogenic parasitic impedances degrade gate fidelity. This paper introduces a digital predistortion (DPD) framework combining infinite impulse response (IIR) and finite impulse response (FIR) filters to characterize and compensate distortions on the flux-control lines of a QPU. Experiments on a flux-tunable QPU demonstrate that the corrected step-response deviates by less than 1% from the ideal target, a significant improvement over the uncorrected case. The method enables automated rapid calibration of flux-control channels and is scalable to multi-qubit processors. Given its explicit targeting of the EuMW 2026 (European Microwave Week) conference, this work directly bridges superconducting qubit control and microwave engineering methodology.
