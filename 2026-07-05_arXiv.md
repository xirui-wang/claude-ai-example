## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Many-body benchmarking of DFT local-registry energetics in bilayer InSe
[2607.01682](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01682) — Jeonghwan Ahn, Abdulgani Annaberdiyev, Jovan Nelson, Nathaniel P. Stern, Hyeondeok Shin — *cond-mat.mtrl-sci*

Bilayer indium selenide (InSe) is an emerging 2D van der Waals semiconductor of growing interest for moiré and twistronics physics, where accurate modeling of the interlayer registry energy landscape is essential. This paper provides a rigorous benchmark of DFT stacking energetics against diffusion quantum Monte Carlo (DMC), one of the most accurate ab initio many-body methods. While DFT predicts near-degeneracy (within 1.5 meV/f.u.) among the AB, AAr, and ABr stacking configurations that share the same interfacial Se–Se registry, DMC resolves these stackings by 8(5) and 41(4) meV/f.u., respectively, with the overall registry-energy range reaching 60(7) meV/f.u. These large, DFT-invisible energy differences directly control the amplitude and shape of the moiré potential in twisted bilayer InSe, implying that DFT-based continuum models of correlated phases in this system may be significantly miscalibrated. This work establishes DMC benchmarking as a necessary validation step for DFT registry-energy models used in 2D moiré systems and provides quantitative targets for improved exchange-correlation functionals.

### Transition-Metal Tailored Ga₂O₂ Monolayer: From Room-Temperature Gas Sensing to Chemical Scavenging
[2607.02012](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02012) — Afreen Anamul Haque, Aniket Singha — *cond-mat.mtrl-sci*

Gallium oxide in its two-dimensional Ga₂O₂ monolayer form is a wide-bandgap semiconductor with potential for chemical sensing applications, but the pristine monolayer suffers from poor sensitivity and weak molecular binding. Using first-principles DFT calculations (VASP, PBE functional), this study systematically screens seven transition-metal substitutional dopants (Pd, Zn, Zr, Mo, Ag, Ti, Pt) for their ability to activate the Ga₂O₂ monolayer toward detection and capture of eleven toxic gases — spanning eight industrial chemicals and three environmental pollutants. All TM-substituted variants exhibit negative formation and binding energies with negligible lattice distortion, and their structural stability at room temperature is confirmed by ab initio molecular dynamics. The performance landscape spans from selective, regenerable sensors ideal for real-time monitoring to strongly chemisorbing scavengers suited for permanent detoxification. This work provides a systematic design map for engineering TM-decorated Ga₂O₂ monolayers as next-generation 2D gas-sensing and environmental remediation nanomaterials, with direct relevance to wearable sensor and air-quality applications.

### Confinement in a magnetically induced WSe₂ quantum dot
[2607.01192](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01192) — Rachid El Aitouni, Mohammed El Azar, Clarence Cortes, David Laroze, Ahmed Jellal — *cond-mat.mes-hall*

Tungsten diselenide (WSe₂) is a prominent transition metal dichalcogenide (TMD) with strong intrinsic spin-orbit coupling and valley-contrasting Berry curvature, making it a leading 2D platform for spin-valley quantum devices. This work theoretically investigates quantum confinement of massive Dirac fermions in WSe₂ using not electrostatic gates but a spatially localized magnetic field, solving the massive Dirac equation in the presence of the inhomogeneous field profile. The localized magnetic potential creates effective quantum confinement with discrete energy levels whose spacing and degeneracy patterns are valley-dependent, reflecting the K and K' valley asymmetry inherent to the WSe₂ band structure. The tunability of level spacing and dot effective size through the magnetic field amplitude and spatial extent offers an alternative route to defining quantum dot qubits in TMDs that avoids the challenges of edge-induced disorder from lithographic confinement. These results advance the understanding of magnetic confinement as a tool for valley-polarized quantum information processing in 2D TMD systems.

### Disorder-induced superconductivity in graphene
[2607.02267](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02267) — Jannes van Poppelen, Tomas Löthman, Annica M. Black-Schaffer — *cond-mat.supr-con*

Monolayer graphene is a canonical 2D Dirac semimetal in which the vanishing density of states at charge neutrality makes superconductivity exceedingly difficult to achieve through conventional mechanisms, typically requiring either strong electron-electron interactions or proximity to a superconducting substrate. This work demonstrates a counterintuitive route: superconductivity emerging entirely from disorder in the form of low concentrations of lattice vacancies or chemisorbed hydrogen atoms, without requiring strong correlations. The defects generate a large density of quasi-localized zero-energy states (an "impurity band") at the Dirac point that dramatically enhances the effective pairing susceptibility, enabling Cooper pairing at interaction strengths far below the pristine-graphene threshold. This mechanism constitutes a counterexample to Anderson's theorem in a 2D Dirac system and establishes controlled defect engineering as a viable alternative to magic-angle twist for inducing superconductivity in graphene. The findings have direct implications for graphene-based Josephson junctions and superconducting quantum devices where vacancy or hydrogenation control is experimentally accessible.
