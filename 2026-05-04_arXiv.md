## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [Coexistence of d-Wave Altermagnetism and Topological States in Janus FeSeX (X = S, Te) Monolayers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26239)
**arXiv:2604.26239** | Alvaro Gonzalez-Garcia et al.

Janus monolayers — single atomic layers with distinct top and bottom faces — break mirror and inversion symmetry without requiring spin-orbit coupling, and this work demonstrates that Janus FeSeX (X = S, Te) monolayers exploit that structural asymmetry to realise d-wave altermagnetism: a collinear magnetic state in which the spin degeneracy is lifted in momentum space despite vanishing net magnetisation. First-principles calculations show that the anisotropic superexchange between Fe sites, amplified by the broken symmetries of the Janus geometry, generates large (~100 meV) momentum-dependent spin splittings characteristic of a d-wave altermagnetic nodal pattern, and that these splittings can be continuously tuned by applying in-plane biaxial strain, offering an electrically or mechanically accessible control knob. When spin-orbit coupling is included, a topological band gap opens at the Fermi level accompanied by quantised spin Hall conductivity plateaus, demonstrating that both altermagnetic and topological-insulating orders coexist within the same monolayer. These results establish the Janus FeSeX family as a rare two-dimensional platform combining non-relativistic spin splitting with quantum spin Hall physics, with direct implications for ultra-thin spintronic devices and 2D topological transistors.

### [Topological phase transitions in twisted bilayer graphene/hBN from interlayer coupling and substrate potentials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27304)
**arXiv:2604.27304** | Huiwen Wang, Wei Jiang

Twisted bilayer graphene (TBG) aligned with hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) realises some of the richest correlated and topological physics known in a two-dimensional platform, including fractional Chern insulators, but a systematic understanding of how the two principal symmetry-breaking parameters — interlayer hopping asymmetry and the hBN-induced staggered potential — jointly control the Chern topology of the moiré flat bands has been lacking. This work presents a comprehensive continuum-model phase diagram that varies both parameters with and without the hBN moiré potential, identifying and classifying every topological phase transition by the mechanism through which the gap closes: band inversions at generic C₃-symmetric k-points, at high-symmetry momenta, or via parabolic band touchings. The Berry curvature distribution is tracked throughout, providing a clear physical picture of how spectral weight redistributes across transitions and of which parameter regimes support large, nearly uniform Berry curvature ideal for hosting fractional Chern insulators. The study clarifies which fabrication-accessible parameters are most critical for engineering desired Chern numbers in TBG/hBN, providing a practical design guide for experiments targeting topological flat bands with high Chern number or topological phase boundaries.

### [Chern number reversal and emergent superconductivity in rhombohedral graphene induced by in-plane magnetic fields](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27788)
**arXiv:2604.27788** | Xiaozhou Zan et al. (13 authors)

Rhombohedral-stacked multilayer graphene aligned with hexagonal boron nitride provides a uniquely tunable moiré platform in which the interplay of topology and superconductivity can be explored without magnetic field breaking time-reversal symmetry, and this experiment studies the eight-layer case — the thickest rhombohedral graphene superlattice yet realised. For electron-doped carriers away from the moiré potential, transport measurements reveal that the quantum anomalous Hall (QAH) Chern number can be reversed by either displacement field or in-plane magnetic field, with the isotropic in-plane response implicating a cooperative role of orbital magnetism and spin-orbit coupling that distinguishes this system from conventional magnetic topological insulators. In the hole-doped regime near the moiré superlattice, three distinct superconducting phases are identified by their contrasting in-plane magnetic field dependencies: one is weakly enhanced, the second is strongly suppressed, and a third appears only under an applied in-plane field — the last being compelling evidence for spin-triplet pairing and representing the first field-induced (rather than field-suppressed) superconducting phase in a graphene moiré system. These findings reveal an eight-layer rhombohedral graphene/hBN superlattice as an exceptionally rich testbed for correlated topological states, advancing the understanding of unconventional pairing mechanisms and Chern-number engineering in two-dimensional materials.

### [Strong coupling between quantized magnon modes in a YIG microstructure and microwaves in a superconducting resonator](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28145)
**arXiv:2604.28145** | Seth W. Kurfman, Philipp Geyer, Anoop Kamalasanan, Karl Heimrich, Kwangyul Hu, Paul Tharnier, Frank Heyroth, Michael Flatté, Georg Schmidt

Yttrium iron garnet (YIG) is the gold-standard material for low-loss magnonics, yet realising strong magnon–photon coupling in sub-10 μm YIG structures — a prerequisite for on-chip quantum hybrid circuits — has remained elusive because thin-film growth and standard microfabrication cannot simultaneously achieve the film thickness and lateral dimensions needed for a sufficiently large effective magnonic mode volume. This work overcomes the obstacle by carving a YIG microplatelet directly from a bulk single crystal via focused-ion-beam (FIB) milling and placing it on the constricted inductive line of an optimised superconducting lumped-element LC resonator, a geometry that concentrates the microwave magnetic field into the platelet volume and maximises coupling. Strong coupling between multiple quantised magnon modes and the LC resonator photons is demonstrated through the characteristic level-repulsion anti-crossing in the transmission spectrum, with coupling rates commensurate with the individual linewidths of both the magnon and photon modes. This result bridges on-chip superconducting circuit quantum electrodynamics with nano-scale magnonics, opening a route to microwave-frequency quantum transducers, magnon-qubit interfaces, and multi-mode hybrid quantum systems all integrated on a single chip.
