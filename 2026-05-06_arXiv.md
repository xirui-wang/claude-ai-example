## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Interplay of Valley, Orbital, Spin, and Layer Degrees of Freedom in Ta₂CS₂ MXene — [2605.01271](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01271)

Ta₂CS₂ is a two-dimensional MXene that can be obtained by exfoliating the bulk MAX-phase precursor Ta₂AlC. This paper by Kunal Dutta, Anupam Mondal, Sayantika Bhowal, Subhradip Ghosh, and Indra Dasgupta investigates from first principles how the valley, orbital, spin, and layer quantum numbers interplay in this 2D system. The authors find that the combination of broken inversion symmetry and strong spin–orbit coupling in Ta₂CS₂ leads to valley-dependent orbital-layer coupling and layer-dependent spin splitting of the electronic bands. Crucially, the intrinsic electric polarization acts as a tuning knob: by switching the polarization direction, valley-dependent orbital moments and Zeeman-like spin splittings can be reversed, providing electrically controllable spin and orbital degrees of freedom. As a practical consequence, the nontrivial orbital and spin textures give rise to substantial orbital Hall and spin Hall conductivities, positioning Ta₂CS₂ as a versatile platform for valleytronic and orbitronic device concepts.

### Signatures of Time-Reversal-Symmetry Breaking in Multiband 2H-TaS₂ Revealed by Zero-Field Josephson Nonreciprocity — [2605.00477](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00477)

2H-TaS₂ is a layered van der Waals transition-metal dichalcogenide that is intrinsically superconducting below ~0.8 K, making it an attractive platform for exploring 2D unconventional superconductivity. In this study, the authors fabricate 2H-TaS₂/2H-NbSe₂ van der Waals Josephson junctions and measure a clear zero-field Josephson diode effect: the critical current is larger in one direction than the other even without a net external magnetic field or broken spatial inversion. The diode efficiency shows no systematic correlation with junction transparency or TaS₂ thickness, ruling out purely extrinsic origins. Theoretical modeling within a Josephson framework shows that interband scattering in the multiband superconductor acts as a phase-locking mechanism that generates an anomalous phase difference and a nonsinusoidal current-phase relation, producing the observed rectification. Taken together with complementary nonlinear Hall transport measurements, the results provide strong evidence that 2H-TaS₂ spontaneously breaks time-reversal symmetry—a rare property with implications for topological and chiral superconductivity in van der Waals materials.
