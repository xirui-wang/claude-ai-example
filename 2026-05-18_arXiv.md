## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [2605.13303] Reconfigurable Chiral Superconductivity (Rhombohedral Pentalayer Graphene)
*(nanoscale SQUID-on-tip group)*

This paper establishes chiral superconductivity (CSC) in rhombohedral pentalayer graphene using spatially resolved thermodynamic imaging, providing direct evidence of spontaneous time-reversal symmetry breaking in a van der Waals material. Using SQUID-on-tip magnetometry at dilution-refrigerator temperatures, the authors image isospin-polarised domains whose chirality can be switched deterministically with currents far below the pair-breaking threshold — a previously inaccessible regime for controlling topological superconducting states. The ability to reversibly toggle between opposite-chirality domains using ultra-low currents makes this system a compelling candidate for fault-tolerant quantum memory based on chiral superconducting order. Rhombohedral pentalayer graphene joins a growing class of moiré and non-moiré 2D correlated superconductors where the interplay of flat bands, strong correlations, and Berry curvature produces rich topological phases. This work demonstrates that the chirality degree of freedom can be treated as a classical bit, opening a path toward non-volatile, gate-tunable topological memory elements.

### [2605.14436] Periodic Behavior of Topology in Graphene with Nanohole Array
*Yong-Cheng Jiang, Xing-Xiang Wang, Xiao Hu*

This paper provides an elegant analytical route to designing topological electronic states in graphene by nanostructuring. The authors derive that for graphene superlattices with triangular or honeycomb arrays of nanoholes respecting C₆v symmetry and described by an m√3 × m√3 supercell index m, the band topology alternates with a period of two (triangular arrays) or six (honeycomb arrays) in m. The topological character is diagnosed through Wyckoff positions of Wannier centres and parity indices at high-symmetry Brillouin-zone points, methods accessible from standard DFT calculations. This work is significant because it converts the problem of engineering 2D topological insulators based on graphene into a simple integer classification, circumventing the need for heavy element substitution or proximity-induced spin-orbit coupling. The results offer a direct material-design guide for graphene nanohole-array systems fabricatable with existing lithography and STM lithography techniques.

### [2605.11183] Symmetry Guided Band-Gap Opening via Periodic Topological Defects in Graphene
*D. N. Garzon, Leonel Cabrera-Loor, Jacopo Gliozzi, Marco Fronzi, Catherine Stampfl, Henry P. Pinto*

Graphene's gaplessness presents a major obstacle to its use as a transistor channel; this work demonstrates that periodic arrays of topological defects can open a controllable band gap through symmetry-governed Brillouin-zone folding. Using DFT supported by tight-binding models, the authors study graphene superlattices containing Stone-Wales and flower-like defects over N×N periodicities: band-gap opening occurs exclusively when N is a multiple of three, because this condition folds the K and K′ Dirac points to the same momentum in the reduced zone, enabling hybridisation. Flower-like defect superlattices produce particularly large and tuneable gaps whose magnitudes decrease systematically as defect separation increases toward the dilute limit. This symmetry principle is general and provides a predictive design rule — valid across defect types — for opening and tuning band gaps in graphene derivatives without breaking time-reversal symmetry or introducing foreign atoms.

### [2605.14214] Spin Hall Effect in Electronic Lévy Glasses: Enhanced Spin Current in the Superdiffusive Regime
*Diego B. Fonseca (Univ. Federal de Pernambuco) et al.*

This study exploits the anomalous transport properties of graphene-ribbon Lévy glasses — systems with randomly distributed heavy-SOC regions producing scale-free path-length distributions — to dramatically enhance the spin Hall effect. Landauer-Büttiker simulations with tight-binding models show that in the superdiffusive transport regime the spin Hall angle reaches approximately 30%, compared with only ~5% in the conventional diffusive regime, while the resistivity remains low and the spin diffusion length stays long. The simultaneous achievement of large spin Hall angle, low resistivity, and long diffusion length breaks a typical trade-off that plagues conventional spin Hall materials. These results suggest that engineering the randomness statistics of heavy-SOC inclusions in graphene is a viable strategy for producing high-efficiency spin-current generators for 2D spintronic applications.

### [2605.04402] Imaging GHz Surface Acoustic Wave Modes in Electrostricted LaAlO₃/SrTiO₃ Heterostructures
*Ranjani Ramachandran, Sayanwita Biswas et al.*

This paper demonstrates room-temperature generation and sub-micron imaging of GHz-frequency surface acoustic waves in LaAlO₃/SrTiO₃, a canonical oxide heterointerface that lacks intrinsic piezoelectricity but supports strong electrostriction. Using atomic acoustic force microscopy (AAFM), the authors image SAW waveforms with better than 1 μm spatial resolution for modes up to 2.2 GHz, finding a shear-horizontal-type character and very low propagation loss of order 10⁻³ dB per wavelength. The electrostriction mechanism — rather than conventional piezoelectricity — is key to the SAW generation, opening a pathway to exploit the rich oxide-interface physics (2DEG conductivity, ferroelectric proximity, Mott transitions) for acoustoelectric and acousto-quantum device integration. These results are directly relevant to the growing field of on-chip microwave-frequency acoustic transducers for quantum information applications, where low-loss shear modes can selectively couple to in-plane spin and orbital degrees of freedom.

### [2604.06959] Microscopic Evidence of Spin-Driven Multiferroicity and Topological Spin Textures in Monolayer NiI₂
*Wang et al. — Phys. Rev. Lett. 136, 026402 (2026)*

This study presents atomically resolved, real-space evidence of spin-driven multiferroicity in a monolayer 2D magnet, NiI₂, using vectorial spin-polarised scanning tunnelling microscopy. The measurements reveal a canted spin-spiral ground state with a fully determined spin rotation plane, accompanied by a 2Q charge modulation arising from the magnetoelectric coupling. Most strikingly, at domain walls of the spin spiral the authors directly image topological spin textures composed of meron–antimeron pairs, which carry half-integer topological charges and are associated with distinct charge modulation patterns and band shifts — microscopically confirming local bound charges induced by ferroelectric polarisation variations at domain walls. The experimental observations are well captured by a realistic spin model incorporating bond-directional Kitaev interactions and the generalised spin-current model of type-II multiferroicity, establishing monolayer NiI₂ as a platform for electric-field control of topological spin textures with low energy dissipation.

### [2605.07336] Breaking the Trade-off: Bulk 2D Ising Superconductivity with High Tc and Giant Interlayer Spacing via Chain Intercalation in (BaS)₁/₃TaS₂
*Ziyi Zhu et al.*

Conventional strategies for enhancing 2D Ising superconductivity through chemical intercalation face a fundamental trade-off: increasing interlayer spacing to decouple TaS₂ layers also reduces the superconducting Tc. This paper reports a new polymorph (BaS)₁/₃TaS₂ in which an unusual Ba-S-S-Ba chain structure is inserted between TaS₂ bilayers, achieving an inter-bilayer spacing of 12.75 Å — more than three times that of pristine 2H-TaS₂ — while simultaneously maintaining a high Tc. The chain intercalation geometry uniquely breaks bulk c-axis mirror symmetry, ensuring that Ising spin-orbit coupling is preserved for each TaS₂ monolayer and providing robust upper-critical-field enhancement beyond the Pauli limit. This result demonstrates a materials-design route to 2D superconductors that are simultaneously well-decoupled, symmetry-protected, and high-Tc, with immediate implications for device applications where ultrathin, gate-tunable Ising superconductors are needed.
