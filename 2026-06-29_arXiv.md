## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [2606.26771] Controlled chemical vapor deposition for synthesis of emerging Mo(W)Te₂ systems
This paper addresses a critical challenge in 2D materials research: the structural polymorphism and chemical instability of Group-VI transition metal ditellurides (MoTe₂, WTe₂) that complicate preparation of high-quality samples and heterointerfaces. The authors introduce a confined-space CVD strategy that lowers the effective growth temperature window, enabling deterministic synthesis of single crystals, alloys, and lateral/vertical heterostructures. Tailored precursor configurations and stepwise thermal ramps allow precise phase and composition control. High-resolution aberration-corrected STEM characterization reveals nearly atomically sharp, compositionally well-defined seamless boundaries at Mo(W)Te₂ lateral heterostructure interfaces — an unprecedented structural quality. This platform provides an in-situ experimental system that avoids the thickness nonuniformity and structural damage associated with mechanical exfoliation, opening new avenues for studying correlated and topological phenomena in clean van der Waals TMD devices.

### [2606.15818] Intrinsic linewidths of confined phonons in few-layer hBN
Hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) is a cornerstone 2D material used as an encapsulant in van der Waals heterostructures, yet the intrinsic phonon dynamics in few-layer hBN have remained poorly characterized. This study employs helium-3 spin-echo spectroscopy — a technique capable of resolving extremely narrow spectral features — to directly measure the intrinsic linewidths of low-energy, optically silent phonon modes at the surface of few-layer hBN. The authors simultaneously observe multiple surface-confined interlayer shear modes whose energies match linear-chain model predictions. Crucially, they find a near order-of-magnitude increase in phonon linewidth (shorter lifetimes) between four-layer and two-layer modes, demonstrating strong confinement-induced reduction in phonon lifetimes. These results are essential for understanding thermal transport and energy dissipation in hBN-encapsulated devices and for engineering phonon-based functionalities in 2D material stacks.

### [2606.27013] Spin-orbit coupling driven topological superconductivity in twisted bilayer graphene–WSe₂ heterostructures
This theoretical paper investigates how proximity-induced spin-orbit coupling (SOC) in a twisted bilayer graphene (tBLG)/WSe₂ heterostructure can drive a topological superconducting phase. Starting from the Bistritzer-MacDonald continuum model of tBLG, the authors incorporate Ising, Rashba, and intrinsic SOC terms and construct the corresponding Bogoliubov-de Gennes Hamiltonian with conventional s-wave pairing. The emergent topological superconducting phases bear an effective p-wave pairing character and exhibit inverted band dispersion protected by a bulk gap, characterized by nontrivial Chern numbers. Varying the twist angle and individual SOC strengths maps out extended phase diagrams of gapless, trivial, and topological phases, with phase boundaries coinciding with closings of direct band gaps. The work provides concrete guidance for experimental realization in tBLG/Nb/WSe₂ heterostructures, where both superconducting proximity and tunable SOC are accessible.

### [2606.27102] Low-energy model for doped graphene nanoribbons
Doped graphene nanoribbons (GNRs) represent a key platform for nanoelectronics and spintronics, but their many-body behavior when the chemical potential lies inside the bulk bands has remained poorly understood. This paper constructs an exact mapping from extended and on-site Hubbard models of free-standing GNRs to a Kanamori Hamiltonian that naturally incorporates ferromagnetic exchange and pair-hopping interactions. The Coulomb matrix elements entering the Kanamori model are determined analytically, and their scaling with ribbon width and length is identified. A low-energy version of the Kanamori Hamiltonian is proposed to describe the GNR response to external fields relevant for nanoelectronics transport channels. The model predicts open-shell, high-spin many-body ground states leading to shell- and spin-blockade phenomena — effects directly relevant to spin-based quantum information processing with molecular junctions.

### [2606.20904] Scanning-probe quantum sensing of microwave and static magnetic field response of an on-chip superconducting resonator
This paper introduces a scanning-probe quantum microscopy approach to characterize the nanoscale electromagnetic environment of a niobium (Nb) on-chip microwave superconducting resonator — a device class central to superconducting quantum computing and microwave quantum sensing. The key innovation is using a quantum spin sensor mounted on the scanning probe: by performing Rabi oscillation measurements, the authors achieve coherent microwave control via the resonator's fringe fields, providing local maps of the microwave magnetic field generated by the resonator mode. Beyond microwave imaging, the technique also visualizes static field response, revealing the formation, evolution, and field-induced depinning of superconducting vortices in the Nb film. This multi-modal approach offers a direct route to diagnosing loss mechanisms in superconducting microwave circuits — including vortex-induced dissipation — at the relevant microscopic scales, with immediate implications for improving qubit coherence and resonator quality factors in quantum hardware.
