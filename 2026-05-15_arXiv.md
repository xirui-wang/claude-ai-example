## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [2605.12298] Magnon polaritons in a van der Waals ferromagnet coupled to a superconducting resonator
*Alvaro Bermejillo-Seco, Luuk J. van der Goot, Matteo Arfini, Yaroslav M. Blanter, Gary A. Steele, Herre S.J. van der Zant*

This work from the Delft group demonstrates strong magnon-photon hybridization using exfoliated flakes of the van der Waals ferromagnet Cr₂Ge₂Te₆ — a 2D ferromagnet where magnetism persists down to the monolayer limit — placed on a low-impedance superconducting microwave coplanar waveguide resonator. Reproducible avoided crossings characteristic of the strong coupling regime were observed across six independent devices with flake thicknesses as thin as ~30 nm, confirming that the coupling is robust against fabrication variation. The coupling strength was found to scale as the square root of the flake thickness, consistent with volume-dependent magnon number, and projections indicate single-monolayer hybridization is within reach of modest resonator impedance engineering. The experiment establishes a CMOS-compatible route to integrating 2D magnetic materials with superconducting quantum circuits, opening avenues for on-chip magnon-based quantum memory and transduction between microwave photons and magnons. This is one of the first demonstrations of strong magnon-photon coupling in a truly van der Waals magnetic material at millikelvin temperatures, with implications for quantum networking architectures.

### [2605.05199] Field-induced asymmetric band flattening and ideal quantum geometry in rhombohedral graphene
*Hongyun Zhang et al.*

Using nanospot angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy (nanoARPES) with in-situ electrostatic gating on rhombohedral pentalayer graphene, this study directly resolves the gate-voltage-dependent evolution of the flat-band structure near the K point. Gating is found to drive a striking asymmetry between valence and conduction flat bands: the valence flat band develops a characteristic M-shaped dispersion (a saddle point at K) while the conduction flat band progressively flattens toward an ideal flat-band limit. Quantum geometry analysis shows the resulting Berry curvature and quantum metric in the flattened conduction band closely approach the ideal conditions required for fractional Chern insulator states, providing spectroscopic evidence for the geometric origin of correlated topological phases observed in transport under electron doping. The work bridges spectroscopy and topology in rhombohedral multilayer graphene, establishing nanoARPES as a key tool for mapping quantum geometry tuned by electrostatic gating in moiré and non-moiré 2D systems.

### [2605.12761] Imaging Interacting Two-Dimensional Anisotropic Electrons in Monolayer ReSe₂
Non-invasive scanning tunneling microscopy imaging of electrostatically gated monolayer 1T-ReSe₂ (a TMD with strongly reduced crystal symmetry due to Re clustering) reveals a rich phase diagram as a function of hole density and temperature. At low hole densities, the electrons crystallize into an oblique Wigner lattice with broken rotational symmetry determined by the native crystal anisotropy, demonstrating that the reduced symmetry of ReSe₂ directly templates anisotropic Wigner crystal order. At intermediate densities, this Wigner crystal melts into a smectic electron liquid crystal phase, where translational order is retained in one direction but lost in the other — analogous to classical liquid crystal phases, but for interacting quantum electrons. These observations establish monolayer ReSe₂ as a unique platform for studying anisotropic correlated electron physics in 2D without moiré engineering, providing real-space images of symmetry-broken electronic phases in a single-layer TMD.

### [2605.12686] On-demand steering of hyperbolic chiral polaritons in a van der Waals metal
*Andrea S. Dai, Fuyang Tay, Ding Xu et al. (D.N. Basov, Milan Delor)*

This work demonstrates the hyperbolic spin Hall effect — polarization-selective directional launching of polaritons — at visible/near-infrared wavelengths using MoOCl₂, a naturally hyperbolic van der Waals metal. Far-field pump-probe microscopy is used to launch hyperbolic plasmon polaritons and image their real-space propagation; circular pump polarization selectively excites polaritons propagating along opposite hyperbolic axes, constituting a nanoscale analog of the photonic spin Hall effect. The inherent crystal hyperbola of MoOCl₂ provides in-plane anisotropy that is intrinsic rather than engineered, enabling deterministic polarization-to-direction mapping without patterning or applied fields. The steering bandwidth (visible/NIR) and gate-tunable carrier density in MoOCl₂ open the prospect of reconfigurable on-chip polariton routing and beamsplitting for nanophotonic logic. This constitutes the first observation of the hyperbolic spin Hall effect in a metallic vdW material at optical frequencies.

### [2605.02646] Valley-Selective Tunneling Density of States in Monolayer MoS₂ Resonant Tunneling Devices
*Abir Mukherjee et al.*

The authors fabricate double-barrier resonant tunneling devices (RTDs) using CVD-grown monolayer MoS₂ as the active channel in a vertical tunnel junction geometry fully compatible with CMOS back-end-of-line processing. By exploiting the strong spin-valley locking in monolayer MoS₂ (where the K and K′ valleys have opposite spin polarization due to broken inversion symmetry and strong spin-orbit coupling), the RTD architecture enables selective resonant tunneling from/to specific valleys under suitable bias and alignment conditions, yielding sharp differential conductance peaks in the I-V characteristic. The valley-resolved tunneling density of states extracted from these peaks matches first-principles band structure calculations and reveals the effective valley splitting induced by the barrier material. This provides a direct electrical readout of valley polarization without optical pumping, and the CMOS compatibility positions monolayer MoS₂ RTDs as a scalable platform for valleytronic logic devices.

### [2605.03766] Gossamer Superconductivity in Moiré WSe₂ Bilayer
*Hui-Ke Jin et al.*

Twisted bilayer WSe₂ near the magic-angle regime is mapped onto an extended triangular-lattice Hubbard model incorporating long-range density-density interactions, and the superconducting ground state is studied using renormalized mean-field theory. At moderate Coulomb repulsion (U/t~6–8), the system avoids a conventional Mott insulator at half-filling because mobile doublons and holes are partially restored by the long-range interactions, giving rise to "gossamer" superconductivity — a correlated SC state with substantially reduced superfluid stiffness compared to the BCS limit but with robust phase coherence driven by antiferromagnetic superexchange. The pairing symmetry is found to be chiral d+id, consistent with broken time-reversal symmetry observed in recent transport experiments on twisted WSe₂. This mechanism provides a theoretical foundation for the experimentally observed proximity of superconductivity to Mott-insulating behavior in moiré TMD bilayers, and predicts distinct signatures in the optical conductivity and thermopower accessible to near-term experiments.

### [2605.00477] Time-Reversal-Symmetry Breaking in Multiband 2H-TaS₂ via Zero-Field Josephson Nonreciprocity
The zero-field Josephson diode effect is reported in van der Waals heterostructure junctions formed by stacking 2H-TaS₂ (the superconducting multiband TMD) on 2H-NbSe₂. In these atomically thin vdW junctions, interband scattering in TaS₂ acts as an effective phase-locking mechanism that generates an intrinsic anomalous phase difference in the Josephson coupling, yielding a nonsinusoidal current-phase relation and — crucially — nonreciprocal critical currents without any applied magnetic field. The key signature is a diode efficiency that changes sign when the bias current direction is reversed while the external conditions are unchanged, directly evidencing the presence of time-reversal-symmetry breaking in the bulk of multiband 2H-TaS₂. This result confirms theoretical predictions that multiband superconductors with interband phase frustration can spontaneously break TRS, and identifies 2D vdW heterostructure Josephson junctions as precision probes of multiband order parameter topology.

### [2605.14408] Multifunctional 2D CrSi₂N₄ Monolayer: Strain-Enhanced Properties
*Rao Uzair Ahmad, Fahd Sikandar Khan, Nasir Javed*

This DFT+HSE06 study characterizes a septuple-layer CrSi₂N₄ monolayer (isostructural with the recently synthesized MoSi₂N₄ family) across a comprehensive range of biaxial strains. The monolayer has an indirect bandgap of 0.58 eV (PBE) / 2.16 eV (HSE06) and an exceptional n-type thermoelectric power factor of 3.5 mW/mK² — exceeding most 2D thermoelectric candidates in the literature — which improves further under 5% tensile strain. Hydrogen evolution reaction performance is also strain-engineered: at +5% biaxial tensile strain, the Gibbs free energy of hydrogen adsorption approaches the optimal thermoneutral value (ΔG_H ≈ 0.46 eV), making CrSi₂N₄ a candidate for strain-tunable electrocatalysis. The combination of semiconducting, thermoelectric, optical absorption in the visible range, and HER activity positions CrSi₂N₄ as a multifunctional 2D platform relevant to energy harvesting and conversion applications.

### [2605.13254] In-Situ Tunable Superconducting Diode with Field-Free Infinite Nonreciprocity
*Razmik A. Hovhannisyan, Taras Golod, Amirreza Lotfian, Vladimir M. Krasnov*

The authors demonstrate a four-terminal planar Nb Josephson junction design where the diode polarity and nonreciprocity magnitude are fully reconfigurable in-situ without any external magnetic field. By independently biasing the four terminals, the device achieves threshold-free ac-current rectification, which in the limit of ideal device operation corresponds to effectively infinite nonreciprocity — the ratio of forward to reverse critical currents diverges. The design eliminates the principal drawback of magnetic-field-operated Josephson diodes (field incompatibility with superconducting circuits), making it directly integrable into superconducting microwave circuits, including transmon qubit chips and microwave signal processors. The ability to in-situ switch polarity without field cycling enables programmable microwave rectification and protection circuitry, and the planar fabrication is fully compatible with standard Nb superconducting circuit foundry processes.

### [2605.11185] Magnetic-Field-Tunable Cyclotron Hyperbolic Polaritons
This theoretical work predicts a new class of hyperbolic polariton arising in high-mobility narrow-gap semimetals (e.g., Dirac or Weyl semimetals with electron/hole Fermi surfaces) when a magnetic field perpendicular to the crystal surface drives cyclotron motion. The cyclotron-driven charge oscillations produce an anisotropic dielectric response tensor: within the cyclotron resonance spectral window, one principal component of the dielectric function is positive (along the field) and the other negative (transverse), creating the sign-contrast condition required for hyperbolic phonon/plasmon polaritons. The predicted modes are field-tunable — field strength sets the cyclotron frequency and thus the hyperbolic window — enabling continuous in-situ control of polariton directionality and canalization from a single cryostat-compatible magnet. The modes are predicted to be directly observable in THz near-field nanoscopy experiments on semimetals such as Cd₃As₂ or WTe₂ in standard dilution-refrigerator-compatible magnetic fields (1–5 T), bridging the fields of THz nanophotonics and topological semimetal physics.
