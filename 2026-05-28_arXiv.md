## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Nematic Phase Transitions in Multilayer Graphene Systems
**[2605.26270](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26270)** — R. David Mayrhofer & Andrey V. Chubukov

This theoretical work investigates spontaneous nematic symmetry breaking in multilayer graphene systems subject to an applied displacement field. Recent experiments on Bernal-stacked and rhombohedral multilayer graphene have revealed a striking variety of electronically ordered phases — including valley-polarized, spin-polarized, and nematically ordered states — that compete and coexist depending on gate tuning. The authors develop a field-theoretic framework to map the phase boundaries between these orders, showing that nematic order is stabilized by the interplay of van Hove singularities and inter-valley Coulomb interactions. The work predicts a cascade of finite-temperature nematic phase transitions as the displacement field is varied, potentially connected to the unconventional superconducting domes observed in the same systems. These results provide a unified picture of competing orders in multilayer graphene moiré and non-moiré devices, with implications for gate-tunable quantum phase engineering.

### Microscopic evidence of spin-driven multiferroicity and topological spin textures in monolayer NiI₂
**[2604.06959](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06959)** — Haitao Wang et al. | *Phys. Rev. Lett.* 136, 026402 (2026)

This work provides the first atomic-scale visualization of spin-driven multiferroicity in a freestanding 2D material, monolayer NiI₂, using spin-polarized scanning tunneling microscopy (SP-STM). The measurements reveal a canted spin-spiral state accompanied by a 2Q charge density modulation, confirming the coupling between spin and charge at the unit-cell level in this 2D van der Waals magnet. At the boundaries between spin-spiral domains, the authors discover topological spin textures in the form of meron/antimeron pairs — half-integer topological charges that form emergent nanoscale quasiparticles. These observations are quantitatively explained by a realistic spin model incorporating anisotropic Kitaev interactions and a generalized spin-current mechanism for type-II multiferroicity. The results establish monolayer NiI₂ as a versatile 2D platform where topological spin textures can be created and controlled electrically, with potential for low-dissipation memory devices.

### Radio-Frequency-Driven Reshaping of the Mesoscale CDW Landscape in 1T-TaS₂ Thin-Film Devices
**[2604.00463](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00463)** — (cross-listed, cond-mat.mtrl-sci / cond-mat.mes-hall)

This study demonstrates that radio-frequency (RF) electrical excitation can be used as a precision tool to reshape the mesoscale charge-density-wave (CDW) texture in quasi-two-dimensional 1T-TaS₂ thin-film devices. Under simultaneous RF and DC bias, the hysteretic current-voltage characteristics associated with the nearly commensurate–incommensurate CDW transition are dramatically altered: the loop collapses or branches into multiple step-like features in a frequency- and amplitude-dependent manner. In-situ Raman spectroscopy confirms that RF driving enhances the intensity and narrows the linewidth of the low-frequency CDW phonon modes, consistent with reduced dephasing and increased CDW coherence length. The authors capture these behaviors with a theoretical model combining an overdamped time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau description of the commensurate CDW phase with a morphology-informed percolative resistor-capacitor transport model. This work bridges quasi-2D correlated electron physics with microwave engineering, suggesting that RF frequency and amplitude can serve as independent control knobs for CDW phase manipulation in van der Waals thin-film devices.
