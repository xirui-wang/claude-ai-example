## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [Nature] Transdimensional Anomalous Hall Effect in Rhombohedral Thin Graphite
Rhombohedral-stacked few-layer graphite supports topological flat bands and strong orbital magnetism, making it a rich playground for anomalous Hall physics. Li et al. report that Hall resistance in rhombohedral thin graphite shows pronounced hysteresis under both perpendicular and in-plane magnetic fields — a combination not expected from either purely 2D or 3D anomalous Hall effect scenarios. This "transdimensional" Hall effect arises because coupled in-plane and out-of-plane orbital magnetizations produce a response that has no analog in conventional 2D or 3D materials. The discovery identifies a new class of anomalous Hall physics unique to transdimensional stacked-carbon systems and opens unexplored territory for correlated topological phenomena in few-layer graphite at the boundary between 2D and 3D behavior.

### [Nature Nanotechnology] Sub-1 nm Gate Length MoS₂ Transistors at Wafer Scale
Scaling 2D transition metal dichalcogenide transistors to sub-nanometer gate lengths is a critical milestone for next-generation semiconductor technology beyond silicon. This work achieves self-aligned MoS₂ FETs with physical gate lengths below 1 nm and channel lengths of 10 nm across full wafers, demonstrating high on/off ratios and subthreshold swing values approaching the thermal limit. The self-alignment process eliminates lithographic gate-to-contact misalignment that has previously been a barrier to yield at the wafer scale. The result validates 2D MoS₂ as a competitive channel material for ultra-scaled CMOS and establishes a manufacturable fabrication pathway for sub-nanometer transistors based on van der Waals semiconductors.

### [Nature Nanotechnology] Quantum Magnetometry on Twisted CrI₃: Moiré Magnetism with Super-Period
Twisted bilayer CrI₃ is an archetypal 2D moiré magnet in which competing interlayer exchange and the moiré potential create spatial modulations of magnetic order. Quantum magnetometry with a spin-defect scanning sensor resolves the spatial magnetic texture of twisted CrI₃ with nanoscale resolution, revealing a dominant magnetic periodicity that is roughly an order of magnitude larger than the geometric moiré superlattice. This unexpected super-period arises from long-wavelength commensurability effects between the twist-induced and exchange-driven modulations, analogous to beating patterns in incommensurate systems. The result deepens understanding of moiré magnetism in van der Waals magnets and provides a new design parameter — the super-period — for engineering topological spin textures and magnonic devices based on twisted 2D magnets.

### [Nature Physics] Self-Intercalation Tunes Ferromagnetic Dimensionality in Fe₃GeTe₂
The van der Waals magnet Fe₃GeTe₂ sits near the boundary between 2D and 3D magnetic behavior, making it sensitive to subtle structural changes. This work demonstrates that controlled self-intercalation of excess Fe atoms between the van der Waals layers continuously tunes the spin-wave stiffness and anisotropy, transitioning the effective magnetic behavior from 3D Heisenberg (isotropic) to 2D Ising (strongly anisotropic) character. The 2D Ising limit stabilizes long-range magnetic order down to the monolayer limit and raises the Curie temperature, offering a purely chemical route — without external strain or gating — to engineer magnetic dimensionality and enhance order in vdW magnets. This approach is generalizable to other intercalatable metallic vdW systems.

### [Phys. Rev. Lett. 137, 046301] Electrically Tunable Heavy Fermion Phase Diagram in Magic-Angle Twisted Trilayer Graphene
Magic-angle moiré systems provide a tunable quantum simulator of heavy-fermion physics, in which correlated flat-band electrons play the role of local magnetic moments and itinerant Dirac carriers mimic the conduction sea. By controlling the displacement field in magic-angle twisted symmetric trilayer graphene, this PRL demonstrates continuous tuning of the Kondo hybridization between these two subsystems. The resulting phase diagram reveals a zero-temperature quantum phase transition from an antiferromagnetically ordered semimetal (small Kondo coupling) to a paramagnetic heavy Fermi liquid (large Kondo coupling), with a diverging effective carrier mass and Fermi surface reconstruction at the quantum critical point (QCP). The experiment establishes moiré heterostructures as a versatile and electrically controllable platform for exploring correlated quantum critical phenomena in two dimensions.
