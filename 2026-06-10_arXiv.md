## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### BSE+ calculations for 2D materials: a unified description of excitons and plasmons ([2606.09192](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09192))
*Amalie H. Svaneborg, Kristian S. Thygesen — cond-mat.mtrl-sci*

This work addresses a long-standing challenge in the optical spectroscopy of 2D materials: prior Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) implementations captured excitonic bound states but failed to consistently describe the collective plasmonic response within the same framework. The authors introduce BSE+, an extended formalism that incorporates corrections beyond the standard ladder-diagram approximation, achieving a unified treatment of both exciton and plasmon excitations in 2D materials without ad hoc adjustments. They validate the approach against experimental optical spectra of benchmark 2D systems, demonstrating improved accuracy for both bound exciton energies and the continuum plasmonic features. The method is particularly significant for transition metal dichalcogenides (TMDs), where strong Coulomb interactions render both excitons and plasmons experimentally observable, and their coupling determines ultrafast optical responses. This framework is expected to accelerate quantitative ab initio predictions of optical properties across the broad class of 2D van der Waals materials.

### Layer-parity-defined surface polarization in Nb₃Cl₈ for excitonic modulation at van der Waals interfaces ([2606.09277](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09277))
*cond-mat.mtrl-sci / cond-mat.mes-hall*

Nb₃Cl₈ is a van der Waals magnet that undergoes a spontaneous symmetry lowering with interlayer charge disproportionation, making its surface properties highly sensitive to the number of layers. This paper demonstrates that the parity (odd vs even) of the layer count defines distinct surface out-of-plane electric polarization states in Nb₃Cl₈ flakes due to the broken inversion symmetry at surfaces. By integrating Nb₃Cl₈ as a gate material in van der Waals heterostructures, the authors show that this parity-controlled polarization can be used to electrostatically modulate the interlayer excitonic transitions in adjacent 2D semiconductor layers. The results reveal a new pathway for layer-count-controlled electrostatic gating in 2D heterostructure devices without the need for external dielectric gates. This parity effect represents a fundamentally 2D phenomenon with direct implications for engineering exciton-based optoelectronics in the 2D limit.

### Correlation enhanced resistance hysteresis near half filling in MoS₂/WSe₂ heterobilayer ([2606.09244](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09244))
*Yong Chen et al. — cond-mat.mes-hall*

The authors fabricate a high-quality MoS₂/WSe₂ moiré heterobilayer device with a field-effect mobility of 17,650 cm²V⁻¹s⁻¹, enabling transport measurements deep into the strongly correlated regime near half-moiré-band filling (ν = 1). Near half filling, they observe pronounced resistance hysteresis as a function of gate voltage, which they attribute to a correlation-enhanced ferroelectric instability: the Coulomb repulsion between electrons on the moiré lattice amplifies the tendency toward charge ordering with a spontaneous electric dipole moment. The hysteresis window grows systematically with decreasing temperature and increasing sample quality, consistent with an interaction-driven transition rather than disorder. This behavior provides transport evidence for ferroelectric order in a purely 2D semiconductor moiré system without the piezoelectric or polar lattice distortions typical of conventional ferroelectrics. The result connects correlated electron physics in moiré systems to ferroelectricity, with implications for non-volatile memory applications in 2D materials.

### Persistent currents, whirlpools, and local Chern markers in twisted TMD Chern insulators ([2606.09812](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09812))
*Francesco Cioni, Lorenzo Cavicchi, Nazzareno Africani, Giacomo Mazza, Fabio Taddei, Amir Yacoby, Marco Polini — cond-mat.mes-hall / cond-mat.str-el*

Twisted TMD homobilayers (e.g., twisted MoTe₂, twisted WSe₂) spontaneously break time-reversal symmetry in their integer Chern insulating ground states, giving rise to spin-polarized bulk orbital currents that form striking whirlpool patterns on the moiré length scale. This paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis of these persistent currents, computing their spatial profile using a continuum model with realistic TMD band parameters, and demonstrating that the peak current density in the moiré unit cell serves as an accurate real-space proxy for the topological Chern number — a directly measurable quantity for scanning probe experiments (e.g., scanning SQUID). The authors further analyze how finite-size effects broaden the Hall conductance plateaus and quantify deviations from ideal quantization in experimentally accessible device geometries. By connecting local Chern markers (real-space topological indicators) to observable current patterns, the work bridges abstract topological invariants and experimental observables in the rapidly growing field of moiré Chern magnets. The results are timely given recent scanning probe experiments on twisted MoTe₂ and WSe₂/MoTe₂ devices that have begun imaging moiré-scale current patterns.

### First-Principles Investigation of Electron-Phonon Coupling and Intrinsic Two-Gap Superconductivity in Hexagonal BAs₃ Monolayer ([2606.08423](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08423))
*Jakkapat Seeyangnok, Udomsilp Pinsook — cond-mat.supr-con / cond-mat.mtrl-sci*

Using density functional perturbation theory and Migdal-Eliashberg calculations, this study predicts that the hexagonal BAs₃ monolayer — a 2D boron arsenide compound — hosts intrinsic phonon-mediated superconductivity with two distinct superconducting gaps arising from separate electron-phonon coupling channels at inequivalent Fermi surface pockets. The calculated electron-phonon coupling constant λ places BAs₃ in the moderately strong-coupling regime, with a predicted critical temperature that could be accessible with current low-temperature transport technology. The two-gap nature is confirmed by the characteristic double-peak structure in the phonon spectral function and the anisotropic gap distribution across the Fermi surface. The results add BAs₃ to the growing catalog of predicted 2D superconductors and motivate experimental synthesis by molecular beam epitaxy or chemical vapor deposition on appropriate substrates. The significance lies in demonstrating that boron arsenide — already known for unusual thermal properties in its bulk form — can host emergent superconductivity when reduced to a monolayer.
