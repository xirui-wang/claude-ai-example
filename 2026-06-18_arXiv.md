## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### High-temperature ferromagnetism and antiferromagnetism in monolayer CrTe₂ — [2606.18148](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18148)
*(cond-mat.str-el / cond-mat.mes-hall / cond-mat.mtrl-sci)*

Anupama S and Mukul Kabir investigate the intricate magnetic phase diagram of monolayer CrTe₂ — a van der Waals 2D magnet — as functions of carrier density and the strength of spin-lattice coupling. The monolayer form of CrTe₂ is particularly interesting because its magnetic ground state (ferromagnetic or antiferromagnetic) is tunable by both electrostatic gating and epitaxial strain, unlike the ferromagnetic bulk. The study employs first-principles simulations to disentangle the contributions of electron–phonon-mediated spin-lattice coupling and charge doping to the Curie/Néel temperature, finding that strong spin-lattice coupling can stabilize high-temperature magnetism well above room temperature. This work advances understanding of magnetism in 2D materials and establishes monolayer CrTe₂ as a versatile platform for gate-tunable 2D spintronics and proximity-effect engineering.

### Optical vortex probe of loop-current chirality in moiré materials — [2606.16676](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16676)
*(cond-mat.mes-hall)*

This paper proposes a new optical probe capable of resolving intrinsic geometric chirality arising from loop currents in moiré materials, with twisted bilayer graphene (TBG) as the representative platform. In TBG, interlayer interference generates chiral electronic circulation on triangular plaquettes, producing a C₃-symmetric Berry curvature pattern. The authors show that this chirality enters the second-order optical response and can be isolated through the angular-momentum difference (Δℓ = 3 channel) of interfering optical vortex beams in helicity-dependent photocurrent measurements. C₃-breaking perturbations activate additional channels, providing a symmetry-selective means to detect and quantify geometric order. This work opens a route to probing loop-current phases and related quantum geometric order in moiré 2D systems using table-top nonlinear optics.

### Monolithic hybrid quantum dot devices in superconducting twisted bilayer graphene — [2606.14685](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14685)
*(cond-mat.mes-hall)*

This paper introduces an interface-free platform for quantum devices by monolithically integrating single-electron quantum dots with gate-tunable superconductivity in magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene (MATBG). Two complementary device types are realized electrostatically: a superconducting island exhibiting 2e-periodic Coulomb blockade (indicating a well-defined gap protected against quasiparticle poisoning) and a proximitized quantum dot embedded at the edge of the superconducting region. The scalability of electrostatic confinement within a single crystalline material overcomes limitations of conventional semiconductor–superconductor hybrids, which suffer from interface disorder and strain. By accessing the regime where flat-band superconductivity competes with strong on-site Coulomb repulsion, this work creates a powerful platform for studying correlated many-body physics with superconducting circuit control and advances the prospects for topological qubit architectures in graphene moiré systems.
