## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Direct Visualization of Moiré-Enhanced Topological Flat Band in Rhombohedral Graphene Superlattice (*Nature Materials*, vol. 25, Apr 2026)
Rhombohedral graphene superlattices host topological flat bands whose filling-dependent behaviour has been linked to the fractional quantum anomalous Hall (FQAH) effect, but the microscopic role of the moiré potential has remained debated. In this work, scanning tunnelling microscopy and spectroscopy provide real-space images of the local density of states in a hexagonal boron nitride-aligned rhombohedral graphene superlattice, directly visualising how the periodic moiré potential enhances the flat-band localisation and introduces a spatially modulated energy gap. The measurements reveal that the moiré confinement suppresses the kinetic energy of flat-band electrons by more than expected from single-particle estimates, consistent with interaction-driven band narrowing. The direct visualisation of the spatially varying topological gap provides key microscopic constraints on theoretical models of the FQAH state in this system. These results demonstrate that real-space STM imaging can disentangle moiré-potential and interaction effects in 2D graphene-based topological systems.

### Low-Temperature Epitaxial Growth of MoS₂ and Monolithic Integration with Si-CMOS (*Nature Nanotechnology*, Apr 2026)
Integrating 2D semiconductors with silicon CMOS requires growth temperatures below the ~400 °C back-end-of-line thermal budget, a constraint that has blocked wafer-scale 2D/CMOS co-integration. This work demonstrates epitaxial MoS₂ monolayer growth at 275 °C on Si/SiO₂ substrates using a specially designed metalorganic chemical vapour deposition chemistry, with X-ray diffraction and Raman spectroscopy confirming single-crystal domains exceeding 10 μm. Field-effect transistors fabricated from the low-temperature MoS₂ achieve on/off ratios >10⁷ and peak mobility ~30 cm²/Vs at room temperature, comparable to films grown at higher temperatures. The process is demonstrated on 150 mm wafers and monolithically integrated with pre-patterned Si-CMOS inverter circuits, producing functional 2D/CMOS hybrid logic gates. This breakthrough removes a fundamental materials integration barrier and opens a practical route toward heterogeneous 2D-CMOS compute architectures.

### Interlayer Exciton Crystal in Transition Metal Dichalcogenide Heterostructures (*Nature Communications*, Apr 2026)
Interlayer excitons in type-II transition metal dichalcogenide heterostructures carry permanent out-of-plane electric dipole moments that generate long-range repulsive interactions, enabling excitonic many-body phases analogous to Wigner crystallisation. In this work, optical reflection and emission spectroscopy on a MoSe₂/WSe₂ heterostructure show sharp spectral features at high exciton densities that are inconsistent with a fluid phase, instead exhibiting the polarisation response expected for a triangular crystal lattice. The crystallisation density and temperature are consistent with theoretical predictions for a repulsive 2D dipolar Bose gas with the measured dipole moment. Magnetic-field-dependent measurements confirm the excitonic character and rule out charge density wave or moiré-pinning artefacts. These results constitute the first direct observation of a bosonic dipolar crystal in a 2D optical system, with potential relevance to quantum simulation of strongly correlated bosonic lattice models.

### Orbital-Dependent Coulomb Drag in Electron-Hole Bilayer Graphene (*Physical Review Letters* 136, 126303)
Coulomb drag between spatially separated electron and hole layers provides a sensitive probe of interlayer correlations without direct electrical contact. Zhang et al. measure drag resistivity in a dual-gated graphene heterostructure where an electron bilayer is separated from a hole bilayer by a few nanometres of hBN, with independent Landau level filling control. The drag signal exhibits strong orbital index dependence: it is greatly enhanced when the dragged and drive layers share the same Landau level orbital quantum number, and nearly vanishes between orbital-mismatched levels. This orbital selectivity arises from the matrix-element structure of the Coulomb interaction between Dirac Landau level wavefunctions, which strongly suppresses coupling between different orbital channels. These results reveal that orbital quantum numbers act as conserved quantum labels that gate Coulomb-mediated interlayer energy transfer, with implications for drag-based probes of correlated phases in 2D bilayer systems.

### Light-Induced Odd-Parity Spin Splitting in 2D Collinear Antiferromagnets (*Physical Review Letters* 136, 126704)
Collinear antiferromagnets with combined space-time reversal symmetry (PT) can host altermagnetic spin splitting in the electronic bands, but PT symmetry itself precludes odd-parity (f-wave, p-wave) spin splitting in equilibrium. This work predicts and analyses a route to transiently inducing odd-parity spin splitting in PT-symmetric 2D collinear antiferromagnets through irradiation with circularly polarised light, which breaks both time-reversal and effective spatial symmetries via the Floquet mechanism. The resulting Floquet-modified band structure exhibits momentum-resolved spin polarisation with f-wave or p-wave symmetry whose sign and magnitude are tunable via laser helicity and intensity. The transient odd-parity state is predicted to be detectable by time-resolved ARPES and could be exploited for laser-controlled spintronic switching. This mechanism extends Floquet engineering to antiferromagnetic spintronics, potentially enabling ultrafast, non-thermal spin-state manipulation in 2D magnetic materials.
