## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### New frontiers in quantum science and technology using van der Waals Josephson junctions ([2604.15276](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15276))

This review by Sarkar, Mukherjee, Basu, Kundu, Kundu, and Deshmukh surveys the rapidly evolving field of Josephson junctions built from van der Waals (vdW) materials, a class of devices that has undergone a paradigm shift driven by the advent of 2D materials. The vdW materials library—spanning superconductors, magnets, topological insulators, and semiconductors—enables the integration of materials with vastly different properties within a single device, granting access to physics inaccessible in conventional Josephson junctions. A central advantage of vdW crystalline materials is their ability to realize Hamiltonians unique to 2D systems by controlling device symmetries, including inversion-symmetry breaking and spin–orbit coupling that can be tuned by electrostatic gating or stacking angle. The authors also highlight the exceptionally long relaxation times of many excitations in 2D heterostructures, opening pathways to quantum sensors where the 2D material itself acts as an efficient bus for transmitting excitations to active sensing elements. This review is significant because it maps out concrete directions for integrating 2D material Josephson junctions into quantum computing and sensing platforms, including microwave-frequency transmon qubits incorporating vdW superconductors.

### Twisted Bilayer Graphene Lifetimes At Integer Fillings: An Analytic Result ([2604.14303](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14303))

Hu, Shao, Crippa, Călugăru, Sangiovanni, Wehling, Glazman, and Bernevig derive analytic expressions for the linewidth and dispersion of correlated single-particle excitations in magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene (MATBG) at integer moiré band fillings, using the topological heavy-fermion (THF) model that captures simultaneously strong correlations and nontrivial band topology. They obtain explicit formulas for both dispersion renormalization and scattering rates for Hubbard-band excitations and low-energy Dirac modes, which are increasingly accessible in angle-resolved photoemission (ARPES) and scanning tunneling spectroscopy (STS) experiments. The analytic framework goes beyond numerical approaches, providing clear physical insight into how the Kondo-like hybridization between localized and itinerant electrons controls quasiparticle lifetimes at each integer filling. These results are significant because understanding quasiparticle lifetimes is essential for interpreting transport, optical, and microwave spectroscopy data in MATBG devices and for designing correlated-electron-based microwave circuits.

### Microscopic evidence of spin-driven multiferroicity and topological spin textures in monolayer NiI₂ ([2604.06959](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06959))

Using vectorial spin-polarized scanning tunneling microscopy (SP-STM), this study provides direct atomic-scale evidence of spin-driven multiferroicity in monolayer NiI₂, a 2D van der Waals material in which ferroelectricity emerges from an underlying spin order. The authors identify a canted spin-spiral ground state accompanied by a 2Q charge density modulation, confirming the magnetoelectric coupling mechanism at the monolayer limit. At spin-spiral domain walls, topological spin textures in the form of meron/antimeron pairs are discovered, extending the catalogue of topological magnetic textures into the strictly 2D limit. This work is significant because monolayer NiI₂ represents a rare single-material multiferroic platform where both magnetic and ferroelectric order are intrinsically linked at the two-dimensional limit, offering rich possibilities for electrically controlled spin devices and non-volatile magnonic memory elements.
