## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Probing Dielectric Screening in van der Waals Heterostructures via Pressure-Tuned Exciton Rydberg Series — [2605.18506](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18506)
*(2D Materials)*

This study exploits the Rydberg series of exciton excited states (1s, 2s, 3s, ...) in monolayer WSe₂ encapsulated in hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) as an in-situ optical probe of the dielectric environment under hydrostatic pressure. As pressure compresses interlayer spacings in the hBN encapsulant, the dielectric screening experienced by the 2D exciton changes, systematically shifting the energies of the higher Rydberg states and modifying the deviation from the hydrogen-like spectrum. The authors develop a quantitative model linking the pressure-induced evolution of interlayer distances to the bulk dielectric tensor of hBN, reproducing the observed spectral changes without adjustable parameters. This work is significant because dielectric screening governs excitonic binding energies, Coulomb interactions, and optical response in 2D materials, and the pressure-tuning approach provides a disorder-free, continuously variable handle on these properties—complementary to electrostatic and substrate-engineering strategies—opening pathways to engineering excitonic many-body physics in vdW heterostructures.

### Termination-Preserved Ultra-high Tunneling Magnetoresistance in Altermagnetic KV₂Se₂O — [2605.18493](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18493)
*(2D Materials)*

KV₂Se₂O is a layered van der Waals altermagnet in which nonrelativistic spin splitting arises without net magnetization, making it an attractive building block for next-generation spintronic devices. A central challenge for altermagnetic tunnel junctions has been low TMR ratios and strong sensitivity to the precise atomic termination at the tunnel barrier interface—a problem this study directly resolves. Using first-principles transport calculations, the authors show that the synergy between compressed nodal-point spin-degenerate channels and coplanar interfacial magnetic order gives intrinsic TMR above 10⁵% for all terminations; crucially, K-termination passivates the interface in a way that preserves bulk spin polarization through orbital symmetry matching, achieving TMR ratios up to 10¹²%. The orbital-resolved analysis establishes a clear microscopic mechanism and a design rule—interface passivation chemistry—that can be generalized to other 2D layered altermagnets, potentially enabling ultralow-power spintronic logic based on voltage-controlled spin splitting.

### Defect Control via Cu Enrichment Enhances Multifunctional Properties in the Polar Semiconductor Cu₁₊ₓMn₁₋ySiTe₃ — [2605.18584](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18584)
*(2D Materials)*

Cu₁₋ₓMn₁₊ySiTe₃ belongs to the CrSiTe₃ family of van der Waals layered multiferroics in which electric polarization is coupled to magnetization, yet its macroscopic ferroelectric response has been suppressed by a high density of stacking faults arising from non-stoichiometry. By inverting the Cu/Mn ratio to form Cu-enriched Cu₁₊ₓMn₁₋ySiTe₃, the authors show that an emergent interstitial site stabilizes a nearly stacking-fault-free monoclinic phase (space group Pm), and measurements of optical second-harmonic generation—a symmetry-sensitive probe of inversion breaking—confirm a pronounced enhancement of the nonlinear optical response. The concurrent improvements in ferroelectric and magnetic properties demonstrate that composition-driven defect engineering is a powerful lever for unlocking the full multiferroic functionality of layered vdW materials, with implications for 2D magnetoelectric devices and non-volatile memory.

### Phase dynamics and dissipation in tunnel ferromagnetic Josephson junctions — [2605.18586](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18586)
*(Microwave Engineering)*

This paper investigates the microwave-regime electrodynamics of tunnel ferromagnetic Josephson junctions (S-I-s-F-S multilayers, where s is a thin superconducting layer) as candidate elements for superconducting quantum circuits. By performing a comparative study of Nb- and Al-based junctions with different ferromagnetic interlayers across micrometric to submicrometric lateral dimensions, the authors extract junction parameters—plasma frequency, quality factor, critical current distribution—from current-voltage characteristics using the resistively-shunted junction microscopic model, and independently validate them through switching-current distribution measurements. Submicrometric Al-based devices are found to reach electrodynamic parameters (ωp/2π ~ GHz, Q ~ 10³) quantitatively comparable to those of state-of-the-art transmon qubits, and exhibit clear signatures of quantum phase diffusion at low temperature, confirming operation in the quantum regime. These results establish ferromagnetic Josephson junctions as a viable platform for microwave quantum circuits that inherently incorporate spin-valve functionality, offering a route toward magnetically tunable superconducting qubits without additional flux-bias circuitry.
