## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Evidence of Metallic Wigner Crystal in Rhombohedral Graphene (arXiv:[2604.00113](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00113))

Tonghang Han and colleagues use low-temperature transport measurements — including nonlinear and hysteretic current-voltage characteristics — to identify two distinct electron-crystal phases in ABC-stacked rhombohedral graphene multilayers (four to six layers) at low carrier densities of 0.3–0.5×10¹² cm⁻². The pinned Wigner crystal phase is evidenced by a sharp onset of nonlinearity below a threshold electric field, while the metallic Wigner crystal (mWC) — a state in which itinerant carriers coexist with a frozen electron lattice — produces a linear Hall coefficient co-existing with insulating nonlinearity. Rhombohedral multilayer graphene is an ideal host because its flat bands near charge neutrality allow Coulomb interactions to dominate kinetic energy at unprecedentedly accessible densities without a large external magnetic field. The mWC state, long predicted but not unambiguously observed in any 2D system, is particularly significant as it challenges simple distinctions between metallic and insulating electron order. These results establish rhombohedral graphene as a clean and highly tunable 2D platform for studying interaction-driven crystalline phases of electrons.

### Moiré Mott Correlated Mosaics in Twisted Bilayer 1T-TaS₂ (arXiv:[2604.02001](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02001))

Ana Vera Montoto, Jose L. Lado, and Adolfo O. Fumega theoretically study what happens when two monolayers of the canonical Mott insulator 1T-TaS₂ are stacked with a twist angle: in monolayer form, the star-of-David charge-density-wave reconstruction produces a triangular lattice of local moments whose Mott gap is correlation-driven, while in the bulk the gap is instead single-particle in origin due to interlayer coupling. Exploiting this stacking-dependent gap mechanism, the authors show that a twisted bilayer exhibits spatially varying competition between the many-body and the single-particle gaps, generating a mosaic superlattice of correlated (Mott) and non-magnetic insulating (trivial) regions with the moiré period. An applied interlayer bias voltage continuously tunes the relative stability of the two regions, enabling gate-controlled charge transfer and on-demand quenching of local moments across the sample. The work provides a new handle on correlated phases in van der Waals materials without requiring proximity to a metal or dopant atoms, and suggests that twist angle serves as a knob to engineer spatial patterns of Mottness. This moiré patterning of correlated states could underpin future devices exploiting spatially selective magnetic order or Kondo physics at the unit-cell scale.

### Electronic Raman Scattering from 2D Metals with Broken Inversion Symmetry (arXiv:[2604.00486](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00486))

Mojdeh Saleh and Saurabh Maiti develop a comprehensive theory of inelastic photon scattering (electronic Raman scattering, eRS) in two-dimensional metallic systems where spin-orbit coupling (SOC) breaks SU(2) spin-rotation symmetry — a class encompassing Rashba, Dresselhaus, and Ising (valley-Zeeman) metals as well as doped TMDs and graphene-on-heavy-metal heterostructures. The key insight is that in conventional metals the Raman probe couples only to charge excitations, but the broken SU(2) introduces a direct photon–spin interaction that allows eRS to couple to spin-flip and chiral-spin collective modes without any need for tuning to an internal optical resonance. The theory predicts sharp resonance features near the SOC energy scale in specific polarization channels, with signal amplitudes in SOC graphene systems exceeding those in conventional 2DEG by orders of magnitude due to the linear Dirac dispersion. This makes electronic Raman a practical non-contact, symmetry-resolved spectroscopic probe for detecting and characterizing spin-orbit-split bands in 2D quantum materials. The work is directly relevant to current efforts to probe spin excitations in moiré heterostructures and gate-tunable SOC systems using table-top optical setups.
