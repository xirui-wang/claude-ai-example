## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Observation of propagating collective spin–valley modes in twisted WSe₂ ([Nature Physics, Xiong et al., 2026](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-026-03280-w))
**2D Materials.** Twisted bilayer WSe₂ at specific moiré fillings is theorized to host an intervalley-coherent (IVC) ordered state—a spin–valley superfluid—analogous to an excitonic condensate but with spin and valley as internal degrees of freedom. Xiong et al. employ a space-and-time-resolved ultrafast pump–probe imaging technique to directly visualize the propagation of charge-neutral collective excitations in this system near the Van Hove singularity filling. Two distinct modes are observed: a fast-propagating mode consistent with the gapless Goldstone (phase) mode of the spin–valley superfluid, and a slower mode consistent with the gapped amplitude (Higgs-like) mode. The two modes carry opposite spin–valley polarization and propagate at very different velocities, providing direct real-space evidence for the phase and amplitude modes of a 2D quantum ordered state. This result establishes twisted WSe₂ as a platform for studying broken-symmetry collective dynamics in moiré 2D materials and opens the door to valley-coherent mode engineering.
