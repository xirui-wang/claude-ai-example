## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Mechanochemical Nano-Writing of an Atomically Thin Metal ([2606.03710](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03710))
Shuai Zhang, Sanfeng Wu, Robert W. Carpick and colleagues demonstrate a breakthrough technique for synthesizing and patterning a 2D superconductor with nanoscale precision. By pressing an atomic force microscope tip onto a van der Waals stack of bilayer MoTe₂ encapsulated with a Pd source layer, the applied mechanical force drives a localized solid-state reaction that produces atomically thin Pd₇MoTe₂—a new 2D metal—with approximately 50 nm lateral resolution. The resulting material is superconducting and fully integrated within the vdW stack, circumventing the need for top-down lithography or high-temperature processing. This mechanochemical writing approach offers a general route to inscribe metallic or superconducting features into 2D heterostructures on demand, with potential applications in quantum device fabrication and on-chip circuitry at the atomic scale.

### BSE+ Calculations for 2D Materials: A Unified Description of Excitons and Plasmons ([2606.09192](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09192))
Amalie H. Svaneborg and Kristian S. Thygesen (DTU) address a longstanding limitation of the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in describing optical response of 2D materials: BSE accurately captures excitonic features but underestimates the real part of the dielectric function due to truncated high-energy transitions, introducing spurious plasmon peaks in electron energy loss spectra (EELS). The BSE+ method resolves this by coupling a four-point BSE for the irreducible polarizability—capturing excitonic correlations—with a two-point RPA Dyson equation that includes high-energy transitions at the random-phase-approximation level. Applied to a series of transition metal dichalcogenide monolayers, BSE+ faithfully reproduces both the sharp excitonic resonances at low energy and the plasmonic structure at high energy, providing a computationally tractable and physically complete description of the optical response of 2D materials and van der Waals heterostructures.

### Symmetry-Breaking Induced Spin Transport and Magneto-Optical Regulation in 2D Altermagnet Ru₂MoSe₄ ([2606.09127](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09127))
Wenpeng Wang, Hang Shi, Yuping Tian, Wei-Jiang Gong, and Xiangru Kong (Northeastern University) propose the 2D van der Waals material Ru₂MoSe₄ as a tunable altermagnet platform for spintronics. In its ground state, the monolayer Ru₂MoSe₄ has fully compensated macroscopic spin—a fundamental obstacle for practical device integration of altermagnets. The authors show that applying uniaxial strain breaks the protecting S₄z T symmetry, generating fully spin-polarized charge currents and a piezo-magnetically induced net magnetization, while AC-stacking bilayers simultaneously exhibit the magneto-optical Kerr effect. These findings establish a feasible mechanism to simultaneously activate electrical spin signals and optical contrast in a single 2D material, opening a route toward spin-filtering devices based on 2D altermagnets.

### Strain-Programmable Exciton Diffusion in Moiré Heterostructures ([2606.10482](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10482))
Chiho Song and coauthors explore how intentionally applied interlayer strain transforms the diffusion landscape for interlayer excitons in WSe₂-MoSe₂ van der Waals heterostructures. Strain breaks the three-fold rotational (C₃) symmetry of the moiré superlattice, creating an anisotropic potential that imprints as linearly polarized interlayer exciton emission aligned to the strain axis. Crucially, the exciton diffusion becomes spatially anisotropic: excitons preferentially migrate along the stretched moiré superlattice orientation over extended distances, providing direct spatial encoding of the moiré potential landscape. This strain-programmable control of exciton flow constitutes a flexible tool for engineering excitonic circuits and optical interconnects within 2D heterostructure platforms.

### Frequency-Resolved Decoherence Spectroscopy of a Semiconductor Charge Qubit Coupled to a High-Impedance Resonator ([2606.09722](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09722))
This work presents a hybrid circuit quantum electrodynamics (cQED) architecture in which a GaAs double-quantum-dot charge qubit is capacitively coupled to a frequency-tunable, high-impedance SQUID-array microwave resonator. By sweeping the qubit transition frequency across 3–6 GHz—enabled by the resonator's broad tunability—the authors perform frequency-resolved decoherence spectroscopy and disentangle energy relaxation from pure dephasing using time-domain measurements. A clear crossover is identified: at qubit frequencies ≤4.5 GHz, charge noise-driven pure dephasing dominates the linewidth, while at higher frequencies phonon- and photon-mediated energy relaxation takes over. This systematic spectroscopic approach directly maps the noise spectrum experienced by the qubit over a wide energy window, providing actionable guidance for minimizing decoherence in GaAs-based and hybrid semiconductor-superconductor quantum processors.
