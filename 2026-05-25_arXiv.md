## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Atomic scale demonstration of ferromagnetism in a single layer FeCl₂ on Au(111) — [2605.22783](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22783)

FeCl₂ is a van der Waals magnetic insulator that can be grown by molecular beam epitaxy on metallic substrates, but its ground-state magnetic and electronic properties in the monolayer limit on a conducting support had not been confirmed. Using spin-polarized scanning tunneling microscopy on a single FeCl₂ layer on Au(111), Candia et al. unambiguously demonstrate ferromagnetic ordering, a wide insulating gap of 3.3 eV, and a strongly spin-polarized conduction band emerging at 1.5 eV above the Fermi level. The Au(111) substrate provides sufficient charge transfer to stabilize the monolayer while preserving its magnetic character, establishing a benchmark for substrate-dependent engineering of 2D magnetic properties. These results are significant because they position single-layer FeCl₂ as a confirmed ferromagnetic insulator compatible with van der Waals heterostructure fabrication, opening pathways for proximity-effect experiments and spin-filter tunnel junctions based on this 2D material.

### Probing Dielectric Screening in van der Waals Heterostructures via Pressure-Tuned Exciton Rydberg Series — [2605.18506](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18506)

Two-dimensional semiconductors embedded in van der Waals heterostructures have optical properties strongly governed by the dielectric environment, yet direct measurement of the encapsulant's dielectric function under perturbation has been difficult. Badola et al. exploit the pressure sensitivity of the Rydberg excitonic series in monolayer WSe₂ encapsulated in hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) to directly extract the pressure-induced change in hBN's dielectric constant, constructing a model based on the pressure evolution of interlayer spacing and bulk hBN dielectric properties. The method converts each excitonic transition into a dielectric sensor, yielding quantitative information about screening without the need for additional metallic contacts or electrodes. This establishes a new non-invasive methodology for dielectric sensing within vdW heterostructures, with broad applicability to other 2D semiconductor systems where the surrounding dielectric profoundly affects excitonic binding energies and optical spectra.

### Spin-caloritronic signatures of soft magnons in bilayer CrSBr — [2605.18478](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18478)

CrSBr is a layered van der Waals antiferromagnet with strong excitonic properties and a layered structure amenable to magnetic field tuning, making it a rich platform for spin caloritronics. Den Teuling et al. calculate the magnon spin angular momentum as a function of applied magnetic field and wave vector in bilayer CrSBr, showing that triaxial anisotropy and intralayer dipolar interactions renormalize the magnon spin beyond the simple ℏ per magnon picture. Near the field-induced magnon softening transition, the spin angular momentum diverges, giving rise to a pronounced peak in the thermal spin Seebeck coefficient that serves as a clear experimental signature. This work not only highlights CrSBr bilayers as a platform for exploring magnon-based spin transport with tunable angular momentum, but also provides a quantitative framework connecting the unusual spin properties of soft magnons to measurable thermoelectric spin voltages.

### Competing incommensurability, electronic correlations, and superconductivity in a hybrid transition metal dichalcogenide — [2605.22522](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22522)

The hybrid TMD polytype 4Hb-TaS₂, composed of alternating strongly correlated 1T and metallic 1H layers, has attracted attention as a potential host of unconventional superconductivity, but the origin of its exotic properties has remained unclear. Souza et al. use STM and STS to reveal an emergent incommensurate interlayer potential arising from the structural mismatch between the two layer types, which competes with the 1T layer's intrinsic charge-density-wave order and suppresses its long-range coherence. DFT+DMFT calculations show that the lattice mismatch locally modulates interlayer distance, tuning both hybridization and charge transfer between the correlated 1T and metallic 1H layers. These findings identify incommensurate potentials as a previously overlooked ingredient in hybrid TMDs and establish a mechanism by which structural incommensurability, electronic correlations, and CDW order jointly control unconventional superconductivity in 2D layered systems.
