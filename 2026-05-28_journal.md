## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Artificial neurons based on printed molybdenum disulfide nanosheet networks
**Nature Nanotechnology** (Published 19 May 2026)

This work demonstrates artificial neurons built entirely from inkjet-printed networks of MoS₂ nanosheets — a semiconducting 2D transition metal dichalcogenide — that reproduce multi-order spiking dynamics on physiologically relevant timescales. The printed MoS₂ film acts as a memristive medium: the percolation-threshold conductance of the nanosheet network enables intrinsic threshold switching behavior analogous to biological neuron firing. By controlling nanosheet concentration and printing resolution, the authors engineer integrate-and-fire, bursting, and adaptive spiking modes within a single printable platform. The device fabrication is scalable and compatible with flexible substrates, making it attractive for wearable neuromorphic sensors and biohybrid brain interfaces. This work establishes 2D TMDs as viable building blocks for neuromorphic hardware beyond conventional silicon-based approaches.

### Photoinduced metal-to-insulator transitions in 2D moiré devices
**Phys. Rev. Lett.** 136, 206502 (2026) — Yiliu Li et al.

Using ultrafast optical pump–probe spectroscopy on gate-tunable 2D moiré devices (twisted bilayer or twisted TMD heterostructures at correlated filling), the authors demonstrate that femtosecond light pulses can reversibly switch the system between metallic and Mott-insulating ground states. The photoinduced transition is attributed to transient photocarrier injection that screens the Hubbard-U interaction, collapsing the correlation gap on sub-picosecond timescales. Time-resolved reflectance maps reveal spatially resolved domain nucleation and growth of the metallic phase, consistent with a first-order transition kinetics framework. The recovery dynamics are strongly dependent on gate-voltage proximity to the correlated insulator filling, with recovery times ranging from tens of picoseconds to nanoseconds. These results open a route to ultrafast all-optical control of correlation-driven phases in 2D moiré quantum materials.

### Microscopic evidence of spin-driven multiferroicity and topological spin textures in monolayer NiI₂
**Phys. Rev. Lett.** 136, 026402 (2026) — Haitao Wang et al. | arXiv: [2604.06959](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06959)

This study uses spin-polarized scanning tunneling microscopy to visualize multiferroic order and topological spin textures in freestanding monolayer NiI₂ at atomic resolution. The measurements directly confirm a canted spin-spiral state with an accompanying 2Q charge density modulation — the signature of spin-driven type-II multiferroicity — in which the electric polarization is generated by the vector product of neighboring spin pairs. Domain walls between spiral domains are found to host bound pairs of merons and antimerons: topologically charged half-skyrmion excitations with winding numbers ±1/2. A minimal spin Hamiltonian incorporating ferromagnetic exchange, bond-dependent Kitaev interactions, and the Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interaction quantitatively reproduces all observed real-space spin textures. The demonstration of electrically controllable topological spin textures in an atomically thin magnet establishes monolayer NiI₂ as a compelling platform for 2D magnetoelectric devices and non-volatile topological memory based on meron manipulation.
