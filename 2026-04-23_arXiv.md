## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Rippled graphene pores as fluidic memristive devices with synaptic and neuromorphic functionalities
[2604.19228](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19228) | Wenzhe Zhou et al.

Nanofluidic memristive devices encode information in ionic conductance states and are attractive for biocompatible neuromorphic computing because they share ionic charge carriers with biological neural systems. This work introduces a new operating principle: a micrometer-size pore whose rim is encased by strongly curved and tightly stacked graphene layers (rippled graphene). The authors demonstrate that the corrugated graphene environment at the pore edge dramatically slows ionic dynamics, producing a robust history-dependent (memristive) conductance that persists at room temperature. The device recapitulates key synaptic plasticity phenomena including spike-timing-dependent plasticity, making it directly relevant to neuromorphic architectures. The result establishes that nanoscale graphene geometry—rather than nanopore size alone—is a powerful design handle for ionic memory devices, with broad implications for carbon-based neuromorphic hardware.

### Intrinsic *i*-wave altermagnetism in 2D graphene superlattices
[2604.19661](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19661) | Cuiju Yu, Jose L. Lado

Altermagnets are collinear antiferromagnets that break time-reversal symmetry and exhibit momentum-dependent spin splitting without net magnetization; to date, they have been realized almost exclusively in *d*-wave transition-metal compounds. This work demonstrates that graphene antidot superlattices—periodic arrays of vacancies in a graphene sheet—harbor an intrinsic magnetic instability that spontaneously generates *i*-wave altermagnetic order, a higher-symmetry class with eight-fold momentum-space spin pattern. Combining density-functional theory and atomistic tight-binding models, the authors establish a symmetry-guided design principle for engineering this state in both monolayer and bilayer graphene antidot systems, showing that the *i*-wave spin splitting is driven purely by Coulomb interactions on the graphene sublattice. The carbon-based platform is advantageous for integration with existing graphene device technologies, proximity coupling to superconductors, and electrostatic tunability, opening a new direction for altermagnetic spintronics in a purely organic 2D material.

### Microscopic evidence of spin-driven multiferroicity and topological spin textures in monolayer NiI₂
[2604.06959](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06959)

Monolayer NiI₂ is the canonical van der Waals type-II multiferroic, where a noncollinear spin spiral directly induces in-plane electric polarization through the inverse Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interaction. Prior evidence for this coupling was largely indirect; this study uses vectorial spin-polarized STM to map the spin texture at atomic resolution, directly resolving a canted spin-spiral ground state with a fully determined spin rotation plane and an associated 2Q charge modulation consistent with spin-lattice coupling. Crucially, at domain walls between spiral domains the authors identify meron/antimeron pairs—topological point defects carrying half-integer topological charge—that are accompanied by characteristic charge patterns and local band shifts, providing the first direct atomistic evidence for bound ferroelectric charges localized at domain walls. The demonstration of coexisting topological spin textures and bound polar charges in a single monolayer places NiI₂ among the most information-dense 2D materials, with potential for electric-field writing of topological magnetic textures.
