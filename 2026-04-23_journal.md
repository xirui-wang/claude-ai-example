## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Angular interplay of nematicity, superconductivity and strange metallicity in magic-angle twisted trilayer graphene
[DOI: 10.1038/s41567-026-03202-w](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41567-026-03202-w) | Naiyuan J. Zhang et al. | *Nature Physics* (2026)

Magic-angle twisted trilayer graphene (MATTG) exhibits an unusually rich phase diagram in which superconductivity, nematicity (spontaneous rotational symmetry breaking), and strange-metallic resistivity appear in close proximity in gate-voltage–temperature space. This work systematically maps these phases as a function of both carrier density and the precise twist angle, revealing that the three orders are angularly coupled: the nematic director locks to a specific crystallographic orientation, the superconducting dome is displaced in density relative to the correlated insulator, and the strange-metal linear-in-T resistivity flanks the superconducting phase in a pattern consistent with quantum-critical fluctuations. The authors find that small deviations from the magic angle strongly modulate the relative stability of nematicity versus superconductivity, suggesting that strain-controlled nematic order acts as a competing order parameter that can either nucleate or suppress Cooper pairing. These results impose strong constraints on theories of moiré superconductivity and highlight twisted multilayer graphene as a uniquely tunable platform for studying the interplay of symmetry-broken orders near a quantum critical point.

### Direct visualization of the moiré potential in rhombohedral graphene
*Nature Materials* (April 2026)

The fractional quantum anomalous Hall (FQAH) effect observed in rhombohedral trilayer graphene aligned to hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) requires a moiré superlattice potential to generate a nearly flat topological band; however, the microscopic form of this potential has until now been inferred only indirectly from transport. This work uses cryogenic scanning tunneling microscopy to directly image the moiré potential landscape at atomic resolution in rhombohedral graphene/hBN heterostructures. The authors resolve a periodic potential corrugation whose depth and spatial symmetry are consistent with the honeycomb (AA/AB stacking contrast) expected from the alignment geometry, and they measure potential depths that agree quantitatively with first-principles predictions for the graphene/hBN coupling. These direct real-space maps confirm the theoretical underpinning of the FQAH state and show that disorder in the moiré potential is spatially correlated on length scales larger than the moiré unit cell, which may explain the robustness of the topological gaps observed in transport. The result establishes direct STM imaging as a powerful probe of the potential landscape governing emergent topology in van der Waals heterostructures.

### Reentrant superconductivity in a naturally occurring Josephson junction array tuned by radio-frequency power
[DOI: 10.1038/s41467-026-71256-8](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-026-71256-8) | *Nature Communications* (2026)

Granular aluminum thin films consist of aluminum nanocrystals separated by thin aluminum-oxide tunnel barriers, naturally realizing a disordered Josephson junction array (JJA) with tunable coupling. This study demonstrates that continuous microwave irradiation in the GHz range drives a reentrant superconducting transition: films cooled through Tc in the absence of RF power show a conventional superconducting state, but applying RF power above a critical amplitude at millikelvin temperatures drives the system back toward the normal state; counter-intuitively, a further increase in RF power restores superconductivity. The authors interpret this non-monotonic behavior as a competition between photon-assisted Cooper-pair breaking (destructive at intermediate RF power) and parametric amplification of Josephson plasma modes that reinforce phase coherence (constructive at high RF power) within the JJA network. This work demonstrates that microwave fields can serve as a knob to engineer the superconducting phase diagram of a granular superconductor, with implications for controlling quantum noise in superconducting qubit circuits where granular aluminum is a key component of high-kinetic-inductance resonators.

### Persistent normal-state third-harmonic generation in superconductors
[PRL 136, 146004](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.136.146004) | *Physical Review Letters* (April 10, 2026)

Nonlinear microwave response is a sensitive probe of pairing in superconductors: below Tc, third-harmonic generation (THG) arises from collective modes and quasiparticle contributions that encode gap symmetry. This work reports THG measurements on Fe-based superconductors that persist anomalously far above Tc, well into the normal metallic state. The authors show that the THG signal tracks the pseudogap temperature scale rather than Tc, demonstrating that strong pairing fluctuations survive well above the superconducting onset. A theoretical analysis attributes the large normal-state THG to short-lived Cooper pairs (preformed pairs) that contribute nonlinear electromagnetic response without establishing long-range phase coherence. These findings provide a new spectroscopic tool—THG microwave spectroscopy—for detecting preformed pairing above Tc in unconventional superconductors and suggest that the pseudogap regime in Fe-based systems has a direct superconducting precursor character analogous to that proposed for cuprates.
