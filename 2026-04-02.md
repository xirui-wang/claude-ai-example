## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [Evidence of Metallic Wigner Crystal in Rhombohedral Graphene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00113)
**arXiv:2604.00113** | Tonghang Han, Jackson P. Butler, Shenyong Ye, Zhenqi Hua, Surajit Dutta, Zach Hadjri, Zhenghan Wu, Jixiang Yang, Junseok Seo, Phatthanon Pattanakanvijit, Emily Aitken, Kenji Watanabe, Takashi Taniguchi, Peng Xiong, Eli Zeldov, Zhengguang Lu, Raymond Ashoori, Long Ju

Wigner crystals — lattices of electrons stabilized when Coulomb repulsion overwhelms kinetic energy — have long been sought in two-dimensional systems, but prior realizations required strong magnetic fields or perfectly flat Landau levels. This work demonstrates for the first time that rhombohedral stacked multilayer graphene (tetra-, penta-, and hexalayer) provides a gate-tunable platform for both a conventional pinned Wigner crystal (WC) and a metallic Wigner crystal (mWC), with neither requiring an external magnetic field. By applying a gate-controlled perpendicular displacement field to flatten the conduction band, the authors observe an insulating state at non-zero carrier density whose transport exhibits the nonlinear, hysteretic current–voltage characteristics of a classically pinned WC. Increasing the displacement field further reveals transport dominated by hole-like carriers whose density is at most 15% of the total nominal electron density — the hallmark of an mWC in which a minority of itinerant holes coexists with the majority pinned electron lattice and disobeys the Streda relation. These results establish rhombohedral multilayer graphene as a rich platform for exploring correlated electron-crystal physics in a continuously tunable 2D system, with immediate relevance for understanding strongly correlated states in moiré and flat-band graphene more broadly.

### [Crystals Caught Doping: Metallic Wigner Crystals in Rhombohedral Graphene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00114)
**arXiv:2604.00114** | Junkai Dong, Tomohiro Soejima, Daniel E. Parker, Ashvin Vishwanath

This companion theoretical paper to arXiv:2604.00113 provides the microscopic mechanism behind the metallic Wigner crystal (mWC) phase observed in rhombohedral multilayer graphene. The authors show that a commensurate Wigner crystal — one whose lattice constant is rationally related to the carrier density — is generically unstable to self-doping: the crystal spontaneously expels a minority of electrons that become itinerant, converting the insulating commensurate WC into an incommensurate, and therefore metallic, mWC. The instability is governed by the competition between the charge gap at commensurability and a "packing bias" — the energy cost of shifting away from a commensurate registry — and can be computed from band structure parameters accessible to DFT. Applied to rhombohedral multilayer graphene, these calculations correctly predict which displacement-field windows support the mWC, the sign and magnitude of the self-doping fraction, and the anomalous hole-like carrier density seen in experiment. The work generalizes earlier notions of Wigner crystal physics to multi-band flat-band systems, providing a predictive framework for engineering exotic charge-ordered metallic states in 2D van der Waals materials.
