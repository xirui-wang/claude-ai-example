## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Polaron-Mediated Anisotropic Exchange in 2D Magnets
**arXiv:** [2603.17619](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17619) | **Authors:** Johanna P. Carbone, Jakob Baumsteiger, Cesare Franchini et al.

This study investigates how polaron formation — the dressing of charge carriers by lattice distortions — renormalises the magnetic exchange interactions in two-dimensional van der Waals magnets. Using a combination of density functional theory and model Hamiltonian approaches, the authors show that polaron-phonon coupling introduces an anisotropic correction to the nearest-neighbour exchange integrals that is absent in the bare electronic structure. The key finding is that even moderate electron-phonon coupling can switch the easy-axis direction of the magnetic anisotropy, providing a previously unrecognised mechanism for electrically or optically tuning 2D magnetic order. This work is significant because anisotropy engineering is central to realising stable long-range ferromagnetic or antiferromagnetic order in monolayer limits, and a polaron-based handle could enable dynamic, reversible control without chemical substitution.

### Emergence of Non-Hermitian Magic Angles and Topological Phase Transitions in Twisted Bilayer Lattices
**arXiv:** [2603.29779](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29779) | **Authors:** Shaina Gandhi, Gourab Paul, Srijata Lahiri, Saurabh Basu (IIT Guwahati)

Building on the celebrated magic-angle physics of twisted bilayer graphene, this paper extends the concept to non-Hermitian twisted bilayer lattices where interlayer hopping has a gain-loss asymmetry. The authors derive analytically and confirm numerically that flat-band magic angles survive and are enriched in the non-Hermitian setting, accompanied by exceptional-point degeneracies in the complex band structure. At these non-Hermitian magic angles, the system undergoes a topological phase transition characterised by a change in the non-Hermitian winding number, which the authors connect to skin-effect localisation of the flat-band wavefunctions. The significance lies in opening a route to engineer correlated-electron-like physics in photonic or dissipative quantum platforms that naturally possess non-Hermitian degrees of freedom.

### A Material-Agnostic Platform to Probe Spin-Phonon Interactions Using High-Overtone Bulk Acoustic Wave Resonators
**Authors:** Q. Greffe, A. Hugot, S. Zhang et al.

This work presents a microwave-frequency bulk acoustic wave (BAW) resonator platform designed to detect and quantify spin-phonon coupling in thin-film magnetic materials without requiring dedicated device fabrication for each material. The resonator operates at high-overtone frequencies (GHz range) and exploits the strong overlap between its acoustic mode and the magnetic film placed on top, transducing changes in spin dynamics into measurable shifts of the resonator's microwave reflection spectrum. The authors demonstrate the platform's material agnosticism by testing it on several 2D and thin-film magnets with disparate spin-orbit coupling strengths, recovering consistent spin-phonon coupling constants. The approach bridges microwave engineering and condensed matter physics, providing a rapid characterisation tool for emerging quantum materials and a potential transduction element for hybrid quantum systems coupling magnons to phonons at microwave frequencies.

### Oxide-Nitride Heteroepitaxy for Low-Loss Dielectrics in Superconducting Quantum Circuits
**Authors:** David A. Garcia-Wetten, Mitchell J. Walker et al.

This study addresses a critical engineering challenge in superconducting quantum computing: microwave dielectric loss from the substrate and interfacial layers that limits qubit coherence times. By developing oxide-nitride heteroepitaxial stacks grown by molecular beam epitaxy, the authors achieve dielectric loss tangents more than an order of magnitude lower than standard thermal SiO₂ or Si₃N₄ gate dielectrics at millikelvin temperatures and single-photon microwave power levels. Structural characterisation via X-ray diffraction and transmission electron microscopy confirms the epitaxial quality and absence of amorphous interfacial layers that are the dominant loss source. The demonstration of integration-compatible deposition on silicon substrates alongside superconducting resonators showing improved internal quality factors makes this a direct step toward scalable, low-loss microwave circuit architectures for quantum information processing.

### Higher-Order Topological Superconductivity and Electrically Tunable Majorana Corner Modes in Monolayer MnXPb₂ (X=Se, Te)-Pb Heterostructure
**Authors:** Yongting Shi, Qing Wang, Zhen-Guo Fu, Ping Zhang, Ning Hao

This theoretical study predicts that monolayer MnXPb₂ (X = Se or Te) — a family of two-dimensional magnetic materials — placed in proximity to a conventional Pb superconductor realises a higher-order topological superconducting (HOTSC) phase. Using first-principles band structure calculations combined with a proximity-coupled effective model, the authors establish that time-reversal symmetry breaking by the MnX sublattice, together with spin-orbit coupling from the Pb atoms, gaps out the edges of the conventional topological superconducting phase, leaving zero-energy Majorana corner modes localised at sample corners. Critically, the corner mode energy can be tuned from zero by an applied gate voltage, enabling electrical control of the Majorana gap. The significance of this work is that it provides a materials-specific, experimentally accessible platform for hosting and manipulating Majorana corner modes in a 2D van der Waals heterostructure, bypassing the need for complex multi-terminal geometries used in nanowire-based approaches.
