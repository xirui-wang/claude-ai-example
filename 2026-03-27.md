## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Low-Field Metal-Insulator Transition in AB-Stacked Bilayer Graphene
**[2603.24976](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24976)** — Chakraborty, Rodin, Adam, Vignale et al. — *cond-mat.mes-hall / cond-mat.mtrl-sci*

This study investigates the interplay of in-plane magnetic fields and transverse electric fields in AB-stacked bilayer graphene, revealing a metal-insulator transition driven by skew-hopping-induced band warping rather than conventional Landau quantization. The central finding is that trigonal warping dramatically lowers the critical magnetic field required to induce band inversion, making the transition accessible at modest laboratory fields. The work provides a detailed theoretical blueprint showing that similar warping-facilitated transitions should occur in other 2D moiré and van der Waals multilayer systems—including TMD bilayers and twisted trilayer graphene—potentially enabling electrically gate-controlled semimetallic states. The results are significant because they suggest a wide family of 2D materials could be engineered to host field-tunable topological phase transitions without the need for extreme conditions.

### Geometric Superfluid Stiffness of Kekulé Superconductivity in Magic-Angle Twisted Bilayer Graphene
**[2603.24766](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24766)** — Ke Wang, Qijin Chen, Rufus Boyack, K. Levin — *cond-mat.supr-con / cond-mat.mtrl-sci / cond-mat.str-el*

Magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene (MATBG) hosts nearly flat bands where quantum geometry — the quantum metric — dominates over kinetic energy in determining collective phenomena. This paper calculates the geometric (quantum metric) contribution to the superfluid stiffness specifically for the Kekulé-bond-ordered (KBO) superconducting phase that breaks the emergent valley symmetry of MATBG, a scenario distinct from the more commonly studied uniform pairing channels. The authors find that the Berry curvature and quantum metric of the KBO Bloch states make a substantial and non-trivial contribution to the superconducting phase stiffness, which sets the scale for the Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless transition temperature observable experimentally. The findings highlight how the exotic band geometry of 2D moiré systems fundamentally reshapes Cooper pairing physics beyond the conventional BCS picture, with direct implications for understanding the anomalously high Tc relative to the Fermi energy in MATBG.

### Gate-Tunable Synthetic Antiferromagnetism with Nonrelativistic Spin Splitting in Graphene/MnS/Graphene
**[2603.20458](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20458)** — Milivojević et al. — *cond-mat.mes-hall*

This theoretical work proposes encapsulating a type-A antiferromagnetic semiconductor (MnS) between two graphene layers to create a gate-tunable altermagnet-like heterostructure with nonrelativistic spin splitting. Ab initio calculations and tight-binding modeling reveal that an applied gate voltage breaks the top/bottom graphene equivalence, inducing opposite ferromagnetic proximity exchange on the two graphene sheets and lifting spin degeneracy without spin-orbit coupling. The resulting spin-resolved conductance in graphene nanoribbons exhibits sharp dips and giant magnetoresistance within a narrow energy window around the Fermi level, fully controllable by the gate. This platform offers a new route to low-dimensional antiferromagnetic spintronics in all-van-der-Waals devices where spin-split currents are electrically rather than magnetically switched.

### Superconducting Parallel-Plate Resonators for the Detection of Single Electron Spins
**[2603.25258](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25258)** — *cond-mat.mes-hall / quant-ph*

This paper presents a multilayer superconducting microwave resonator architecture with sub-Ohm characteristic impedance designed to maximize the vacuum coupling strength to single electron spins. The parallel-plate geometry minimizes far-field magnetic leakage while concentrating the microwave magnetic field in a very small mode volume, achieving a Purcell factor F_P > 10¹⁵ — orders of magnitude beyond conventional coplanar waveguide resonators. The authors demonstrate fabrication routes yielding resonators with intrinsic quality factors exceeding 2×10⁴ at the single-photon level, and characterize performance in applied magnetic fields up to 500 mT (required for electron spin resonance). The analysis quantifies how the achieved Purcell factor enables single-spin detection via both photon-counting and dispersive readout schemes, representing a significant advance toward on-chip microwave-based single-spin spectroscopy and quantum sensing.
