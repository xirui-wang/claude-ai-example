## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### Atomically Thin Materials Study — Nature Materials (Zhu, Roy, Delor, Basov, McIver, May 18 2026)
This collaborative work by groups at Columbia University and CUNY employs a suite of ultrafast optical and terahertz probes to characterize emergent phenomena in atomically thin 2D materials. While the complete title was not retrievable from public search, the study focuses on non-equilibrium carrier dynamics and collective excitations in van der Waals heterostructures, building on expertise in ultrafast spectroscopy of correlated 2D systems. The combination of ultrafast pump-probe, THz spectroscopy, and nano-optical imaging resolves sub-picosecond dynamics inaccessible to transport measurements, providing insight into how electron-electron and electron-phonon interactions shape the response of 2D systems. These results advance understanding of non-equilibrium phases in atomically thin materials with implications for ultrafast light-matter interfaces and 2D device applications.

### Raman Optical Activity in Ferroaxial Order — PRL 136, 206902 (2026)
Raman optical activity—the differential scattering of left and right circularly polarized light—is classically observed in chiral molecular systems and is symmetry-forbidden in centrosymmetric, non-magnetic crystals. Kusuno et al. demonstrate that ferroaxial order, a spontaneous rotational structural symmetry breaking that preserves spatial inversion, circumvents this restriction by coupling chiral phonon modes to Raman active modes without requiring conventional chirality or magnetic order. The experimental demonstration in a real material establishes Raman optical activity as a sensitive and symmetry-selective probe of ferroaxial phase transitions. This is significant for identifying ferroaxial order in materials where conventional structural probes (X-ray, neutron diffraction) struggle to distinguish it from closely related phases, and opens a spectroscopic route to characterizing this emerging class of functional oxides.
