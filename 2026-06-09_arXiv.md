## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [2606.06780] Descriptor Covariance and Correlation Hierarchy in Moiré Exciton Photoluminescence
*Katsunori Wakabayashi, NIMS, Tsukuba — cond-mat.mes-hall, cond-mat.mtrl-sci*

This paper addresses the complex spatial inhomogeneity of photoluminescence (PL) spectra in moiré transition-metal dichalcogenide (TMD) heterobilayers, specifically MoSe₂/WSe₂. Motivated by hyperspectral PL mapping revealing micron-scale correlations among nine spectral descriptors, Wakabayashi proposes a descriptor-based disorder-filter picture: different spectral descriptors couple to different components of a multi-scale disorder landscape (arising from twist-angle variation, strain, and dielectric fluctuations), naturally producing a hierarchy of spatial correlation lengths. The minimal theory captures why some descriptors (e.g., peak energy) are correlated over longer scales than others (e.g., linewidth), offering a framework to disentangle disorder contributions in moiré systems without full spectral peak fitting. This work is significant for understanding and controlling optical properties of moiré TMD devices, where inhomogeneity directly impacts exciton-based quantum emitter performance and the readout of moiré-trapped exciton lattices.

### [2606.07138] Chromium Chalcohalide Janus Monolayer Ferromagnets with Perpendicular Magnetic Anisotropy and High Curie Temperature
*cond-mat.mtrl-sci, cond-mat.str-el — Published Phys. Rev. B (2026)*

This study presents a comprehensive first-principles investigation of CrXY Janus monolayers (X = S, Se, Te; Y = Cl, Br, I) — noncentrosymmetric two-dimensional magnetic materials in which the top and bottom surfaces are inequivalent, breaking inversion symmetry. Both 1T and 1H structural polymorphs were systematically screened for dynamical stability; all but the 1H-CrTeY variants are found to be dynamically stable, with the 1T phase being energetically preferred by more than 0.55 eV/f.u. Key findings include perpendicular magnetic anisotropy and elevated Curie temperatures compared to their centrosymmetric analogues, making these materials candidates for perpendicular magneto-optical and spintronic applications. The inversion-breaking inherent to the Janus structure also enables Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interactions that could support topological spin textures, extending the materials palette for 2D magnon-based technologies.

### [2606.03663] Enhanced Superconductivity in Atomically Thin Noble Metals: From Quantum Confinement to Interface-Induced Lifshitz Transition
*Chun-Jie Zhang, Bing Zhang, Yapeng Wu, Xiao-Ping Li, Lei Wang — cond-mat.supr-con*

This paper addresses the long-standing challenge of inducing superconductivity in intrinsically non-superconducting noble metals (Au, Ag, Cu) by exploiting two-dimensional confinement effects. Using first-principles calculations, the authors establish a unified framework in which quantum confinement in the atomically thin limit creates discrete subbands near the Fermi level, while substrate interface engineering further tunes the band structure through interface-induced Lifshitz transitions (changes in Fermi surface topology). The combination of these effects amplifies the marginal electron-phonon coupling to superconducting-relevant temperatures, providing concrete design strategies — material choice, film thickness, and substrate selection — for experimentally realising noble-metal superconductors. This work has implications for ultrathin 2D superconducting devices and for understanding the fundamental limits of superconductivity in simple metals.

### [2606.05426] A Nanoscale Magnetic Spectrum Analyzer Based on Qubit Dressed States
*Jan Rueschkamp et al. — cond-mat.mes-hall*

This paper demonstrates a broadband nanoscale magnetic spectrum analyzer using nitrogen-vacancy (NV) center qubits in diamond as sensing probes, directly relevant to microwave-frequency spin dynamics. The key innovation is exposing the NV qubit to a microwave dressing field during a dynamical decoupling sequence, which frequency-mixes and amplifies target signals at arbitrary frequencies (including microwave frequencies) into the qubit detection band, overcoming the narrow native bandwidth of conventional NV sensing protocols. The technique is validated by detecting both coherent and noisy spin-wave dynamics in a yttrium-iron-garnet (YIG) thin film over a broad frequency range from sub-MHz to multi-GHz, a regime central to microwave-frequency magnonics and spintronic devices. The generalisation to other qubit platforms (superconducting qubits, quantum dots) underscores the broad significance of this frequency-mixing approach for nanoscale microwave spectroscopy in condensed matter and quantum information hardware.
