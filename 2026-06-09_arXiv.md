## Extended Summaries: 2D Materials & Microwave Engineering

### [2606.06780] Descriptor Covariance and Correlation Hierarchy in Moiré Exciton Photoluminescence
*K. Wakabayashi, NIMS, Tsukuba — cond-mat.mes-hall, cond-mat.mtrl-sci*

This paper addresses the complex spatial inhomogeneity of photoluminescence (PL) spectra in moiré transition-metal dichalcogenide (TMD) heterobilayers, specifically MoSe₂/WSe₂. Motivated by hyperspectral PL mapping that reveals micron-scale correlations among nine spectral descriptors, Wakabayashi proposes a descriptor-based disorder-filter picture: different spectral descriptors couple to different components of a multi-scale disorder landscape (arising from twist-angle variation, strain, and dielectric fluctuations), naturally producing a hierarchy of spatial correlation lengths. The minimal theory captures why some descriptors (e.g., peak energy) are correlated over longer scales than others (e.g., linewidth) and explains the near-perfect anti-correlation between dominant and central spectral energies, without requiring full spectral peak fitting. This work provides a quantitative framework for disentangling disorder contributions in moiré TMD devices, directly informing the optimisation of moiré-trapped exciton lattices and single-photon emitters based on 2D heterostructures.

### [2606.07138] Chromium Chalcohalide Janus Monolayer Ferromagnets with Perpendicular Magnetic Anisotropy and High Curie Temperature
*M. Bosnar, J. M. Lendinez, A. Yu. Vyazovskaya … M. M. Otrokov — cond-mat.mtrl-sci, cond-mat.str-el — Phys. Rev. B 113, 184425 (2026)*

This comprehensive DFT study systematically explores the family of Janus CrXY monolayers (X = S, Se, Te; Y = Cl, Br, I) — two-dimensional magnetic materials formed by an X-Cr-Y atomic trilayer that breaks inversion symmetry by design. Both 1T and 1H structural polymorphs are considered across the full compositional space; all 1T-phase members except CrTeY variants are dynamically stable, with the 1T phase favoured over 1H by more than 0.55 eV per formula unit. Key magnetic properties include perpendicular magnetic anisotropy and Curie temperatures substantially elevated compared to their symmetric CrX₂ counterparts, driven by the broken-inversion-symmetry enhancement of magnetic anisotropy energy. The Janus symmetry-breaking also activates Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interactions that open the door to topological spin textures such as skyrmions, making this materials family a rich platform for 2D magnon-based logic, spin-orbit torque devices, and next-generation magneto-optical applications.

### [2606.03663] Enhanced Superconductivity in Atomically Thin Noble Metals: From Quantum Confinement to Interface-Induced Lifshitz Transition
*C.-J. Zhang, B. Zhang, Y. Wu, X.-P. Li, L. Wang — cond-mat.supr-con*

This first-principles study addresses the fundamental challenge of inducing superconductivity in intrinsically non-superconducting noble metals (Au, Ag, Cu) by exploiting quantum confinement and interface effects in the atomically thin limit. The authors establish a unified framework in which quantisation of electronic subbands in few-monolayer films concentrates spectral weight near the Fermi level, while substrate-induced interface effects trigger a Lifshitz transition that creates new Fermi-surface sheets with strongly enhanced electron-phonon coupling. Together, these effects amplify the marginal pairing interaction of bulk noble metals to temperatures potentially accessible in experiment, with concrete design strategies linking film thickness and substrate choice to predicted Tc. The work demonstrates that 2D geometry and interfacial band engineering are generalisable strategies for unlocking superconductivity in "forbidden" elemental metals, with implications for ultrathin superconducting qubits and 2D Josephson junction devices.

### [2606.05426] A Nanoscale Magnetic Spectrum Analyzer Based on Qubit Dressed States
*J. Rueschkamp et al. — cond-mat.mes-hall*

This paper demonstrates a fundamentally new approach to nanoscale microwave-frequency magnetic spectroscopy using nitrogen-vacancy (NV) center qubits in diamond as the sensing probe. The key innovation is applying a microwave dressing field to the NV qubit during a dynamical decoupling sequence; this field frequency-mixes and amplifies target signals at arbitrary frequencies — including microwave frequencies far outside the qubit's native detection band — into the detection window, circumventing the narrow-bandwidth limitation of conventional NV protocols. The technique is validated by measuring both coherent spin-wave (magnon) signals and broadband magnetic noise from a yttrium-iron-garnet (YIG) thin film across a wide frequency range from sub-MHz to multi-GHz, directly probing the magnon dispersion relevant to microwave magnonics and spin-wave-based signal processing. The scheme generalises to superconducting qubits and quantum dots, establishing frequency-domain multiplexing via qubit dressing as a versatile platform for nanoscale microwave characterisation of spintronic and quantum hardware components.
